## Match Report

FULHAM held Liverpool to a 2-2 at a chilly Craven Cottage down by the River Thames yesterday afternoon in front of 27,547 fans with goals for Fulham from Harry Wilson in the 17th minute assist by Raul Jimenez cancelled out by Florian Wirtz from an assist by Conor Bradley in the 57th minute and Cody Gakpo in the 90th+4 minute but Fulham pulled back a late equaliser through substitute Harrison Reed to make it 2-2 in 90th+7 minute from an assist by fellow substitute Kevin to level the scores. The Reds who now play again in London against Arsenal on Thursday.

Fulham came into the game off the back of three wins against West Ham United (away) 1-0, Nottingham Forest (home) 1-0 and Burnley (away) 3-2, a draw against Crystal Palace (away) 1-1 and a single defeat against Newcastle United (away) (EFL Cup) 1-2. Liverpool came into the game off the back of four wins against Wolverhampton Wanderers (home) 2-1, Tottenham Hotspur (away) 2-1, Brighton & Hove Albion (home) 2-0 and Inter Milan (away) (Champions League) 1-0 and a single draw against Leeds United (home) 0-0.

Fulham won this exact fixture 3-2 last season, last winning consecutive home league games against Liverpool in May 2007. Fulham were one of just three teams Liverpool failed to beat in their Premier League title winning campaign last season (drawn one, lost one) along with Nottingham Forest (drawn one, lost one) and Arsenal (drawn two). Fulham have won just one of their last 19 Premier League games against the reigning champions (drawn three, lost fifteen), though that victory did come against Liverpool in March 2021 (1-0 at Anfield).

The last four Premier League games between Fulham and Liverpool have produced a total of 20 goals, with both teams scoring at least twice in three of those four matches. After losing five consecutive Premier League games against London sides, Liverpool have now won their last two. Meanwhile, the Reds have also scored in their last 20 league games in the capital since a 0-0 draw at Chelsea in April 2023.

Fulham had won five of their last eight Premier League home games (lost three), keeping a clean sheet in four of those. Those four clean sheets are already twice as many as they kept at Craven Cottage last season. Liverpool have earned seven points from their last three Premier League away games (won two, drawn one), more than they had in their previous eight on the road (6 – won two, drawn non, lost six).

Fulham boss Marco Silva has lost 11 of his Premier League games against the reigning champions, the highest 100% loss rate of any manager in the competition. Fulham’s Harry Wilson has been involved in four goals in his last seven home league appearances (3 goals, 1 assist), more than he had in his previous 20 at Craven Cottage (3 goals). Hugo Ekitiké scored three goals in his last two Premier League games, having scored just once in his first six on the road for Liverpool. He last scored in more consecutive away league games in December 2021 (4 with Reims).

Fulham manager Marco Silva started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Berd Leno in goal, then a back three of Issa Diop, Joachim Andersen and Jorge Cuenca –then a four-man midfield with two wing backs in Timothy Castagne, Sasa Lukic, captain Tom Cairney and Antonee Robinson then two attacking midfielders in Harry Wilson and Emile Smith Rowe with Raul Jimenez up top.

Liverpool manager Arne Slot started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Alisson Becker in goal, then a back four of Bradley, Ibrahima Konate, captain Virgil van Dijk, and Milos Kerkez then two holding midfielders in Ryan Gravenberch and Curtis Jones then three attacking midfielders in Dominic Szoboszlai, Alexis MacAllister, and Florian Wirtz with loan striker Cody Gakpo up front.

Fulham kicked off the first half. Liverpool shot early, right footed by Cody Gakpo took a deflection off Andersen for a corner in the 6th minute. In the 11th minute Gakpo was put through and shot wide left-footed with only Leno to beat from assist by Mac Allister. Fulham opened the scoring in the 17th minute with an opening goal struck home by Harry Wilson to make it 1-0 after a VAR decision for offside when in favour of the home side. The assist for the Wilson shot was from Raul Jimenez. Fulham had rattled Liverpool with the Wilson goal and the Whites continued to press for a second goal after soaking up Liverpool pressure on the counterattack.

Gakpo headed against the outside of the post but was adjudged to be offside in the 32nd minute. Fulham led 1-0 at interval. Liverpool kicked off second half 1-0 down. Gakpo offside again for goal scoring chance in 47th minute. Liverpool free kick 25 yards taken by Szoboszlai right-footed wide of the far-right hand post in 52nd minute.

Mac Allister header against Fulham cross bar in 53rd minute. Liverpool equalised through a left-footed shot from inside the Fulham penalty area by Florian Wirtz to make it 1-1 in the 57th minute after another VAR decision to award the second goal of the match. The assist for the equalise was from Conor Bradley for the Reds to level the scores. Gakpo right-footed shot wide of target in 63rd minute as Reds looked to add a second goal and the lead finally. Wilson yellow carded in 70th minute.

Fulham replaced captain Tom Cairney with Sander Berge and Emile Smith Rowe for Kevin in 74th minute. Liverpool repacked Florian Wirtz for Jeremy Frimpong in 76th minute. Harry Wilson chipped left-foot on target but ball bounced off cross bar after initial Jimenez shot was parried by Alisson in 77th minute.

Harry Wilson and Raul Jimenez were replaced by Adama Traore and Jonah Kusi-Asare. Liverpool replaced Curtis Jones for Fabio Chiesa in 85th minute. Fulham replaced Sasa Lukic with Harrison Reed in the 90th+2 minute. Liverpool surely scored the winner in the 90th+4 minute to win the game through Cody Gakpo 2-1 but Fulham weren’t finished.

Fulham pulled back a late equaliser through substitute Harrison Reed to make it 2-2 in 90th+7 minute from an assist by fellow substitute Kevin to seal a deserved draw and sent the Fulham fans wild, against the out going Champions Liverpool who were rather sluggish for most of the game until the two second half goals when they finally came to life. They missed Alexander Isak, Hugo Ekitike and of course Mohamed Salah on duty with Egypt.
