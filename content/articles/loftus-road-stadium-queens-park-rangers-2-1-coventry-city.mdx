## Match Report

QUEENS PARK RANGERS came from behind to rock SKYBET League Leaders Coventry City 2-1 in front of 17,321 fans at the Matrade Loftus Road Stadium yesterday thanks to goals from Richard Kone from an assist by man of the match Nicholas Madsen in the 66th minute and then Madsen popped up with the winner in the 73rd minute to seal the points. Coventry City had opened the scoring through Josh Eccles’ effort from an assist by Jack Rudoni in the 53rd minute but Coventry City’s away record continued to remain poor. They are now joint top with Middlesbrough on goal difference on 58 points.

QPR hosted SKYBET League leaders Coventry City at Matrade Loftus Road yesterday afternoon. QPR came into the game in indifferent form with one win against Sheffield Wednesday (home) 3-0 way back on 4th January, since when they had drawn two against Oxford United (away) 0-0 and Stoke City (away) 0-0 and lost against West Ham United (away) FA Cup 3 1-2 and Wrexham (home) last time out 2-3.

Meanwhile Coventry City managed by legend Frank Lampard came into the game off the back of two wins against Millwall (home) 2-1 and Leicester City (home) 2-1 and three defeats against Birmingham City (away) 2-3, Stoke City (away) FA Cup 3 0-1 and last time out Norwich City (away) 1-2.

Coventry City were top n 58 points and QPR 12th on 40 points. The match earlier in the season ended 1-7 to Coventry City so QPR were looking for revenge for that beating. QPR were beaten by a six-goal margin in this season’s reverse fixture against Coventry (1-7 in August 2025), which remains their joint-heaviest defeat in a Championship match (since 2004-05).

Coventry had already scored seven goals against QPR this season; the last time they scored 10+ across home/away games against an opponent in a single league campaign was in 1963-64, also against QPR (ten).

QPR had only won one of their last seven games in the Championship (D3 L3). Since Boxing Day, only Blackburn and Sheffield Wednesday (both 0) have fewer league wins in the second tier. Coventry have lost three of their last six league games (won two, drawn one), more defeats than they suffered across their first 23 matches in the Championship this season (won fifteen, drawn six lost two).

QPR manager Julian Stephan started with a 4-5-1 formation with Joe Walsh in goal, then a back four of captain Jimmy Dunne, Ronnie Edwards, Steve Cook and Rhys Norrington-Davies then a five-man midfield of Kader Dembele, Nicholas Madsen, Isaac Hayden, Harvey Vale, and Paul Smyth with Richard Kone up front.

Coventry City manager Frank Lampard started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Carl Rushworth in goal, then a back four of Milan van Ewijk, Bobby Thomas, Liam Kitching and Jay Dasilva then two holding midfielders in Jack Rudoni and captain Matt Grimes then three attacking midfielders in Tatsuhiro Sakamoto, Josh Eccles and Ephron Mason-Clark with Ellis Simms up front.

Coventry City kicked off the first half wearing a changed strip of all orange. QPR made one change with Ronnie Edwards coming in for Amadou Mbengue.

QPR went close to an opening goal in the 11th minute after a Paul Smyth left-wing cross into the six-yard box where Richard Kone fired his right-footed shot into the side netting.

With QPR’s next attack Harvey Vale saw a right-footed shot saved by carl Rushworth in the Sky Blues goal. Kader Dembele was replaced by Daniel Bennie in the 22nd minute after an injury from a clash with Ephron Mason-Clark.

Richard Kone yellow carded for foul on Josh Eccles in 24th minute. QPR corner in 26th minute came to nothing and again in 32nd minter another corner wasted.

Coventry City replaced Ephron Mason-Clark with Romain Esse in the 36th minute. Rushworth made a great save in the 36th minute to deny a header by Kone which looked like the opening goal, but the Coventry City goalkeeper made a super save.

QPR kicked off the second half with score at 0-0. Coventry City opened the scoring through Josh Eccles’ header from inside the six-yard box effort from an assist by Jack Rudoni in the 53rd minute as Coventry City’s away record continued to remain poor. They are now joint top with Middlesbrough on goal difference on 58 points.

But QPR came back to win 2-1 thanks to goals by Richard Kone right-footed shot from an assist by man of the match Nicholas Madsen in the 66th minute and then Madsen with a right-footed shot popped up with the winner in the 73rd minute to seal the points.
