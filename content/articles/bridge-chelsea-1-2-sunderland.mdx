## Match Report

CHELSEA were stunned at Stamford Bridge in front of 39,579 fans as the Black Cats fans went home the happier after a 2-1 away win thanks to aa stoppage time winner by Chemsdine Talbi from an assist by Brian Brobbey in the 90th+2 minute to seal the win. Chelsea opened the scoring when Alejandro Garnacho fired home left-footed from close range from an assist by Pedro Neto to make it 1-0 in the 4th minute but then Sunderland equalised through Wilson Isidor who hooked the ball home from close range to make it 1-1 in the 22nd minute after Chelsea failed to clear a long throw in.

Chelsea came into the game with four wins against Ajax (5-1) at home, Nottingham Forest 3-0 (away), Liverpool 2-1 (home), Benfica 2-1 (home), and Brighton lost 1-3 (home). Meanwhile Sunderland came into the game with two wins Wolverhampton Wanderers 2-0 (home), Nottingham Forest 1-0 (away), two draws Aston Villa 0-0 (home), Crstal Palace 1-1 (away) and one loss Manchester United 0-1 (away).

Chelsea had won their last three home league games against Sunderland by an aggregate score of 11-3, most recently a 5-1 victory on the final day of the 2016-17 campaign. Having won four of their first six Premier League games against Chelsea between 1996 and 2001 (L2), Sunderland has since won just three of their 26 against the Blues (draw two, lost twenty-one).

None of the sixteen Premier League meetings between Chelsea and Sunderland at Stamford Bridge have been drawn (thirteen Chelsea wins, three Sunderland wins). It’s the second most played specific fixture without seeing a single draw in the competition, after Fulham at home to Newcastle (seventeen). Only Manchester City (thirty-four) have earned more home points in the Premier League in 2025 than Chelsea (33 – won ten, drawn three lost one), with the Blues sole defeat at Stamford Bridge this calendar year coming against Brighton last month. Sunderland has 14 points from their eight Premier League games this season, their best return at this stage of a top-flight campaign since 1999-00 when they also had 14 points and won their ninth game.

Sunderland is unbeaten in their last thirteen away league games against London sides (won four, drawn nine), since a 2-1 loss at Fulham in April 2018. It’s the Black Cats’ longest ever unbeaten league run in the capital. Chelsea failed to score in their first Premier League game of the season (0-0 vs Crystal Palace) but have netted 16 goals in their seven games since. Only Manchester City (seventeen) scored more in the competition than the Blues this term. Chelsea has the youngest starting XI age in the Premier League this season (24 years, 116 days), while Sunderland has the third youngest (25 years, 92 days). The Blues are still yet to feature a single player over the age of 28 under Enzo Maresca, with the Italian the only manager (inc. caretakers) in Premier League history not to use a player aged 30+ in any game. Excluding penalties, no team has scored more goals from set pieces in the Premier League this season than Chelsea (eight, level with Arsenal). Six of these have come from corners, just one fewer corner goals than they netted in the whole of last season.

Sunderland keeper Robin Roefs has conceded six goals (excluding own goals) in the Premier League this season, from an xGoT of nine. His record of preventing three goals is the highest of any Premier League goalkeeper this term.

Chelsea manager Enzo Maresa started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Sanchez in goal, then a back four of Reece James, Josh Acheampong, Trevor Chalobah and Marc Cucurella then a two-man midfield holding of Moises Caicedo and captain Enzo Fernandez then three attacking midfielders in Pedro Neto, Joao Pedro, and Alejandro Garnacho with Marc Guiu up front.

Sunderland manager Regis Le Bris started with a 5-4-1 formation with Robin Roefs in goal then a back five of Trai Hume, Nordi Mukiele, Dan Ballard, Lutsharel Geertruida and Reinaldo Mandava then four midfielders in Granit Xhaka captain, Bertrand Traore, Noah Sadiki, and Enzo Le Fee with Wilson Isidor up front.

Sunderland kicked off the first half. The visitors went a goal behind in the 4th minute when Alejandro Garnacho fired home left-footed from close range from an assist by Pedro Neto to make it 1-0.

Chelsea a goal to the good, looked like the most likely to score again although Sunderland did not allow the Blues to have it all their own way. In the 21st Sunderland equalised from a long throw in after Chelsea’s defence failed to clear the danger and Wilson Isidor hooked the ball home from close range to make it 1-1. In the 44th minute Trevoh Chalobah saw a right-footed shot from 25 yards out tipped over the cross bar by Robin Roefs for a corner.

The teams went in level at 1-1 at the half time interval. Chelsea kicked off in the second half. Sunderland started the second half well and had another long throw which Chelsea dealt with and two quick corners in succession but the Blues defence survived the visitors’ pressure.

Chelsea brought on Estevao in the 57th minute for Alejandro Garnacho who had scored his first ever goal for Chelsea earlier in the match in all competitions. Chelsea came on strongly in the 60th minute an Estevao Sana’a left-footed shot from inside the Sunderland penalty deflect off Sunderland defender Dan Ballard for a corner after an assist from marc Cucurella. Sunderland manager Regis Le Bris brought on Chemsdine Talbi for Bertrand Traore in the 64th minute. Lee Fee was yellow carded for heavy challenge on Acheampong in the 69th minute.

Enzo Fernandez, right-footed shot from 22 yards out, was saved well by Roefs in the 70th minute. Sunderland made a double substitution in the 72nd minute with Brian Brobbey and Chris Rigg coming on for Wilson Isidor and Enzo Le Fee. Chelsea brought on Jamie Gittens and Tosin for Marc Guiu and Josh Acheampong in 73rd minute.

With time running out for Chelsea to win the game Enzo Maresca brought on Tyrique George and Andrey Santos for Pedro Neto and Joao Pedro in the 84th minute. Black Cats fans went home happier after a 2-1 away win stunned the home fans thanks to aa stoppage time winner by Chemsdine Talbi from an assist by Brian Brobbey in the 90th+2 minute to seal the win.

Sunderland fans were noisy to the end as their side held on for a deserved 2-1 win as Chelsea’s front line failed to fire.

**Half Time Chelsea 1 Sunderland 1 (3 minutes added time)**
