## Match Report

YESTERDAY afternoon Reading came from 2-1 down to beat Luton Town 3-2 in an exciting match at Kenilworth Road in front of 11,374 fans. Early on the visitors Reading scored with their first attack through Kelvin Ehibhatiomhan in the 5th minute and the home side came back to lead thanks to goals before the half time interval by Nigel Lonwijk with a close range tap in from an assist by Emiliano Lawrence on loan from Manchester City in the 45th+3 minute and George Saville with a near post header in the 53rd minute from an assist by Jordan Clark. But later on, Reading equalised in the 81st minute through a close-range header by Kelvin Ehibhatiomhan with his second goal of the game to make it 2-2 from an assist by Matt Ritchie and then Kelvin Ehibhatiomhan sealed his hat-trick with his third goal of the game to make it 2-3 from an assist by excellent Matt Ritchie to seal the points.

Earlier in the week Luton Town reached the EFL Trophy Final on 12th April against Stockport County at Wembley Stadium after beating Plymouth Argyle and Northampton Town in the Quarter Final and Semi Final having been reinstated in the competition after Swindon Town played an illegible player in the last 16 tie.

Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere was hoping to carry that cup form into the last batch of SKYBET League 1 fixtures to make a late charge for the playoffs, come the end of the season. It was Reading who themselves were looking for a playoff push too as they were 8th placed.

Luton Town started the day in 10th with Reading 8th on 51 points both teams from 34 games played with 11 remaining to be played after yesterday’s match.

Luton Town came into the game off the back of two wins against Northampton Town (home) EFL Trophy 2-1, Plymouth Argyle (home) EFL Trophy 2-1, two draws against Port Vale (away) 1-1 and Burton Albion (home) 1-1 and one defeat against Wigan Athletic (away) 0-1.

Meanwhile Reading came into the game off the back of three wins against Bradford City (home) 2-1, Wycombe Wanderers (home) 3-2 and Wigan Athletic (away) 2-1 and two draws against Port Vale (away) 1-1 and Bolton Wanderers (home) 1-1.

Luton Town had lost just one of their last six league games against Reading (won two, drawn three), though that sole defeat came in the reverse fixture in December during a 3-2 loss. Reading had failed to score in each of their last three away league games against Luton Town, one more goalless display as across their prior 18 such fixtures combined.

Luton Town had failed to win any of their last four games in League One (drawn two, lost two), they endured a winless run in the third tier between April 2008 and August 2018 (nine games). Reading was unbeaten across their last five league games (won three, drawn two), whilst overall, the Royals have now failed to score in just one of their last 32 League One outings (0-2 loss to Bradford in December 2025).

Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Josh Keeley in goal, then a back four of Isiah Jones, Nigel Lonwijk, captain Kai Naismith, and Joe Johnson then two holding midfielders in George Saville and Kasey Palmer then three attacking midfielders in Jake Richards, Jordan Clark and Emilo Lawrence with Devante Cole up top.
