## Match Report

TOTTENHAM HOTSPUR celebrated 125 years since they won their FA Cup in 1901 against Sheffield United as a non-league team yesterday by wearing a replica of the kit, but it was the visitors Aston Villa who spoiled the celebration with an accomplished victory 2-1 at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in front of 57,718 fans. Aston Villa goals were scored by Emi Buendia with a close range shot from an assist by Donyell Malen in the 21st minute and Morgan Rogers with a shot in the 45th+3 minute from an assist by Emi Buendia. Tottenham Hotspur pulled a goal back from Wilson Odobert in the 54th minute from assist by Randal Kolo Muani.

Tottenham Hotspur turned their attention to the Emirates FA Cup 3rd Round tie against high flying Aston Villa at The Tottenham Hotspur Stadium yesterday teatime on the day the North London club remembered two former players who died during the week Martin Chivers and Terry Yorath both internationals for England and Wales.

Tottenham Hotspur came into the game off the back of one win against Crystal Palace (away) 1-0, two draws against Sunderland (home) 1-1 and Brentford (away) 0-0 and two losses to Liverpool (home) 1-2 and AFC Bournemouth (away) 2-3. Aston Villa came into the game off the back of three wins against Nottingham Forest (home) 3-1, Chelsea (away) 2-1 and Manchester United (home) 2-1 a draw against Crystal Palace (away) 0-0 and a single defeat against Arsenal (away) 1-4.

Tottenham have lost two of their last three FA Cup ties against Aston Villa, going out against the Villans in the 4th round last season. The last team to eliminate Spurs from the FA Cup in consecutive seasons were Manchester United in 2007-08/2008-09.Aston Villa have already won 2-1 at Tottenham in the Premier League this season.

As a top-flight side, the last team they beat twice away from home in a single campaign was Norwich City in 2012-13. Tottenham had progressed from 18 of their last 19 FA Cup third round ties, doing so in each of the last 11 campaigns since a 2-0 loss at Arsenal in 2013-14. Aston Villa had won their last two FA Cup third round ties, having been eliminated at this stage in each of the seven campaigns before this. Morgan Rogers had been involved in five goals in four appearances against Tottenham in all competitions (3 goals, 2 assists), scoring and assisting in Aston Villa’s 2-1 win against them in the FA Cup last season.

Tottenham Hotspur manager Thomas Frank started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Guglielmo Vicario in goal, then a back four of Pedro Porro, the captain Cristian Romero, Micky van de Ven and Ben Davies then two holding midfielders in Joao Palhinha and Archie Gray then three attacking midfielders in Wilson Odobert, Xavi Simons, and Mathys Tel with Richarlison up front. Tottenham Hotspur made five changes from the last match.

Aston Villa manager Unai Emery with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Marco Bizot in goal, then a back four of Matty Cash, Ezri Konsa, Pau Torres, and Ian Maatsen then two holding midfielders in Boubacar Kamara and Lamare Bogarde then three attacking midfielders in captain John McGinn, Emi Buendia and Morgan Rogers with Donyell Malen up front. Aston Villa made six changes from the last match. Prior to kick off the fans inside the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium stood to applaud Martin Chivers and Terry Yorath who both died in the last week.

Aston Villa kicked off the first half wearing a changed strip of all black with white scores. Tottenham Hotspur wore a reproduction of the 1901 kit when the Spurs won the FA Cup for the first time as a non-league team when they beat Sheffield United in 1901 - 125 years ago. Aston Villa lost Boubacar Kamara was replaced by Youri Tielemans in the 9th minute due to the midfielder being injured. Aston Villa took the lead in the 21st minute through an Emi Buendia left-footed shot from inside the Tottenham Hotspur penalty area from an assist by Donyell Malen to make it 0-1.

Then with Villa’s next attack Matty Cash saw a right-footed shot from 25 yards out saved by Guglielmo Vicario in the 24th minute. Tottenham’s home league record in 2025 was the worst in their history but they did win eight matches at home in 2025 along way to reach the Europa League Final. Tottenham Hotspur forward Richarlison pulled up with a hamstring injury that left the field injured in the 29th minute and Tottenham Hotspur brought on Randal Kolo Muani in the 30th minute. John McGinn saw left-footed ruling shot inside Spurs penalty area in 45th+2 minute saved by Vicario. Aston Villa made it 2-0 before half time through a left-footed shot by Morgan Rogers to double the lead in the 45th+3 minute, the assist was by Emi Buendia.

Aston Villa led 2-0 at half time. The Spurs fans booed the team off at the half time interval. Tottenham Hotspur kicked off the second half a goal down. In the 52nd minute Xavi Simons saw a right-footed shot from 22 yards out tipped away for corner by Marco Bizot as Spurs looked to pull a goal back.

Tottenham Hotspur pulled. A goal through Wilson Odobert with a right-footed 20 yards out from an assist by Randal Kolo Muani in the 53rd minute. Archie Gray was yellow carded in the 57th minute for foul on Emi Buendia. Marlen saw a right-footed shot inside the Spurs penalty area saved by Bizot in the 62nd minute. Aston Villa manager Unai Emery brought on Ollie Watkins and Jadon Sancho for John McGinn and Donyell Malen in the 63rd minute. Emi Buendia Yellow carded in 67th minute for a sloppy foul.

Joao Palhina saw a right-footed shot blocked by Pau Torres in the 71st minute. Villa looked for a third goal to seal the victory with less than a quarter of an hour left. Sancho was good since he came on and Tielemans ran the midfield well for Villa after McGinn was substituted. Aston Villa brought on Lucas Digne and Andres Garcia for Ian Maatsen and Matty Cash in 78th minute. Tottenham Hotspur brought on Dane Scarlett for Mathys Tel in 78th minute. Tottenham Hotspur brought on Dominic Solanke and Djed Spence for Pedro Porro and Wilson Odobert in 83rd minute.

Aston Villa sealed the win and a place in the Emirates FA Cup 4th Round draw on Monday night.
