## First 45 minutes

LUTON TOWN returned to winning ways in SKYBET League 1 with a narrow 1-0 home win over Wigan Athletic thanks to a 9th minute left-footed shot inside the visitors’ penalty area by former Millwall midfielder George Saville who scored his first goal for the Hatters from an assist by Liam Walsh in front of a midweek crowd of 9,975 fans at Kenilworth Road.

Luton Town hosted Wigan Athletic in SKYBET League 1 last night at Kenilworth Road. Luton Town came into the game off the back of a 1-2 away defeat against Bradford City at Valley Parade last time out and a 0-1 away Carabao Cup 1st Round loss to Coventry City.

The previous two matches saw the Hatters beat Peterborough United 2-0 away at London Road and beat AFC Wimbledon at home 1-0 in the first game of the campaign after back-to-back relegations from the Premier League.

Wigan Athletic came into the game off the back of a 2-0 home win over Peterborough United in the league and a 1-0 Carabao Cup win over Notts County. The previous two matches saw 2-0 win over Leyton Orient and a 3-1 win over Northampton Town.

Luton Town manager Matt Bloomfield started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Josh Keeley in goal then back three of Nigel Lonwijk, Mark McGuinness. and captain Kai Naismith then a four-man midfield of Gideon Kodua, Liam Walsh, George Saville, and Cohen Bramall then Millenic Alli and Jordan Clark behind striker Jerry Yates. Luton Town were missing Nahki Wells up front.

Wigan Athletic manager Ryan Lowe started with a 3-1-4-2 formation with Sam Tickle in goal, then a back three of Will Aimson, captain Jason Kerr, and Morgan Fox then Tobias Brenan then a four-man midfield of Jensen Weir, Callum Wright, Ryan Trevitt and Luke Robinson, then Baba Adeeko and Paul Mullin up front.

Luton Town kicked off the first half attacking the Oak Road end of the ground. Luton came forward during Adam Dilley 7th minute appreciation, but it was a minute after when Luton own took the lead through a George Saville left-footed shot from inside the penalty area from an assist by Liam Walsh to make it 1-0. It was a difficult finish which was well taken by Saville for his first goal of the season.

In the 11th minute Tobias Brenan was yellow carded for a foul on Chen Bramall. Wigan responded well and in the 15th minute Ryan Trevitt, who was on loan from Brentford, saw a long-distance right-footed shot hit the far post with Josh Keeley beaten in the Hatters goal.

The game was very much stop and start as League 1 is compared with the Championship and Premier League which Luton fans had seen over the last two years. Paul Mullin saw a left-footed shot saved well by Keeley in the 24th minute which stemmed from a mistake by Mark McGuinness. Luton, then came forward through Jordan Clark who saw a right-footed shot from close range by Jordan Clark saved by Sam Tickle in the 27th minute.

Wigan hit the cross bar in the 29th minute through Will Aimson who picked up a loose piece of handling by Keeley, it should have been 1-1 then but Luton survived. Wigan went close again from a centre by Jensen Weir with the header from Callum Wright was which headed on target but saved by Keeley in the 39th minute. Luton Town led 1-0 at the interval.

**Half Time Luton Town 1 Wigan Athletic 0 (1-minute added time)**

## Second 45 minutes

Wigan Athletic kicked off the second half 1-0 down. Wigan replaced Tobias Brenan with Maleace Asamoah at half time. In the 55th minute Wigan captain Jason Kerr was yellow carded for a shirt pull on Millenic Alli.

Luton Town replaced Cohen Bramall with Lasse Nordas in the 56th minute as the Hatters went with two strikers up top. Alli saw a right-footed pile driver shot from 25 yards out saved by Tickle in the 58th minute.

Luton pressed for a second goal with some heavy pressure on the Wigan goal. The Luton fans came alive as the Hatters had set peace at the Kenilworth end of the ground where most Hatters fans were seated.

Luton came out all guns blazing in the second half. Luton replaced Gideon Kodua with Zack Nelson in the 66th minute. George Saville was yellow carded for back chat in the 67th minute.

Wigan were nowhere near as good as they were in the opening half. Wigan replaced Paul Mullin with Christian Saydee and Callum Weight off and Dara Costelloe on in the 74th minute.

Luton, with five minutes of time remaining, came forward again looking for a second goal to seal the points as both sides went in search of a goal before the end of the match.

Wigan brought on Jonny Smith and Joseph Hungbo for Luke Robinson and Jensen Weir in the 87th minute. Luton Town brought on Tom Holmes for Jerry Yates in 90th+1 minute.

Tickle the goalkeeper shot wide from a corner in the 90th+4 minute as the home side hold firm to take all three points.

Luton Town deserved the keenly fought 1-0 victory which sees the Hatters rise to 5th spot, a point behind leaders Stevenage. Luton Town host Cardiff City next Saturday at home.
