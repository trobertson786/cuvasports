## Match Report

Brentford beat Liverpool 3-2, so the reigning Premier League Champions bad run continued at the Gtech Community Stadium yesterday evening in the Premier League. Brentford goals were scored by Ouattara, Schade and Igor Thiao with Liverpool replying through Kerkez and Salah in front of 17,214 fans.

Brentford came into the game off the back of two wins West Ham United 2-0 (away), Manchester United 3-1 (home) a draw Aston Villa 1-1 (home) and two losses Manchester City 0-1 (home) and Fulham 1-3 (away). Liverpool came into the match off the back of one win Eintracht Frankfurt 5-1 (away) and four defeated Manchester United 1-2 (home), Chelsea 1-2 (away), Galatasaray 0-1 (away), and Crystal Palace 1-2 (away).

Having lost just one of their first three Premier League meetings with Liverpool (one win, drawn one), Brentford have now lost each of their last five against the Reds. Liverpool had scored at least once in all eight of their Premier League games against Brentford, their second best 100% scoring rate against an opponent after QPR (fourteen).

Liverpool had lost each of their last four Premier League away games against London sides – they last lost five consecutive league visits to the capital between March and November 1970. Brentford had won two of their last three Premier League matches, more than in their previous seven combined (won one, drawn two, lost four). The Bees are looking for consecutive wins for the first time since April/May last season under Thomas Frank.

Liverpool had lost five of their last eight away games in the Premier League (won three), as many as their previous 43 beforehand (won twenty-three drawn fifteen). The Reds could lose three consecutive league games on the road for the first time since January/February 2023 – a run that included a 3-1 defeat at Brentford, their only Premier League loss to the Bees. Liverpool had lost their last three Premier League games, as many as they had in their previous 39 (won twenty-seven drawn nine). They lost four in a row in February 2021, while they last had a 4+ game losing streak within their first nine games of a league season in 1993-94.

Liverpool had lost each of their last three Premier League games thanks to goals scored in the final 10 minutes of games, and having equalised in each match. No team has ever lost four in a row thanks to goals scored in the final 10 minutes, while only Crystal Palace in November 2016 lost four in a row having drawn level each time. Michael Kayode created seven chances for Brentford against West Ham, the joint most by a player in a Premier League match this season. Those seven chances account for 37% of the chances he has created across 20 appearances overall (7/19).

Though he’s still waiting for his first Premier League goal for Liverpool, Alexander Isak scored in all four of his appearances against Brentford. No player has a better 100% scoring rate against an opponent in the competition. Brentford’s Igor Thiago has netted five Premier League goals this season, with only Erling Haaland (eleven) and Antoine Semenyo (six) scoring more. Thiago had the most shots (six), shots on target (three), shots from inside the box (six) and touches in the opposition box (ten) in the Bees’ 2-0 win over West Ham on MD8.

Brentford manager Keith Andrews started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Caoimhin Kelleher in goal, then a four of Michael Kayode, Kristoffer Ajer, Sep van den Berg, captain Nathan Collins, and then two holding midfielders in Yehor Yarmoliuk and ex Liverpool legend Jordan Henderson then three attacking midfielders in with Kevin Schade, Mikkel Damsgaard and Dango Ouattara with Igor Thiago up front.

Liverpool manager Arne Slot started with a 4-2-3-1 formation Giorgi Mamardashvill in goal, then back four of Conor Bradley, Ibrahima Konate, captain Virgil van Dijk, and Milos Kerkez then two holding midfielders in Curtis Jones and Dominik Szoboszlai then three attacking midfielders in Mohamed Salah, Florian Wirtz and Cody Gakpo with Ekitike up front.

Liverpool kicked off the first half wearing a changed strip of green and white. Brentford started well and pushed Liverpool back and the home side opened the scoring after a long throw into the Liverpool six-yard box which was headed onto Dango Ouattara with a left-footed shot from an assist by Nathan Collins at the near pos in the 5th minute. Brentford replaced Yehor Yarmoliuk with Vitaly Janelt in the 29th minute due to injury. Brentford were on top at 1-0 up and looked likely to score a second goal than Liverpool to equalise.

Brentford continued to use the long throw to cause Liverpool problems. In the 38th minute Janelt saw a left-footed shot go wide of the target as Liverpool continued to defend resolutely. In the 39th minute Mikkel Damsgaard saw a right-footed shot from 22 yards out tipped away for a corner by Liverpool goalkeeper Mamardashvill. Liverpool Curtis Jones had a right-footed shot from the edge of the Brentford penalty area saved by Kelleher in the 41st minute. Brentford continued to press for a second goal before the interval.

Brentford made it 2-0 in the 45th minute after Kevin Schade fired home one on one with goalkeeper to double lead with the assist by Vitaly Janelt. Liverpool pulled a goal back through Kerkez in the 45th+5 minute with a close-range shot.

Brentford led 2-1 at half time. Brentford went further ahead 3-1, in the 60th minute when Igor Thiao fired home a penalty after Liverpool captain had fouled and given a penalty away. Liverpool could have gone further behind, but chances were wasted, and a stone wall second penalty was not given. Then with time running out Mohamed Salah pulled a goal back from an assist by Dominic Szoboszlai in the 89th minute to make it 3-2.

There was 12 minutes of added time, but Brentford hung on for a deserved win over Liverpool 3-2 much to the pleasure of the home fans in W13,
