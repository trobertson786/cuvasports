## Match Report

LINCOLN CITY beat Luton Town 3-1 at the LNER Stadium yesterday lunchtime in front of 9,255 fans. It was a great start to an important week for the Imps and Michael Skubala’s side with Chelsea the World Champions in the City on Tuesday. yesterday Ben House gave the Imps the ideal tonic with a lovely right-footed finish in the 12th minute from an assist from Conor McGrandles to make it 1-0 and that was the way it stayed until Jordan Clark sneaked in an equaliser from a well worked free kick to make it 1-1 in the 77th minute assist by Millenic Alli then in the 85th minute substitute Justin Obikwu fired home left-footed to make it 2-1 to seal the win but the Imps weren’t finished as Ben House fired home his second of the game in the 90th minute to make it 3-1.

Lincoln City came into the match after a 1-0 away win last time at Burton Albion and a 2-2 home draw with Wigan Athletic. Lincoln City were 7th with 15 points whereas the visitors Luton Town were 9th placed with 12 points and the visitors came into the game off the back of a 2-3 home defeat to Plymouth Argyle and a 4-1 home EFL trophy win over Barnet. The Blackpool away game was cancelled due to call ups during the international break.

Lincoln City manager Michael Skubala had a big week ahead with Chelsea the World Champions due in Lincoln on Tuesday night in the Carabao Cup 3rd round Cup tie.

Lincoln City started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with George Wickens in goal, then back four of captain Tendayi Darikwa, Adam Jackson, Sonny Bradley and Adam Reach then two holding midfielders in Tom Bayliss and Conor McGrandles then three attacking midfielders in Freddie Draper, Ben House and Rob Street with ex-Hatter James Collins leading the line.

Luton Town manager Matt Bloomfield started with a 3-1-4-2 formation with Josh Keeley in goal, then a back three of captain Kai Naismith, Mads Andersen, and Nigel Lonwijk then captain George Saville then a four-man midfield of Teden Mengi, Zak Nelson, Jordan Clark, and Millenic Alli with two strikers in Jerry Yates and Nahki Wells up front.

Luton Town kicked off first half wearing a changed strip of lime green and black. Lincoln City were in their traditional red and white striped shirts and black shorts. Lincoln City started well and in the opening exchanges Adam Reach saw a left-footed free kick from 25 yards out hit the cross bar and bounce out  Luton Town’s three man defence were being overrun and it was no surprise that Lincoln City took the lead through a crisp right-footed shot from Ben House from an assist by Conor McGrandles to make it 1-0.

Luton Town’s two-win backs Alli and Lonwijk seemed to be unsettled. In the 26th minute Freddie Draper saw a bicycle kick on target saved well by Josh Kelley. Lincoln City looked the more likely to score again but Luton Town came into the game a little more as Lincoln were forced to defend a couple of Luton corners and a right-footed shot by Jordan Clark.

Lincoln City led 1-0 at the interval but should really have been out of sight. Lincoln City kicked off the second half 1-0 up. Luton Town replaced Jerry Yates with Joseph Gbode. Freddie Draper caused Luton problems, and he was fouled twice by George Saville and Teden Mengi who were both yellow carded. Tony Bayliss or Adam Reach stood over the free kick on the edge of the penalty area. Lincoln City free kick 25 yards out which came nothing. Ben House headed just over the cross bar at the back post from an assist by Draper.

Luton Town replaced captain Teden Mengi with Shayden Morris in the 59th minute. Luton Town forced a corner in 63rd minute but Lincoln cleared the anger quickly. Lincoln City replaced Rob Street with Tom Hamer in 66th minute. Luton Town replaced Nak Nelson and Kai Naismith with Cohen Bramall and Chris Makosso in 65th minute.

Luton did very little in the second half and with wholesale changes by Matt Bloomfield it was Lincoln that looked the most likely to add to the House goal, but Sonny Bradley fired high and wide from 10 yards out in the 75th minute it could have been 2-0 up and three points but it was not to be. Luton Town pulled a goal back through a well worked free kick which saw Jordan Clark thread a right-footed shot beyond George Wickens to make it 1-1 in the 77th minute assist by Millenic Alli.

Lincoln City replaced James Collins with Justin Obikwu and Freddie Draper with Frankie Okoronkwo in 80th minute. Then in the 85th minute substitute Justin Obikwu fired home left-footed to make it 2-1 to seal the win but the Imps weren’t finished as Ben House fired home his second of the game in the 90th minute to make it 3-1.

It was a deserved win 3-1 to the Imps who host Chelsea next in the Carabao Cup. It was the first time Lincoln City had beaten Luton Town since 1999.
