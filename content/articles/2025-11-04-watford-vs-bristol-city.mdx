## Match Report

WATFORD’s record under Javi Gracia continued on an upward path after the Hornets took another point from a draw at Vicarage Road last night as the Hornets had to settle for share of the points with the Robins 1-1 in front of a crowd of 19,574 thanks to goal from Marc Bola with a left-footed shot after a long ball over the top from inside his own half by the man of the match Imran Louza. Bristol City equalised from a Scott Twine right-footed free kick from 22 yards out which beat Egil Selvik on his near post to make it 1-1 in the 29th minute.

Watford hosted Bristol City in the SKYBET Championship last night at Vicarage Road. Watford came into the game off the back of 1-1 away draw at Potman Road against Ipswich Town and a 3-0 home win over Middlesbrough and a 2-1 home win over West Bromwich Albion both at Vicarage Road in Javi Gracia’s first home match on his return to the club and a 1-3 defeat against table toppers Coventry City away. Bristol City came into the game off the back of 0-1 home defeat to Blackburn Rovers, a 1-5 away defeat to Stoke City, and two home wins at Ashton Gate over Birmingham City 1-0 and Southampton 3-1.

Watford have won three of their last four home league meetings with Bristol City without conceding (Lost 1) and have lost just one of their last six (Won 3 Drawn 2). Bristol City won their most recent game against Watford in April, and could win back-to-back league matches against them for the first time since a run of three between 1990 and 1991.Watford have won each of their last four home league games, their longest run since a five-game spell ending in November 2024 – the Hornets haven’t won five in a row while also scoring more than once in each victory on home turf since 1985 in the top-flight (seven).

Bristol City have lost back-to-back league games for the first time under Gerhard Struber, losing more in a row in February/March 2024, a four-game streak under Liam Manning. Watford’s Imrân Louza has scored in each of his last four Championship appearances, with Troy Deeney in 2015 the last Hornet to go on a longer scoring streak in the league (6 in a row).

Watford manager Javi Gracia started with a 4-4-2 formation with Selvik in goal, then a back four of Max Alleyne, Kevin Keben, Mattie Pollock, and Marc Bola then a four-man midfield of Imran Louza, Jeremy Peris, Edo Kayembe, and Nestory Irankunda then two strikers in Luca Kjerrumgaard and Mamadou Doumbia up front.

Bristol City manager Gerhard Struber started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Radek Vitek in goal, then a back three of George Tanner, captain Rob Dickie, and Hayden Roberts then a four-man midfield of Ross McCrorie, Adaam Randell, Zak Vyner and Sinclair Armstrong then two attacking midfielders in Scott Twine and Aris Mehmeti with Fally Mayulu up front.
