## Match Report

Queens Park Rangers fired a second blank 0-0 after Stoke City on Saturday against Oxford United on a rainy night last evening 0-0 in front of 10,478 fans `in SKYBET Championship last night at the Kassam Stadium

Oxford United in 23rd spot came into the game off the back of one win against Southampton (home) 2-1, two draws against Bristol City (home) 0-0 last time out and Milton Keynes Dons FA Cup 3 (away) 1-1 (won on penalties) and two defeats against Ipswich Town (away) 1-2 and Swansea City (home) 0-1.

QPR in 11th spot came into the game off the back of one win against Sheffield Wednesday (home) 3-0, one draw against Stoke City (away) 0-0 last time out and three defeats against West Ham United FA Cup 3 (away) 1-2, Norwich City (home) 1-2 and West Bromwich Albion (away) 1-2.

Oxford United were winless in four league meetings with Queens Park Rangers (drawn one, lost three), but have never gone five without a win against the Rs. QPR won this exact fixture last season 3-1 but have never won back-to-back league games at Oxford United before.

Oxford United were without a win in their last 10 midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday) Championship games (drawn four, lost six) since defeating Luton Town 3-2 in January 2025.

Meanwhile QPR had won each of their last three away league games played in midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday), last winning four in a row in August 1971. Oxford United had only won one of their last eight league matches against London clubs (drawn three, lost four), beating Millwall 1-0 away in January 2025.

Oxford United manager Matt Bloomfield started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Jamie Cumming in goal, then a back four of Sam Long, Michal Helik, Ciaron Brown and Jack Currie then two holding midfielders in Will Vaulks and Brian De Keersmaecker and then three attacking midfielders in Stan Mills, Cameron Brannagan the captain and Myles Peart-Harris with Will Lankshear up front. Oxford United made one change from Saturday against Bristol City’s 0-0 draw with Will Vaulks replacing Yonus Konak.

Queens Park Rangers Head Coach Julian Stephan started with a 4-4-2 formation with Joe Walsh in goal, then back four of Amadou Mbengue, captain Jimmy Dunne, Steve Cook and Esquerdinha then four midfielders in Kader Dembele, Nicholas Madsen, Isaac Hayden, and Paul Smyth with two strikers in Richard Kone and Daniel Bennie up front. QPR made two changes with Esquerdinha and Daniel Bennie coming in for Rhys Norrington-Davies and Rayan Kolli.

QPR kicked off the first half as the rain lasted down in South Oxfordshire at the Kassam Stadium.

Oxford United in 23rd spot started well and took the early play to QPR. Matt Bloomfield the U’s new manager was yet to win at home since his arrival. In the ninth minute Cameron Brannagan found Sam Long and the right-back whistled a right-footed shot just over Joe Walsh’s cross bar.

In the 12th minute Kader Dembele provided Paul Smyth with an inch perfect cross field pass of 70 yards, and the winger ran at Long before crossing into the six-yard box but neither Richard Kone nor Daniel Bennie could get on the end of it.

Then with QPR’s next move down the left-flank Smyth crossed into Bennie but his right-footed shot was deflected for a corner which came to nothing for the visitors. In the 28th Stan Mills saw a right-footed shot from outside the penalty area go wide of the left-hand had post.

Then in the 29th minute Nicholas Madsen saw a right-footed shot blocked after an assist by Daniel Bennie for the Rs. Paul Smyth went the closest to opening the scoring with a delicious right-footed shot from 22 yards out from an assist by Richard Kone which was tipped away for a corner by Jamie Cumming at full stretch in the 33rd minute.

The teams went in level at the interval 0-0 with Matt Bloomfield the happier of the two managers.

Oxford United replaced central defender Michal Helik with Brodie Spencer in the 46th minute. Oxford United kicked off the second half kicking with the wind which swirled around the three-sided venue.

In the 50th minute Richard Kone headed a curling Nicholas Madsen free kick over the Oxford cross bar. Oxford United had a free kick in the 53rd minute out by the corner flag taken by Brannagan into the near post but QPR cleared their lines quickly.

Kader Dembele was yellow carded in 55th minute. In the 56th Esquerdinha the QPR left-back was injured and was stretched off and was replaced by Rhys Norrington-Davies in the 65th minute.

Brannagan took free kick into the QPR six-yard box, but Bennie headed clear then in the follow up play Will Vaulks saw a right-footed shot tipped by Walsh for a corner. That was the U’s best scoring chance in the game in the 65th minute.

Steve Cook saw a right-footed shot from 25 yards out whistle wide of the top corner of the post from a throw in by Smyth in the 67th minute. Oxford United made a double change with Will Lankshear and Will Vaulks for Mark Harris and Jamie McDonnell in the 70th minute. The latter making his U’s debut.

In the 73rd minute a Myles Peart-Harris cross to the back post was fired high and wide by Stan Mills at the back post when he should have scored. Oxford were improved after the double change.

QPR double substation saw Issac Hayden and Richard Kone replaced by Sam Field and Rayan Kolli in the 75th and 76th minute.

Oxford replaced Stan Mills with Matt Phillips in the 76th minute. Smyth curling right-footed shot into the bear post saved well by Cumming in the 77th minute.

In the 82nd minute QPR corner taken by Madsen punched clear by Cumming.

Late on QPR replaced Kader Dembele with Harvey Var in the 85th minute.
