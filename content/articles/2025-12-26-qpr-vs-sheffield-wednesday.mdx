## Match Report

QPR returned to winning ways with a 3-0 home win over bottom of the table Sheffield Wednesday in front of 16,327 fans at Matrade Loftus Road thanks goals in 14th minute from Rumarn Burrell which was his 10th goal of the season with a right-footed shot from an assist by Nicolas Madsen, then substitute Rayan Kolli bagged alate brace with the first of his two goals in the 81st minute with a right-footed shot from an assist by Richard Kone and then another goal in the 88th minute with a right-footed shot from an assist by Kieran Morgan to complete the 3-0 rout and send QPR to 11th spot 3 points outside the play offs.

QPR came into the game off the back of two wins against Birmingham City (home) 2-1 and Leicester City (home) 4-1, a draw against Portsmouth (away) 1-1 and two defeats against Middlesborough (away) 1-3 and Norwich City (home) last time out 1-2. Sheffield Wednesday came into the game off the back of three draws against Blackburn Rovers (home) 0-0, Hull City (home) 2-2 and Watford (away) 1-1 and two losses against Ipswich Town (away) 1-3 and Derby County (home) 0-3.

QPR are winless in their last five league games against Sheffield Wednesday (drawn two, lost three), since a 4-1 home win in April 2021. Sheffield Wednesday have won their last two away league games against QPR, both by a 2-0 scoreline. They’ve never won three in a row, away against the Hoops before. Sheffield Wednesday have won four of their last seven away league games against London sides (lost three), as many as they had in their previous 20 (drawn five, lost eleven). They have lost both visits to the capital this season, however. QPR are winless in their last five league games played on a Sunday (drawn two, lost three), with this their first Sunday match since a 1-1 draw with Norwich in December 2024. Sheffield Wednesday have lost seven of their last eight league games played on a Sunday (won just one, including each of the last five in a row.

QPR manager Julien Stephan started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Ben Hamer in goal, then back four of Mbengue, captain Jimmy Dunne, Steve Cook and Rhys Norrington-Davies then two holding midfielders in Jonathan Varane and Nicolas Madsen then three attacking midfielders in Koki Saito, Rumarn Burrell and Kwame Poku with Richard Kone up front.

Sheffield Wednesday manager Henrik Pedersen started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Pierce Charles in goal, then a back three of Dominic Iorfa, Gabriel Otegbayo and Cole McGhee then a four-man midfield of Sean Fusire, Jarvis Thornton, captain Barry Bannan and Harry Amass then two attacking midfielders in Charlie McNeill and Jamal Lowe with Bailey Cadamateri up front.

Sheffield Wednesday kicked off the first half on a poor Loftus Road pitch that looked worse for wear in the wintery sunshine with a midday kick off. QPR opened the scoring in the 14th minute after Rumarn Burrell fired home with a right-footed shot from 12 yards out after an inch perfect pass from Nicolas Madsen to make it 1-0. It was his tenth goal of the season. After scoring Burrell took a knock and was replaced by Rayan Kolli in the 21st minute. Nicolas Madsen saw a right-oozed free kick from 20 yards out saved by Pierce Charles in the 29th minute. Jonathan Varane left the field injured in the 45th minute and was replaced by Sam Field.

QPR led 1-0 at half time but had kicked up two injuries ahead of next week’s FA Cup 3rd Round tie at West Ham United. QPR replaced Kwame Poku with Kader Dembele in the 46th minute. Sheffield Wednesday replaced Dominic Iorfa for Liam Palmer and Nathan Redmond for Jarvis Thornton in 57th minute. Richard Kone saw a header tipped way by Charles from a Dembele corner in the 58th minute.

Then late on to seal the win substitute Rayan Kolli bagged alate brace with the first of his two goals in the 81st minute with a right-footed shot from an assist by Richard Kone and then another goal in the 88th minute with a right-footed shot from an assist by Kieran Morgan to complete the 3-0 rout and send QPR to 11th spot 3points outside the play offs.
