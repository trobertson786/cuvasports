## Match Report

GIDEON KODUA was the difference between the Hatters and the Chairboys in the local Beds-Bucks Home Counties derby encounter yesterday afternoon at Kenilworth Road as the Luton Town midfielder scored both goals in the 7th and 44th minutes of the first half and then two further goals in the second half in the 70th minute by captain Jordan Clark and then substitute Shayden Morris slotted home a fourth in the 78th minute to seal a 4-0 home win in front of a crowd of 10,765 fans.

Luton Town hosted Wycombe Wanderers in the Beds Bucks Boxing Day derby at Kenilworth Road yesterday afternoon. Luton Town came into the game off the back of one win against Exeter City EFL Trophy (home) 4-0, three draws Port Vale (home) 2-2, Leyton Orient (away) 1-1 and Fleetwood Town FA Cup (away) 1-1 (lost 2-4 on penalties) and one defeat against Reading (away) 2-3 last time out. Wycombe Wanderers came into the game with one win against Bolton Wanderers (home) 2-1, one draw Burton Albion (away) 0-0 and three defeats Plymouth Argyle (home) 0-1, Exeter City FA Cup (away) 0-4 and Northampton Town EFL Trophy (away) 0-2.

Luton Town had lost just one of their last nine league games against Wycombe Wanderers (won six, drawn two), scoring 2.1 goals on average during that time. Wycombe Wanderers have lost each of their last three league games against Luton Town, as many defeats as across their prior 12 league games combined (won four, drawn five).

Wycombe had won two of their last three league games played on Boxing Day (lost one), as many victories as across their prior nine matches beforehand combined between 2012 and 2020 (drawn two, lost five). Luton had twice faced Wycombe on Boxing Day in the Football League before, failing to win either match between the pair played in 2014 (1-1) and 2015 (0-2).

Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Josh Keeley in goal, then a back four of Nigel Lonwijk, Teden Mengi, Mads Andersen and Joe Johnson then two holding midfielders in Liam Walsh and Lamine Fanne then three attacking midfielders in Gideon Kodua, captain Jordan Clark, and Chris Bramall with Nahki Wells up front. One change with Kai Naismith replaced by Joe Johnson masking his first home start of the campaign.

Wycombe Wanderers manager Michael Duff started with a 4-1-4-1 formation with Will Norris in goal, then back four of captain Jack Grimmer, Dan Casey, Anders Hagelskjaer and Daniel Harvie then Ewan Henderson then Junior Quitirna, Magnus Westergaard, Luke Leahy, and ex-Hatter Fred Onyedinma with James Bell up front. One change with Magnus Westergaard is coming in for Jamie Mullins.

Wycombe Wanderers kicked off the first half. Luton Town took the lead in the seventh minute through a right-footed shot from Gideon Kodua out right side of the Wycombe penalty area from an assist by Lamine Fanne to make it 1-0 and send the Hatters on their way. Wycombe goalkeeper Will Norris should have done better with his effort to save the shot.

In the eleventh minute Cohen Bramall headed a Gideon Kodua cross wide of the near post it could have been 2-0 then. Luton went close. To add another goal with a left-footed shot from Kodua who cut in from the right flank, but his effort went wide of the far post in the 28th minute.

Nahki Wells left-footed shot from 20 yards out tipped away by Will Norris for a corner in the 29th minute. Luton went close again to a second goal as Wells shot was cleared off the line via the post off Daniel Harvie in the 33rd minute. Luton could and should have been at least three up by the break against a poor Wycombe outfit. Westergaard heavy challenge in 41st minute yellow carded by referee Dean Whitestone. Wycombe gifted Luton Town a second goal after a dreadful mix up between goalkeeper Will Norris and Daniel Harvie gave Gideon Kodua an empty goal to shot into to make it 2-0 in the 45th minute.

Luton Town led 2-0 at the interval. Luton Town kicked off in the second half. Wycombe improved early in the second half with Fred Onyedinma seeing a left-footed shot blocked from inside the Hatters penalty area in the 49th minute. Wycombe replaced Westergaard with Alex Lowry in the 58th minute. Luton Town replaced Nahki Wells with Jerry Yates and Lamine Fanne for George Saville in the 68th minute.

Luton Town made it 3-0 in the 70th minute through captain Jordan Clark with a left-footed shot to seal the win from an assist by Jerry Yates. Wycombe made two further substitutions after the third goal with Luke Leahy for Caolan Boyd-Muncie and Junior Quitirna for Taylor Allen in 75th minute. Luton Town replaced Teden Mengi for Hakeen Odoffin and Cohen Bramall for Shayden Morris in 76th minute. Luton Town were not finished s substitute Shayden Morris slotted home a fourth in the 78th minute with a cool left-footed finish to make it 4-0 and send the Chairboys packing after a thorough Boxing Day beating.

Luton Town ended the afternoon in 8th spot, just 4 points outside the play offs after a good Boxing Day home win which came two years and three days since they beat Newcastle United 2-0 in the Premier league how the Hatters have tumbled down the league tables but now under Jack Wilshere are making a push for the SKYBET League 1 play offs. The Hatters host Leyton Orient next time out at home on the 29th of December.
