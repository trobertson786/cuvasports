## Match Report

CHELSEA manager Liam Rosenior saw his first Premier League win 2-0 at home over Brentford yesterday afternoon thanks to goals by Joao Pedro from an assist by Enzo Fernandez in the 26th minute and a Cole Palmer penalty converted in the 76th minute to seal a good comfortable home win.

Chelsea returned to Premier League in a West London derby against Brentford at Stamford Bridge yesterday after the midweek loss 2-3 to Arsenal at home in the Carabao Cup semi-final 1st leg tie which was Liam Rosenior’s first home match of the new managerial campaign in SW6.

Chelsea came into the game off the back of Chelsea came into the match with one win against Charlton Athletic (away) FA Cup 3 5-1 two draws against Manchester City (away) 1-1 and AFC Bournemouth (home) 2-2 and three defeats against Fulham (away) 1-2, Aston Villa (home) 1-2 and Arsenal (home) Carabao Cup Semi-Final 1st leg 2-3.

Brentford came into the game off the back of four wins AFC Bournemouth (home) 4-1, Everton (away) 4-2, Sunderland (home) 3-0, Sheffield Wednesday (away) FA Cup 3 2-0 and a draw against Tottenham Hotspur (home) 0-0.

Chelsea had won just one of their last eight Premier League games against Brentford (D4 L3), a 2-1 victory in this exact fixture last season. None of Brentford’s nine-way league games against Chelsea had been drawn, with the Bees winning four and losing five. Chelsea had only won one of their previous nine Premier League games (drawn five, lost three), while they’ve conceded the opening goal in each of their last three league games; they last did so in four straight matches in November 2023.

Brentford lost 11 of their last 15 away London derbies in the Premier League (won three, drawn one), conceding at least twice in each defeat. Indeed, they’ve lost each of their last three by a 2-0 scoreline. Brentford are unbeaten in each of their last six Premier League games (won four, drawn two), only enjoying longer unbeaten runs in the competition in March 2023 (twelve games) and September 2023 (seven).

Only Manchester City (52%) and Arsenal (43.8%) have spent a larger amount of time in a winning game state than Brentford (37.2%) in the Premier League this season, while the Bees lead the competition for both goals (thirteen) and expected goals (10.6) in the final 15 minutes of matches in 2025-26.

Liam Rosenior will take charge of his first Premier League match; since Maurizio Sarri in August 2018 (3-0 win over Huddersfield Town), just one head coach has won their first Premier League game in charge of Chelsea (drawn four, lost two), with Graham Potter beating Crystal Palace in October 2022 (drawn four lost two inc. interims).

Chelsea’s Liam Delap scored eight goals in 31 league games for Liam Rosenior’s Hull City in 2023-24, his second-most prolific season behind his 12 Premier League goals for Ipswich Town last term. Among players with at least 450 minutes in the competition this season, only six averages more run in behind per 90 than Delap (13.1).

No Premier League duo have combined for more goals this season than Brentford’s Igor Thiago and Kevin Schade (four), with the German assisting three of the Brazilian’s goals. Indeed, in 2025-26 only Bukayo Saka and Martin Ødegaard (sixteen), Morgan Rogers and Ollie Watkins (sixteen) and Rayan Cherki and Erling Haaland (fourteen) have created more chances for one another than the Brentford pair (thirteen).
