## Match Report

ARSENAL remained top of the Champions’ League group table after hosted bottom placed Kairat Almaty from Kazakhstan who had beaten Celtic in the earlier qualifying rounds of the Champions League to reach the last 36 league although they were bottom placed. Arsenal saw the yellow and black army off in style at the Emirates Stadium last night with a 3-2 victory thanks to goals from Viktor Gyokeres, Kai Havertz and Gabriel Martinelli as the Gunners ran riot on Match Day 8. Kairat Almaty goals were scored by Jorginho from the penalty spot in the 7th minute and a header by Richdinho in the 90+4 minute in front of a crowd of 50,200 fans.

Arsenal’s meeting with Kairat will be just the second time ever that an English side have hosted an opponent from Kazakhstan in European competition, after Manchester United’s 1-0 win over Astana in September 2019 in the UEFA Europa League.

This was Arsenal’s first meeting with Kairat; the Gunners haven’t lost any of the last 11 occasions when facing a team for the first time in European competition, winning 10 of those (drawn one), since a defeat versus Rennes in March 2019 (3-1 loss in the UEFA Europa League).Arsenal have won all seven of their UEFA Champions League games this season by a 2+ goal margin. No team in European Cup/UEFA Champions League history has ever won eight in a row by 2+ goals.

Kairat Almaty have just one point from their seven UEFA Champions League matches this season. They’ve conceded the joint-most goals (nineteen), have the highest xG against (19.6) and have faced the most shots on target (fifty-eight) of any side this term. Arsenal is looking for their eighth straight victory in the UEFA Champions League – only one other English side has managed 8+ consecutive wins in European Cup/Champions League history, with Pep Guardiola’s Manchester City winning ten in a row between May 2023 and March 2024.

Arsenal is the only team who haven’t trailed at any point in the UEFA Champions League this season. Kairat, meanwhile, trailed for 316 minutes, the third most behind Ajax (429) and Villarreal (376). Only four teams have had fewer shots from outside the box than Arsenal (twenty-two) in the UEFA Champions League this season. Despite this, no side has scored more goals from distance than the Gunners (four).

Bukayo Saka could make his 50th appearance for Arsenal in major European competition (currently on forty-none) – if he plays in this match, he would be the second-youngest player to reach this milestone for the Gunners (24y 145d on the day of the game), after Cesc Fàbregas (22y 331d in 2010).Kairat’s Temirlan Anarbekov has only conceded 10 goals from 15.1 xG on target in the UEFA Champions League this season; indeed, his goals prevented differential is the highest of any goalkeeper in this edition (5.1 – excluding own goals).

As well as keeping the joint-most clean sheets by a goalkeeper in the 2025-26 edition (five), only one goalkeeper (Ugurcan Çakir – forty-seven) has made more line-breaking passes than Arsenal’s David Raya (forty-five) in the UEFA Champions League this season. Arsenal came into the game off the back of three wins against Inter Milan (away) 3-1 Champions League, Chelsea 3-2 (away) EFL Cup 1st leg and Portsmouth (away) 4-1 FA Cup 3, one draw against Nottingham Forest (away) 0-0 and one loss against Manchester United (home) 2-3.

Kairat came into the match off the back of four defeats against Club Brugge (home) 1-4 Champions League, Olympiakos (home) 0-1 Champions League, Copenhagen (away) 2-3 Champions League and Inter Milan (away) 1-2 Champions League and one draw against Varazdin (away) 0-0.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta started with a 4-3-3 formation with Kepa Arrizabalaga in goal then back four of captain Ben White, Ricardo Calafiori, Mosquera and Myles Lewis-Skelly then three midfielders in Kai Havertz, Cristian Norgaard and Eberechi Eze with Noni Madeuke, Viktor Gyokeres and Gabriel Martinelli up front.

Kairat Amarth manager Rafael Urazbakhtin started with a 4-2-3-1 formation Temirlan Anarbekov in goal, then a back four of Alexandr Shirobokov, captain Aleksandr Martynivich, Egor Sorokin and Mata then two holding midfielders in Dan Glazer and Yerkin Tapalov then three attacking midfielders in Alexandr Mrynskiy, Jorginho and Agilent Sadybelov with Edmilson up front.

Kairat kicked off the first half. Arsenal opened the scoring in the second minute through Viktor Gyokeres with a right-footed shot from an assist by Kai Havertz to make it 1-0. In the 4th minute Kairat were awarded a penalty after VAR decision after Ricardo Calafiori pulled the shirt of Jorginho and spot kick taken by Jorginho to make it 1-1 in the 7th minute. Calafiori was yellow carded by the Swiss referee Urs Schnyder.

Arsenal made it 2-1 in the 15th minute through a left-footed shot by Kai Havertz after a fast attacking break away from an assist by Ben White the Gunners captain. The visitors’ defence was very weak against the fast Gunners front line. Arsenal should have gone 3-1 up in the 24th minute but Myles Lewis-Skelly’s left-wing cross aimed for Viktor Gyokeres who fired high and wide from barely six yards out.

Noni Madeuke cut inside the Kairat penalty area in the 26th minute but his right-footed shot was deflected away for another corner. The resultant corner came to nothing as Calafiori up from the back again fouled Jorginho. Lewis-Skelly fired a left-footed shot wide of the target in the 33rd minute. Viktor Gyokeres was through on goal again in the 34th minute but his close range left-footed shot was blocked by Kairat goalkeeper Temirlan Anarbekov.

Arsenal went 3-1 up in the 37th minute after goal given by VAR as Gabriel Martinelli slotted the ball home from an assist by Kai Havertz. Gyokeres added a fourth in the 41st minute but was ruled offside. Kairat Almaty scored a second goal through a header by Richdinho in the 90+4 minute.
