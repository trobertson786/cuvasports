## Match Report

ARSENAL beat Chelsea 1-0 in front of 59,452 fans at the Emirates Stadium last night to reach the Carabao Cup Final against Manchester City or Newcastle United at Wembley on 22 March thanks to a 90th+7 minute winning goal from former Chelsea striker Kai Havertz after he came forward and rounded Robert Sanchez to slot the ball from after an inch perfect assist by Declan Rice to seal win 4-2 on aggregate in the Semi-Final 2nd leg in a rainy night in London N5.

The Gunners hosted Chelsea in the EFL Carabao Cup Semi-Final 2nd Leg at the Emirates Stadium last night having come into the game 3-2 up after the 1st away leg at Stamford Bridge 3 weeks ago. A place in the Wembley Final against Manchester City leading in the 1st leg at home in the 2nd leg against Newcastle United the holders in the other semi-final were the opponents in the 22 March 2026 Final.

Arsenal came into the game off the back of five wins against Inter Milan (away) 3-1 Champions League, Chelsea 3-2 (away) EFL Cup 1st leg and Portsmouth (away) 4-1 FA Cup 3, Kairat Almaty (home) 3-2 Champions League and Leeds United (away) 4-0 last time out, one draw against Nottingham Forest (away) 0-0 and one loss against Manchester United (home) 2-3.

Chelsea came into the London derby match against Arsenal off the back of five wins for Liam Rosenior the new coach against Napoli 3-2 (away) Champions League, Crystal Palace (away) 3-1, Pafos 1-0 (home) Champions League, Brentford (home) 2-0 and West Ham United (home) 3-2 last time out, and a single defeat against Arsenal (home) 2-3 EFL Cup Semi Final 1st Leg.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta started with a 4-3-3 formation with Kepa Arrizabalaga in goal then back four of Piero Hincapie, William Saliba, captain Gabriel, and Jurrien Timber then a three-man midfield of Eberechi Eze, Martin Zubimendi and Declan Rice with three attackers in Noni Madeuke, Viktor Gyokeres and Gabriel Martinelli.

Chelsea manager Liam Rosenior started with a 4-2-3-1 formation Robert Sanchez in goal, then the back four of captain Malo Gusto, Wesley Fofana, Trevor Chalobah and Jorrel Hato them two holding midfielders in Moises Caicedo and captain Enzo Fernandez then three attacking midfielders in Andrey Santos, Joao Pedro and Marc Cucurella and Liam Delap up front.

The only goal of the game was scored thanks to a 90th+7-minute winning goal from former Chelsea striker Kai Havertz after he came forward and rounded Robert Sanchez to slot the ball after an inch perfect assist by Declan Rice to seal the win 4-2 on aggregate in the Semi-Final 2nd leg in a rainy night in London N5.
