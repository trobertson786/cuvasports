## Match Report

PETERBOROUGH UNITED substitute Gustav Lindgren hit a late leveller as Peterborough United stretched their unbeaten run to five League One games with a draw against Reading at the West Homes Stadium in front of 7,225 fans last night.

Lindgren equalised 10 minutes after coming on to cancel out Daniel Kyerewaa's first-half opener with 12 minutes to go. Posh will feel they should have won it after a host of good chances went begging.

Harry Leonard and Matthew Garbett both failed to find the target during a good opening by Peterborough. Kyerewaa put Reading in front after bundling home a 26th-minute corner which was won after Peterborough goalkeeper Alex Bass made a mess of clearing a simple back pass.

Finley Burns made a brilliant block to keep Posh out during a goal-mouth scramble in stoppage-time. Joel Pereria made a good save to tip away a Leonard header, before Jack Marriott forced a simple save from Bass.

Lindgren equalised after tapping home Peter Kioso's perfect cross in the 78th minute. Garbett was inches away from a dramatic winner, before Pereria made a fine save to deny Lindgren stoppage-time.

Peterborough United came into the game off the back off four wins against Leyton Orient (home) 1-0, Port Vale (away) 1-0, Northampton Town (home) 2-1 and Reading (away) 2-1 and a defeat against Barnsley FA Cup (home) 0-1.

Reading came into the game off the back of two wins against Plymouth Argyle (away) 4-1, Luton Town (home) 3-2 and two defeats against Bradford City (away) 0-2 and Peterborough United (home) 1-2.

Peterborough United manager Luke Williams started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Alex Bass in goal, then a game four of Peter Kioso, Tom Lees, David Okagbue, and captain Carl Johnston then two holding midfielders in Archie Collins and Ben Woods then three attacking midfielders in Matthew Garbett, Jimmy-Jay Morgan and Kyrell Lisbie and Harry Leonard up front.

Reading manager Leam Richardson started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Joel Pereira in goal, then a back four of Andy Yiadom, Paudie O’Connor, Finley Burns, and Jeriel Dorsett then two holding midfielders in captain Lewis Wing and Charlie Savage then three attacking midfielders in Mamadi Camara, Kamari Doyle and Daniel Kyerewaa and Jack Marriott the loan striker up top.

Peterborough United kicked off the first half wearing all blue with Reading in a changed strip of tan and white. Peterborough United started well but it was Reading that looked like the most likely to score early on. They had beaten Plymouth Argyle 4-1 on Boxing Day.

Peterborough United went close to opening the scoring after a free kick which saw Matthew Garbett volley right-footed just over the Reading crossbar in the 17th minute.

Reading opened the scoring from a corner which Peterborough United failed to clear and Daniel Kyerewaa slotted the ball home from close range at the back post from an assist by ex-Posh player Jack Marriott in the 26th minute to make it 0-1. In the 31st minute Kyrell Lisbie came forward and shot right footed, but his effort was blocked by paddies O’Connor for Reading.

Reading led 0-1 at half time against Peterborough United, who struggled to make an impact in front of goal.
