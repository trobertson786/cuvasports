## Match Report

THE IMPS are going up sung the 617 Squadron in the GBM Stand as Lincoln City defeated Blackpool at the LNER Stadium - Sincil Bank in front of 9,812 fans in the football outpost of Lincolnshire where the home side beat Blackpool 4-0 thanks to the goals by Conor McGrandles with a header in the 38th minute and Rob Street with a right-footed shot into an empty net to make it 2-0 in the 78th minute and Dom Jefferies finished off the game in style with a third in the 85th minute with a right-footed shot from an assist by Rob Street and Ryan One hit a fourth in the 89th minute from an acute angle seal the points. Lincoln City in 2nd spot go to league leaders in the SKYBET Championship Cardiff City next Saturday in a 1230 kick off.

Lincoln City came into the game off the back of four wins against Mansfield Town (away) 2-0, Northampton Town (home) 4-0, Plymouth Argyle (away) 4-1 and Wigan Athletic (away) 1-0 and one draw against Bolton Wanderers (home) 1-1. Blackpool came into the game off the back of one win against Mansfield Town (home) 1-0, two draws against Bolton Wanderers (away) 2-2 and Huddersfield Town (away) 2-2 and two losses against Plymouth Argyle (home) 0-4 and Luton Town (away) 0-1.

Lincoln City had failed to win any of their last four league games against Blackpool (drawn two, lost two), conceding exactly two goals in three of those matches. After winning this exact fixture 2-0 last season, Blackpool will be looking to claim successive away league victories over Lincoln City for the first time since April 1991. Lincoln City were unbeaten across their last 16 league games (won twelve, drawn four), they’ve never enjoyed a longer run in the third tier of English football ever before so we’re looking to extend the run. Blackpool had failed to win any of their last five away league games (drawn two, lost three), conceding over two goals on average per fixture during that run (twelve goals in total).
