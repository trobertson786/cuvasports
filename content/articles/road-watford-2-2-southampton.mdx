## First 45 minutes

WATFORD held Southampton to a 2-2 draw at Vicarage Road in front of 19,075 fans yesterday afternoon. Southampton opened the scoring through Cameron Archer with a close-range right-footed shot in the 10th minute from an assist by Nathan Wood-Gordon then Watford equalised through Kwadwo Baah with a great right-footed she from aa tight angle from an assist by Rocco Vata in the 65th minute to level the scores,. Then Southampton went ahead through a brilliant left-footed free kick by Ryan Manning in the 78th minute before Watford equalised again through their own free kick by Nestory Irankunda in the 81st minute to make it 2-2 and that is how the game ended.

Watford hosted Southampton at Vicarage Road yesterday in the SKYBET Championship. The Hornets came into the game off the back of a 1-1 away draw at the Liberty Stadium against Swansea City last weekend whilst Southampton came into the game off the back of a 3-0 away Carabao Cup 2nd Round win against Norwich City at Carrow Road in the week and a 1-2 home defeat to table toppers Stoke City at St Mary’s Stadium last weekend.

Watford manager Paulo Pezzolano started with a 3-5-2 formation with Selvik in goal, then a back three of Max Alleyne the son of Mark Alleyne the Gloucestershire CCC coach and former Gloucestershire and England cricketer, Kevin Keben and James Abankwah then a five-man midfield of Jeremy Ngakia captain Moussa Sissoko, Hector Kyprianou, Edo Kayembe and Marc Bola with Nestory Irankunda and Luca Kjerrumgaard the two strikers up front.

Southampton manager Will Still the husband of Watford fan Emma Saunders and the SKY presenter started with a 3-4-3 formation with Gavin Bazunu in goal, then a back three of Taylor Harwood-Bellis, Nathan Wood-Gordon and captain Jack Stephens then a four-man midfield of Mads Roerslev, Finn Azaz, Shea Charles and Ryan Manning then two attacking midfielders in Jay Robinson and Cameron Archer with the Ryan Fraser main striker up front.  Matheus Fernandes was sold to West Ham United in the week for £40m.

Southampton kicked off the first half defending the Vicarage Road end of the ground. Southampton went close to opening the scoring through Jay Robinson effort from close range which was scrambled away by Egil Selvik for a corner in the 10th minute.

Southampton took the lead from the resultant corner taken by Ryan Fraser and fired home right-footed from close range by Cameron Archer to make it 0-1 in the 10th minute assist by Nathan Wood-Gordon. Archer was on loan to Watford a season ago and made minor impact.

Watford free kick in 12th minute after Luca Kjerrumgaard was up ended by Ryan Manning. Free kick taken right footed by Nestory Irankunda was fired high and wide of the target.

Southampton a goal to the good, looked more likely to score again than Watford. Southampton went close through a Finn Azaz shot in the 41st minute which was saved well by Selvik.

Southampton deserved the 1-0 lead at the interval as Watford had produced truly little up front in the opening half.

**Half Time Watford 0 Southampton 1 (2 minutes added time)**

Secomd 45 minutes

Watford kicked off second half 0-1 down. Neither side made any half time substitutions. Max Alleyne made a great tackle to deny Cameron Archer in the 52nd minute. Jeremy Ngakia came forward in the 56th minute and fired a right-footed shot wide of the target.

Watford manger Paulo Pezzolano made a double substitution with Kwadwo Baah and Rocco Vata for Max Alleyne and Nestor Irankunda in 57th minute. Southampton Taylor Harwood-Bellis’s yellow carded in 64th minute for foul on Kwadwo Baah. Then in the 65th minute Watford equalised through Kwadwo Baah with a right-footed shot from the edge of the penalty area to make it 1-1 and set up the game for the remaining 25 minutes. The assist for the goal came from fellow substitute Rocco Vata.

After the goal Watford looked much better going forward with some free-flowing football. Imran Louza and Giorgi Chakvedtdze were not present, so they were on the move with transfer deadline on Monday. Nestory Irankunda was yellow carded in 75th minute. Southampton manager Will Still made a triple change with Damien Downes for Adam Armstrong for Cameron Archer and Kuryu Matski for Ryan Fraser in the 76th minute. Southampton went 1-2 up from a Ryan Manning free kick in the 78th minute with looked like the winner but then Watford had a free kick of their own and Nestory Irankunda fired home from 22 yards out to make it 2-2 in the 81st minute.

The game ended 2-2, which was a fair result for both teams as neither really deserved all 3 points on offer. Watford were better in the second half. There is now an international break with next games on 13th September.
