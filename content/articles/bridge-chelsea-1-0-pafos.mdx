## Match Report

CHELSEA narrowly beat a plucky Pafos at Stamford Bridge last night in the Champions League Match Day 7 1-0 thanks to a Moises Caicedo header from a corner in the 78th minute.

Chelsea came into the match off the back of two wins against Brentford (home) 2-0, Charlton Athletic FA Cup 3 (away) 5-1, one draw against Manchester City (away) 1-1 and two defeats against Arsenal League Cup Semi-Final 1st Leg (home) 2-3 and Fulham (away) 1-2. Pafos came into the game off the back of three wins against Omonia Aradippou (home) 2-0, Akritas (home) 2-1 and Enosis (away) 2-0 and two losses against Olympiakos Nicosia (away) 0-2 and Apollon (away) 0-2.

This is just Chelsea’s third ever meeting with Cypriot opposition, winning 1-0 away and drawing 2-2 at home with APOEL Nicosia in the 2009-10 UEFA Champions League previously. This was Pafos’ first ever meeting with English opposition. Cypriot sides have only won one of their 21 meetings with English teams in all competitions (drawn three, lost seventeen), with AEK Larnaca winning 1-0 at Crystal Palace in this season’s UEFA Conference League.

Chelsea was unbeaten in their last 14 home games in the UEFA Champions League group stage/league phase (W10 D4), winning the last five in a row including all three this season by an aggregate score of 9-1. Pafos were yet to score away from home in the UEFA Champions League, with two 0-0 draws being followed by a 2-0 loss at Juventus last time out. Of teams in the bottom 12 coming into this round of matches, only Benfica (eight) conceded fewer goals than Pafos (9). This despite the Cypriot side facing more shots than other side in the UEFA Champions League this season (one hundred and twenty-three).

In terms of average age, this fixture will see the side with the youngest starting XI in the UEFA Champions League this season (24y 21d for Chelsea) face the team with the oldest (30y 96d for Pafos).Pafos have only used 18 players in the UEFA Champions League this season; the joint-fewest of any side, along with Union Saint-Gilloise (18). Cole Palmer, who was missing, had been involved in four goals in his last three appearances in major European competition for Chelsea (1 goal, 3 assists), though he’s not featured since netting against Bayern Munich on MD1 this season. 18-year-old Estêvão has scored in all three of his UEFA Champions League starts for Chelsea.

The only teenager to score in each of his first four starts in the competition is Kylian Mbappé (2017), the only Brazilian to do so is Elpídio Silva (2001), while no Chelsea player has ever done so. Pafos’ David Luiz won the UEFA Champions League with Chelsea in 2012. The Brazilian has scored in two of his last three appearances at Stamford Bridge in the competition, with PSG in March 2015 and for the Blues against Roma in October 2017. At start of play Chelsea were 14th on 10 points and Pafos were 29th on 6 points with two games remaining in the League stage of the competition.

Chelsea manager Liam Rosenior started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Filip Jorgensen in goal, then a back four of Malo Gusto, Wesley Fofana, Benoit Badiashile and Jorrel Hato then two holding midfielders in captain Reece James and Moises Caicedo then three attacking midfielders in Pedro Neto, Enzo Fernandez, and Alejandro Garnacho with Liam Delap up front.

Pafos manager Albert Celades Lopez started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Jay Gorter in goal, then a back three of Ken Sema, Derrick Luckassen and Kos Pileas then a four-man midfield of Bruno, Pepe, Ivan Sunjic and Jaja then two attackers in Mislav Orsic and captain Vlad Dragomir with Anderson our top.

Chelsea kicked off the first half defending the Shed End. Chelsea had an Enzo Fernandez goal ruled out for offside after an assist by Pedro Neto in the 17th minute.

In the 31st minute Pafos came forward and Jaja saw a right-footed shot hit Reece James on his hand, but referee Erik Lambrechts waived away shouts for a penalty kick to be awarded.

Moises Caicedo saw a right-footed shot from 12 yards out blocked by Kostas Pileas in the 35th minute. Moises Caicedo, right-footed shot beaten out by Gorter in 41st minute.

Pafos kicked off the second half. Chelsea continued to take the play to Pafos who defended for their lives. Liam Rosenior made five substitutions and then finally in the 78th minute Pafos conceded to a Moises Caicedo header from a corner in the 78th minute of the game to make it 1-0.

Chelsea ended the night in the Champions League top 8 with Napoli away next week in the final round on Match Day 8 in Italy in Naples.
