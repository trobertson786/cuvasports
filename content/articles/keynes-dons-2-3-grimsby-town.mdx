## Match Report

ONE could have been confused as the game started it could have been Real Madrid against Barcelona as MK Dons were in all white and Grimsby Town in blue and red stripes but not long after you realised it was a mid-table League 1 encounter between sides managed by Paul Warne and David Artell.

Grimsby Town survived a second-half fightback from 10-man MK Dons to triumph at Stadium MK in front of 7,758 fans of which 1,200 were from Grimsby.

Goals from Kieran Green, Jaze Kabia and Charles Vernam gave the visitors a 3-0 first-half lead, with the Dons also seeing Jon Mellish sent off. George McEachran's own goal and Connor Lemonheigh-Evans gave MK Dons hope, but the Mariners held out.

Grimsby took the lead when Green turned Vernam's cross in the ninth minute. Moments after being denied by Craig MacGillivray, Kabia made it 2-0 in the 23rd minute when he converted the rebound after the goalkeeper had spilled Vernam's effort.

Vernam added the third just before the half-hour mark as his crisp strike from outside the box found the top corner. Dons’ defender Mellish was dismissed in the 36th minute for catching Kabia with a high boot, but the hosts scored just after half-time when McEachran turned Marvin Ekpiteta's cross into his own net.

Lemonheigh-Evans rifled home from Callum Paterson's long throw in stoppage time, with Jack Sanders missing a chance to equalise with the final kick.

Grimsby Town deserved the 3-0 half time led thanks to goals by captain Kieran Green in the 9th minute then Jaze Kabia in the 23rd minute and Charles Vernam in the 29th minute had given the Mariners the advantage.

MK Dons hosted Grimsby Town in SKYBET League 2 yesterday as Paul Warne’s side took on the Mariners who knocked mighty Manchester United out of the Carabao Cup competition in the last 2 weeks. MK Dons were 7th on 11 points with Grimsby Town in 8th spot on 11 points both were 3 points off the league leaders Gillingham on 14 points after 6 matches.

MK Dons came into the game off the back of a 0-1 home league defeat to Walsall 0-1 and Grimsby Town came into the game off the back of a 0-1 home defeat to Bristol Rovers at Blundell Park last Saturday and a 2-1 away EFL trophy defeat to Bradford City in mid-week.

MK Dons manager Paul Warne started with a 4-3-3 formation with Craig MacGillivray in goal, then a back four of Luke Offord, Marvin Ekpiteta, Jack Sanders, and Jon Mellish then a three-man midfield of new signing Kane Wilson from Derby County, Liam Kelly, and captain Alex Gilbey then three attackers in Rushian Hepburn-Murphy, Callum Paterson, and Scott Hogan.

Grimsby Town manager David Artell started with a 4-1-4-1 formation of Christy Pym in goal, then a back four of Jaysdem Sweeney, Tyrell Warren, Cameron McJannet, and Harvey Rodgers then George McEahran then a four-man midfield of Darragh Burns, captain Kieran Green, Evan Khouri and Charles Vernam with Jaze Kabia up front.

MK Dons kicked off the first half wearing all white strips with Grimsby Town in a changed strip of blue and red stripes it was league 2 you could have been mistaken it was Real Madrid versus Barcelona in an empty stadium.

Grimsby Town opened the scoring in the 9th minute through Kieran Green to make it 0-1 from an assist by Charles Vernam to send the Mariners on their way, which pleaded with their travelling fans from North Lincolnshire who had made the journey down to North Buckinghamshire.

In the 22nd minute Grimsby Town should have gone 0-2 up but Scott Hogan’s right-footed shot was saved by Craig MacGillivray. Then with Grimsby Town’s next attack in the 23rd minute Jaze Kabia slotted home from close range to double the lead and made it 0-2 from an assist by the industrious George McEachran.

Things worsened for MK Dons boss. Paul Warne as his defenders gave too much space to Charles Vernam and he fired in a right-footed shot from 25 yards beyond the diving MacGillivray to make it 3-0 in the 29th minute.

Paul Warne replaced Luke Offord with Aaron Nemane in the 34th minute as the goals had all come down the left flank. In the 35th minute Liam Kelly saw a right-footed shot fired high and wide for the home side from 22 yards out.

Jon Mellish was given a straight red for a high facial boot on Scott Hogan who went down like a felled tree, so MK Dons were down to 10 men and 0-3 down.

Grimsby Town led 0-3 at the interval and the second half was only going to get tougher for the home side with a man short.

Grimsby Town kicked off the second half 3-0 up. In the 47th minute of the second half.

The hosts scored just after half-time when George McEachran turned Marvin Ekpiteta's cross into his own net to make it 1-3 in the 47th minute.

Lemonheigh-Evans rifled home from Callum Paterson's long throw in stoppage time in 90th+1 minute to make it 2-3, with Jack Sanders missing a chance to equalise with the final kick.
