## Match Report

LUTON TOWN play off push was improved yesterday as they saw off promotion chasing Bradford City 2- in the lunchtime kick off at 12.31 in front of a crowd of 11,000 with an accomplished 2-1 home win which saw the Hatters rise to 7th spot, some 3 points outside the play off zone thanks to goals from Jack Richards with a left-footed shot from outside the penalty area from an assist by the impressive Gideon Kodua in the 39th minute and then Shayden Morris added a second in the 56th minute from an assist by captain Jordan Clark in the 55th minute to seal the valuable points for Jack Wilshere’s side. Bradford City scored a goal in the 90th+6 minute with late consolation goal from Stephen Humphrys with a right-footed shot to make it 2-1.

Luton Town hosted Bradford City at Kenilworth Road in the lunchtime SKYBET 1 fixture it was 7th placed Luton Town on 42 points against 5th placed Bradford City on 49 points, so it was a key play off promotion match. Luton Town came into the match off the back of one win against Blackpool (home) 1-0, a draw against Lincoln City (home) 2-2 and three defeats against Huddersfield Town (away) 0-1, Plymouth Argyle (away) 0-1 and Swindon Town (home) EFL Trophy 1-2. Bradford City came into the match off the back of two wins against Doncaster Rovers (home) 1-0 and Blackpool (away) 2-1 and three defeats against Lincoln City (away) 0-3, Huddersfield Town (away) 0-1 and Cardiff City (home) 1-2.

Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Keeley in goal, then a back four of Nigel Lonwijk, Mads Andersen, Teden Mengi and Joe Johnson then Kasey Palmer and captain Jordan Clark then a three-man midfield Shayden Morris, Jake Richards, and Gideon Kodua with Devante Cole up front.

Bradford City manager Graham Alexander an ex-Luton Town player of long ago started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Sam Walker in goal, then a back three of Matthew Pennington, Aden Baldwin and Curtiss Tilt then a four-man midfield of Josh Neufville, captain max Power, Jenson Metcalfe and Ibou Touray then two attacking midfielders in Kayden Jackson and Tyreik Wright then Paul Mullin up top.

Bradford City kicked off the first half wearing a changed strip of all sky blue with maroon and amber trim,. Luton Town gave debuts to Devante Cole up front the son of Andy Cole the profile Manchester United striker. Luton Town started slowly and Jordan Clark saw a left-footed shot from outside the Bantams penalty area saved by Sam Walker in the 10th minute.

Shayden Morris saw a right-footed shot in the 18th minute go high and wide of the target as the Hatters pushed for an opening goal against 5th placed Bradford City, who appeared well marshalled at the back as one would expect from a side managed by Graham Alexander. Curtis Tilt was yellow carded in the 27th minute for a challenge on Shayden Morris as they ran in on goal. Joe Johnson took the free kick left footed, but it went wide of the target.

Bradford City went close with a Tyreik Wright shot from an assist by Ibou Touray in the 33rd minute. Luton Town’s best move came in the 37th minute down the left-flank when Jordan Clark cut in and shot right-footed on target, but his effort bounced back off the cross bar with Walker beaten but the loose balls as cleared by the Bradford defence quickly. Luton Town opened the scoring with their next attack through a smart left-footed shot from Jake Richards to make it 1-0 at the Oak Road end in front of the travelling Bradford fans from an assist by Gideon Kodua in the 39th minute.

Bradford scored a goal down to the Hatters who lost shape at the back after the goal as the Hatters ended the first half with a flourish. Josh Neufville was the second Bradford player yellow carded in the 43rd minute.

Luton Town led 1-0 at the interval. Luton Town kicked off the second half a goal to the goal. Bradford forced a corner in the 49th minute after a left-wing centre by Paul Mullin. Bradford free kick in dangerous position after Clark conceded foul in 52nd minute. Luton Town doubled their lead in style as Jordan Clark came forward and found Shayden Morris and the midfielder converted with a lovely left-footed shot to make it 2-0 in the 55th minute.

Bradford City made a treble substitution in the 61st minute replacing Paul Mullin, Tyreik Wright and Kayden Jackson with Antoni Sarcevic, Stephen Humphrys and Ethan Wheatley. Within minutes of coming on Wheatley saw a left-footed shot when one on one with Kelley but Luton goalkeeper saved in the 63rd minute. Bradford was better after the substitutions with more energy, but Luton still led by two goals. Kodua on the counterattack in teh 66th minute should have made it 3-0 with a right-footed shot from 12 yards out but Walker saved.

Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere brought on two substitutes with Jake Richards and Shayden Morris replaced by Davy van den berg and Isaiah Jones in the 67th minute. In the 72nd minute Isaiah Jones should o made it 3-0 but his effort was saved by Walker. Bradford City fourth substitution saw Matthew Pennington replaced by Bobby Pointon in the 74th minute.

Luton Town replaced Gideon Kodua with Nahki Wells in the 77th minute. Wells was of course a former bantams forward who scored goals at Stamford Bridge when the Bantams knocked Premier League Chelsea out of the FA Cup 5th Round 4-2 at Stamford Bridge 14 years ago.

Luton Town final substitutions saw George Saville and Ali Al-Hamadi come on for Jordan Clark and Devante Cole in the 83rd minute. Bradford City replaced Josh Neufville with Lee Evans in the 85th minute. Kelley denied Stephen Humphrys with a right-footed shot in the 87th minute to keep another clean sheet.

Wheatley saw a left-footed shot deflected for a corner in the 89th minute after a long ball over the back four. Kasey Palmer saw a right-footed short from 22 yards out saved well by Walker as Luton pushed for a late third goal. Bradford City scored a goal in the 90th+6 minute with late consolation goal from Stephen Humphrys with a right-footed shot to make it 2-1.
