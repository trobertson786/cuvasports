## Match Report

MILLWALL undid QPR’s seven game unbeaten run with a deserved 2-1 win in Shepherds Bush thanks to first half goals by Femi Azeez in the 36th minute and a Mihailo Ivanovic header in first half stoppage time to seal the London derby victory in front of a bumper crowd of 17,408 in W12. Rumarn Burrell pulled a goal back from an assist by Koki Saito in the 85th minute. QPR hosted Millwall in a London derby at Loftus Road. QPR were on an unbeaten run of seven games coming into the game.

QPR started with 4-2-3-1 formation with Nardi in goal then with back four of captain Jimmy Dunne, Steve Cook, Liam Morrison, and Rhys Norrington-Davies then Nicolas Madsen and Jonathan Varane then Paul Smyth, Harvey Vale, and Richard Kone with Michael Frey up top.

Millwall started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Max Crocombe in goal then a back four of Ryan Leonard, captain Jake Cooper, Tristan Crama and Zak Sturge then QPR player and Australian international Massimo Luongo and Billy Mitchell then three attacking midfielders in Femi Azeez, Will Smallbone and Thierno Ballo and Mihailo Ivanovic up front.

QPR and Millwall remembered Gordon Jago who managed both sides. QPR kicked off the first half wearing traditional blue and white hoops whilst Millwall in changed strip of all black with red trim. Millwall went close through Will Smallbone with a right-footed shot from 25 yards out which was tipped away by Paula Nardi for a corner. The assist was from the influential Luongo. Millwall lost Massimo Luongo with an injury in the 15th minute as he left the field in a stretcher, and he was replaced by Casper De Norre in the 15th minute.

QPR went close through a Paul Smyth left-footed shot in the 19th minute which shaved the top of the cross bar from an assist by Richard Kone. Ryan Leonard was yellow carded for a heavy challenge on Norrington-Davies in the 34th minute. Millwall opened the scoring in the 36th minute through a breakaway goal by Femi Azeez to make it 0-1 after a wrong decision by Paul Nardi in the QPR goal to gift the visitors an opening goal.

Jake Cooper was yellow carded for foul Richard Kone in 39th minute. Millwall a goal up looked likely to add a second than QPR despite the early pressure in the game by QPR. Millwall increased their lead in first half stoppage time to lead 0-2 thanks to a close-range header by big Serbian Mihailo Ivanovic with a header from a centre by man of the match Femi Azeez in the 45th+4 minute.

The teams went in at half time with Millwall winning 0-2 at the interval. Millwall kicked off the second half 2-0 up. QPR replaced Harvey Vale and Liam Morrison with Amadou Mbengue and Kader Dembele in the 54th minute. Thierno Ballo yellow carded in 56th minute for foul on Dunne. Smyth yellow carded in 58th minute as referee started to pull his card too frequently.

Koki Saito came on for Michael Frey and Rumarn Burrel for Paul Smyth in the 60th minute. QPR final roll of the dice saw Kieran Morgan come on for Jonathan Varane in the 72nd minute. Millwall brought on Alfie Doughty and Camiel Neghli for Thierno Ballo and Femi Azeez in the 74th minute.

QPR tried hard to get back into the game but Millwall’s defence was resolute. Millwall brought off Casper De Norre for Luke Cundle and Mihailo Ivanovic for Luke Cundle in 79th minute and 80th minute. Rumarn Burrell pulled a goal back from an assist by Koki Saito in the 85th minute. Millwall held on for a 2-1 London derby win.

**Half Time QPR 0 Millwall 2 (4 minutes added time)**
