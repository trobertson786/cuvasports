**OXFORD – CAPITAL FOOTBALL**

## RANGERS FIRE ANOTHER BLANK

Will Powell for Capital Football at the Kassam Stadium

SKYBET Championship

Oxford United (0) 0

Queens Park Rangers (0) 0

Crowd 10,478

---

QUEENS PARK RANGERS fired a second blank against Oxford United on a rainy night last evening 0-0 in front of 10,478 fans in SKYBET Championship at the Kassam Stadium after having drawn 0-0 away four days ago at Stoke City.

Oxford United started the evening in 23rd spot having come into the game off the back of one win against Southampton (home) 2-1, two draws against Bristol City (home) 0-0 last time out and Milton Keynes Dons FA Cup 3 (away) 1-1 (won 4-3 on penalties) and two defeats against Ipswich Town (away) 1-2 and Swansea City (home) 0-1.

QPR arrived at the Kassam Stadium in 11th spot having come into the game off the back of one win against Sheffield Wednesday (home) 3-0, one draw against Stoke City (away) 0-0 last time out and three defeats against West Ham United FA Cup 3 (away) 1-2, Norwich City (home) 1-2 and West Bromwich Albion (away) 1-2.

Oxford United were winless in four league meetings with QPR (drawn one, lost three), but had never gone five without a win against the Rs. QPR won this exact fixture last season 3-1 but have never won back-to-back league games at Oxford United before. Oxford winless run was extended to six against the Hoops after the result and the Rs back-to-back record still stood. Oxford United’s record without a win in their last 10 midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday) Championship games (drawn four, lost six) since defeating Luton Town 3-2 in January 2025 was extended.

Meanwhile QPR had won each of their last three away league games played in midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday), last winning four in a row in August 1971. Oxford United had only won one of their last eight league matches against London clubs (drawn three, lost four), beating Millwall 1-0 away in January 2025.

QPR kicked off the first half as the rain lashed down in South Oxfordshire at the Kassam Stadium. Oxford United started well and took the early play to QPR. Matt Bloomfield the U’s new manager was yet to win at home since his arrival. In the ninth minute Cameron Brannagan the U’s skipper, found Sam Long and the right-back whistled a right-footed shot just over Joe Walsh’s cross bar.

In the 12th minute Kader Dembele provided Paul Smyth with an inch perfect cross field pass of some 70 yards, and the winger ran at Long before crossing into the six-yard box but neither Richard Kone nor Daniel Bennie could get on the end of it.

Then with QPR’s next move down the left-flank the dangerous Irish winger, Paul Smyth crossed into Daniel Bennie, but his right-footed shot was deflected for a corner which subsequently, came to nothing for the visitors. In the 28th Stan Mills for the U’s saw a right-footed shot from outside the penalty area go wide of the left-hand post.

Then in the 29th minute QPR’s Nicholas Madsen saw a right-footed shot blocked after an assist by Daniel Bennie for the visitors. Paul Smyth went the closest to opening the scoring with a delicious right-footed shot from 22 yards out from an assist by Richard Kone which was tipped away for a corner by Jamie Cumming who was at full stretch in the 33rd minute.

The teams went in level at the interval 0-0 with Matt Bloomfield the happier of the two managers after the first half showing.

Oxford United replaced central defender Michal Helik with Brodie Spencer in the 46th minute. Oxford United kicked off the second half kicking with the wind which swirled around the three-sided venue as the rain continued to pour down.

In the 50th minute Richard Kone headed a curling Nicholas Madsen free kick over the Oxford United cross bar. Oxford United then had a free kick in the 53rd minute out by the corner flag taken by Cameron Brannagan, which was hit into the near post, but QPR cleared their lines quickly at the second attempt.

Kader Dembele was yellow carded in 55th minute. In the 56th Esquerdinha the QPR left-back was injured and was stretched off and was replaced by Rhys Norrington-Davies in the 65th minute. In the 58th minute Will Vaulks was yellow carded for that challenge.

Then U’s Cameron Brannagan took a free kick into the QPR six-yard box, but Bennie headed clear then in the follow-up play Will Vaulks saw a right-footed shot tipped over the cross bar by Joe Walsh who was in good form, for a corner. That was the U’s best scoring chance in the game.

Steve Cook saw a right-footed shot from 25 yards out whistle wide of the far post from a throw in by Paul Smyth in the 67th minute. Oxford United made a double change with Will Lankshear and Will Vaulks replaced by Mark Harris and Jamie McDonnell in the 70th minute. The latter making his U’s debut.

Oxford United improved after the double substitution and in the 73rd minute a Myles Peart-Harris cross to the back post was fired high and wide by Stan Mills at the back post when he should really have scored.

QPR made a double substitution which saw Issac Hayden and Richard Kone replaced by Sam Field and Rayan Kolli in the 75th and 76th minute.

Oxford replaced Stan Mills with Matt Phillips ex QPR in the 76th minute to boo’s, from the travelling Rs fans. Paul Smyth saw a curling right-footed shot into the near post saved well by Jamie Cumming in the 77th minute.

In the 82nd minute QPR corner taken by Nicholas Madsen punched clear by Jamie Cumming. Late on QPR head coach Julien Stephan replaced Kader Dembele with Harvey Vale in the 85th minute to add fresh legs.

There was nine minutes of added time with the score 0-0. Miles Peart-Harris was replaced by Siriki Dembele in 90th+2 minutes but neither side could manage a late winner to take the points, so they were shared.

Oxford United remained 23rd on 24 points four points adrift of Portsmouth and Blackburn Rovers on 28 points. QPR ended the evening in 12th on 40 points – 3 points off 6th placed play off zone with Wrexham and Coventry City both at home to come before the end of January.

It was QPR’s third clean sheet in four matches which was a positive, but Esquerdinha injury will need a scan and an assessment ahead of the weekend.
