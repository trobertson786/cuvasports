## Match Report

QUEENS PARK RANGERS returned to winning ways in style with a terrific 3-1 away win against SKYBET Championship struggles Leicester City in front of 27,494 fans at The King Power Stadium. The Foxes who won the Premier League in 2016 ended the day in 23rd spot and are destined for a relegation struggle with West Bromwich Albion, Oxford United, Blackburn Rovers and Portsmouth. QPR trailed to a Jordan James strike from outside the penalty area in the 14th minute which beat the unsighted Joe Walsh but then the R’s came back strongly with a goal before half time through Harvey Vale from a long ball by Ronnie Edwards, an own goal by Ben Nelson from a cross into the six yard box by Harvey Vale in the 50th minute and a solid Ronnie Edwards back post header to take all the points at a sunny King Power Stadium and head back down the M1 joyous with a first win in four games and a first away win since Hull City away in January.

Leicester City had lost four of their last eight home league games against Queens Park Rangers (won three, drawn one), including the Hoops’ most recent trip to The King Power Stadium in March 2024. QPR were looking for a league double over Leicester City for the first time since the 2010-11 campaign when they won the Championship title under Neil Warnock. Leicester City had lost their last five home league games against London teams by an aggregate score of 12-0, last winning in December 2024 in a 3-1 win against West Ham United.

Leicester City started the day in 21st position on 38 points from 37 games a point behind Blackburn Rovers (39 points) and equal with Oxford United (38 points) and West Bromwich Albion in 23rd spot on (37 points) it was all rather tight for the Foxes fans who back in 2016 we crowned Premier League Winners – how the mighty had fallen. QPR started the day in 18th spot on 47 points from 37 matches 9 points above the drop zone but winless in four games so very much on the slide.

QPR had won just two of their last 21 away league games that have kicked off at 3 o’clock on a Saturday (drawn seven, lost twelve), wins against Wrexham back in September 2025 and Bristol City in October 2025 this season were the last wins on the road. Hull City away in January (3-1 win) was a 12.30 p.m. kick off. Leicester City manager Gary Rowett, before today had won just two of his last 12 league games against QPR (drawn five, lost five) and is winless in three (drawn one, lost two).

Leicester City came into the game off the back of one win last time out on Tuesday against Bristol City (home) 2-0, three draws against Ipswich Town (away) 1-1, Middlesbrough (away) 1-1 and Stoke City (away) 2-2 and one defeat against Norwich City (home) 0-2.

QPR came into the game off the back of four defeats against Birmingham City (away) 0-1, Middlesbrough (home) 0-4, Sheffield United (home) 0-2, Southampton (away) 0-5 and one win against Hull City (away) 3-1 five games ago. Leicester City made one change from last time out against Bristol City with Divine Mukasa coming in for Bobby De Cordova-Reid. QPR made two changes with Isaac Hayden and Rayan Kolli coming in for Koki Saito and Steve Cook.

Leicester City manager Gary Rowlett started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Jakub Stolarczyk in goal, then a back four of captain Hamza Choudhury, Caleb Okoli, Ben Nelson and Luke Thomas then two holding midfielders in Oliver Skipp and Jordan James with three attacking midfielders in Abdul Fatawu, Divine Mukasa and Stephy Mavididi with Jordan Ayew up front. Leicester City made one change from last time out against Bristol City with Divine Mukasa coming in for Bobby De Cordova-Reid.

QPR manager Julian Stephan started with a 4-4-2 formation with Joe Walsh in goal, then a back four of Amadou Mbengue, captain Jimmy Dunne, Ronnie Edwards and Rhys Norrington-Davies then a four-man midfield of Paul Smyth, Isaac Hayden, Kieran Morgan and Harvey Vale with Richard Kone and Rayan Kolli up top. QPR made four changes with Isaac Hayden, Kieran Morgan, Rhys Norrington-Davies and Rayan Kolli coming in for Koki Saito, Jonathan Varane, Jake Clarke-Salter and Steve Cook.

Leicester City kicked off in the first half. Leicester City started the better of the two sides in the Spring sunshine at The King Power Stadium. Leicester City opened the scoring in the 14th minute through a Jordan James right-footed shot from 22 yards out from an assist by Ben Nelson to make it 1-0 to the Foxes. QPR lacked conviction in the midfield and up front with Richard Kone and Rayan Kolli struggling to make an impact for the travelling R’s fans. Ben Nelson was shown a yellow card in the 25th minute for a foul. Richard Kone was yellow carded in the 28th minute for a strong challenge.

Leicester City Abdul Fatawu and Stephy Mavididi caused the QPR wing backs problems with attacks down both flanks so that Issac Hayden and Paul Smyth had to track back when Leicester City came forward on the counterattack. In the 36th minute after a corner had been half cleared by Leicester City the loose ball fell to Kieran Morgan and he saw a right-footed volley hit Rayan Kolli rather than testing the goalkeeper Jakub Stolarczyk in the Foxes goal.

QPR equalised through a delightful finish from Harvey Vale after a long ball assist by Ronnie Edwards which caused the Leicester defence and goalkeeper problems to make it 1-1 in the 43rd minute. The teams went in level at 1-1 with QPR ending the second half the best of the two sides. Neither team made any half time substitutions.

QPR went up 2-1 in the 50th minute on a fast counterattack which saw Richard Kone find Harvey Vale who crossed into the six-yard box where Ben Nelson put through his own goal to give the R’s wearing a changed strip of green and white hoops the advantage.

Jimmy Dunne headed over a Ronnie Edwards cross in the 57th minute as the R’s looked to increase their lead. Then in the 58th minute Pasul Smyth saw a right-footed shot tipped away by Stolarczyk for a corner. QPR increased their lead from the corner taken by Harvey Vale as defender Ronnie Edwards rose at the back post to head in a third goal for the super hoops to make it 3-1 in the 58th minute.

Leicester City manager Gary Rowlett made a triple substitution after the third QPR goal with Oliver Skipp, Jordan Ayew and Divine Mukasa replaced by Patson Daka, Harry Winks and Bobby De Cordova-Reid in the 60th and 61st minute. Leicester City came on strongly after the change in formation, but QPR were steady in defence.

QPR manager Julien Stephan brought on Kwame Poku and Jonathan Varane for Rayan Kolli and Kieran Morgan in the 72nd minute. QPR replaced Paul Smyth with Koki Saitio a couple of minutes later in the 84th minute.

QPR ended the day in 16th spot on 50 points ten points outside the play offs. Leicester City remains deep in relegation trouble after the sixteenth loss of the campaign. QPR fans were loud and proud as they congratulated the players after the victory in the sun in the corner as the rest of the ground was empty!

**Half Time Leicester City 1 QPR 1 (2 minutes added time)**

**Full Time Leicester City 1 QPR 3 (5 minutes added time)**
