## Match Report

BARNSLEY knocked Peterborough United out of the Emirates FA Cup 2nd Round at the Weston Homes London Road Stadium in front of a crowd of 4,157 thanks to the only goal from Patrick Kelly, who came off the bench to fire Barnsley into the third round of the FA Cup for the first time in three years with a 1-0 win at Peterborough United yesterday.

Davis Keillor-Dunn, who had a goal chalked out for offside on the stroke of half-time, nodded over the first chance of the game for the visitors. Declan Frith had the hosts' best chance of the first half as he fired into the side netting after a determined run down the wing. After the break, Ben Woods fired a free kick over the bar for Posh.

Kelly could only nod a cross straight at Alex Bass before the hosts' keeper made a good save to keep out Jonathan Bland. Neil Farrugia did well down the wing to tee up Kelly for a tap in to break the deadlock with 17 minutes to go. Harley Mills had the hosts' best effort to level when he struck straight at Michael Cooper. Bass made a trio of late saves, but it was not enough for Posh to avoid missing the third round for the first time since 2023.

Peterborough United under new manager Luke Williams who had been in place a month after the sacking of Darren Ferguson saw his improving Posh side host Barnsley in the Emirates FA Cup 2nd Round tie at the West Homes London Road Stadium yesterday afternoon with a place in the Emirates FA Cup 3rd Round draw at stake.

This is the first ever FA Cup meeting between Peterborough and Barnsley, and the first in any cup competition since the 1993-94 League Cup second round, the Posh winning 4-2 on aggregate back then. Barnsley are unbeaten in their last six away games against Peterborough in all competitions (won four, drawn two) and have already won 1-0 at London Road this season in League One.

Peterborough have progressed from 19 of their last 22 FA Cup second round ties (including each of the last two seasons), going out at this stage in 2014-15, 2020-21 and 2022-23. Barnsley won their last two away games in the FA Cup with last winning three in a row on the road between January 1956 and January 1957.

Peterborough United beat fellow SKBYBET League 1 side Cardiff City 1-0 at home the Emirates FA Cup 1st Round tie whilst Barnsley beat National League side York City at home 3-2. Peterborough United came into the game after three losses against Swindon Town EFL Trophy (away) lost 0-1, Doncaster Rovers (away) lost 1-2, Stevenage (home) lost 0-1 and two wins against Stockport County (home) 3-0 and Crawley Town EFL Trophy (away) 2-1.

Barnsley came into the match after two wins against Luton Town (home) 5-0, Doncaster Rovers (away) 2-1, a draw against Stockport County (home) 1-1 and two losses against Port Vale EFL Trophy (away) 0-5 and Lincoln City EFL Trophy (home) 0-2.

Peterborough United manager Luke Williams started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Alex Bass in goal, then back four of Peter Kioso, Tom Lees, Tom O’Connor, and Harley Mills, two holding midfielders in Archie Collins and Ben Woods then three attacking midfielders in Brandon Khela, Matt Garbett and Kyrell Lisbie with Jimmy-Jay Morgan up front. Peterborough United made four changes with James Dornelly, David Okagbue, Declan Frith and Gustov Lindgren coming in for Peter Kioso, Tom O’Conner, Brandon Khela and Jimmy-Jay Morgan.

Barnsley manager Conor Hourihane started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Murphy Cooper in goal, then a back four of Jonathan Bland, Georgie Gent, Jake Rooney, and Mael de Gevigney then two holding midfielders in dam Phillips and Jon Russell – then three tucking midfielders in Neil Farrugia, captain Davis Keillor-Dunn, and Cylan Vickers with Fabio Jalo up front. Barnsley made seven changes with Jon Russell, Cylan Vickers, Fabio Jalil, Georgie Gent, Jake Rooney, Neil Farrugia and Jonathan Bland coming in for Tennai Watson, Marc Roberts, Jack Shepherd, Luca Connell, Patrick Kelly, Reyes Cleary, and Vimal Yoganathan.

Barnsley kicked off the first half and took the early play to Peterborough with negligible effect. Peterborough came back strongly through Declan Frith and Ben Woods who caused the visitors’ defence problems.

In the 28th minute Declan Frith shot left-footed into the side netting. Barnsley had Fabio Jalo and Phillips booked in the first half for fouls. Peterborough went close through Lindgren right-footed shot on target headed out by Jon Russell fort Barnsley in 44th minute. The teams went in level at half time at 0-0.
