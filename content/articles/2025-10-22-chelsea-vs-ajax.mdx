## Match Report

Chelsea hosted Ajax in the Champions League last night at Stamford Bridge and beat them convincingly 5-1 thanks to goals by Marc Guiu in 18th minute, Moises Caicedo in the 25th minute, Enzo Fernandez in the 44th minute, Estevao in 45th+4 minute and Tarique George in 48th minute. Ajax scored only goal from penalty spot through Wouf Weghorst in 33rd minute.

This is the third European meeting between Chelsea and Ajax, having met twice in the 2019-20 UEFA Champions group stage – the Blues won 1-0 in Amsterdam, before a 4-4 draw at Stamford Bridge. Ajax were winless in their last 11 major European matches against English teams (drawn two, lost nine) since a 1-0 win over Tottenham Hotspur in April 2019. They’ve failed to score a single goal in any of their last five, since a Mohammed Kudus goal against Liverpool in September 2022.

Chelsea had lost just two of their last 12 UEFA Champions League matches at Stamford Bridge (won nine, drawn one), both of which were against Real Madrid in quarterfinals in 2021-22 and 2022-23. The Blues are unbeaten in their last 12 group games at Stamford Bridge (won eight, drawn four) since a Valencia victory back in September 2019. Ajax had lost their last five major European competition matches (three in UEFA Europa League last season, first two Champions League games this season). They haven’t lost six in a row since doing so between April 1988 and September 1989.

With Ajax on a five-game losing run in major European competition, this will be the seventh time Chelsea have faced a team who’ve lost their last 5+ games in Europe – they’ve won their previous six by an aggregate score of 14-0. The only English teams to lose against a side on such a run are Man City in 1970 vs Linfield in the Cup Winners’ Cup and Man Utd in 2019 vs Astana in the UEFA Europa League. Chelsea was the only side yet to give a single minute to a player aged 30+ in the UEFA Champions League this season. Only two English sides have gone three or more games without fielding a player in their 30s – Liverpool in 2002-03 (six) and Spurs in 2016-17 (six).

John Heitinga had lost his first two UEFA Champions League matches as manager, the first Ajax boss to ever do that. Heitinga is one of four people to lose his first two games both as a player and manager in the competition with a club (with Ajax in 2003), along with Mark van Bommel at PSV, Ivan Leko at Club Brugge and Krunoslav Jurcic at Dinamo Zagreb, none of whom also lost their first three as Heitinga could do. Jorthy Mokio became the second youngest Ajax player to appear in the UEFA Champions League when he debuted on MD2, aged only 17 years and 214 days. He was the 52nd teenager to appear in the competition for Ajax – the most of any team.

Only two players have made more tackles in this season’s UEFA Champions League than Chelsea midfielder Moisés Caicedo (nine) – he made more tackles than any other player across MD2, with seven in the win over Benfica. Although he is yet to score in the UEFA Champions League for Chelsea, one of João Pedro’s six previous major European goals was netted against Ajax back in September 2023 in the UEFA Europa League for Brighton in a 2-0 win. No player has ever netted against Ajax for two different English clubs in major European competition.

Chelsea manager Enzo Maresca started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Philip Jorgensen in goal then a back four of Moises Caicedo, Wesley Forfana, Tosin Adarabioyo and Jorrel Hato then two holding midfielders in captain Enzo Fernandez and Romeo Lavia then three attacking midfielders in Facundo Buonanotte, Estevao and Jamie Gittins with Marc Guiu up front.

Ajax manager John Heitinga started with a 4-3-3 formation with Remko Pasveer in goal then a back four of Lucas Rosa, Ko Itakura, Josip Sutalo and Youri Baas then three midfielders in Wouf McConnell, Oscar Gloukh and captain Kenneth Taylor then three strikers in Raul Moro, Wouf Weghorst and Mikva Godts up front. Ajax kicked off the first half. Chelsea started positively with the rain lashing down on the bright green Stamford Bridge pitch under the floodlights. Kenneth Taylor was yellow carded in 15th minute for foul on Buonanotte which turned red, so Ajax captain was sent off.

Chelsea made it 1-0 in the 17th minute after Marc Guiu tapped in from close range from an assist by Wesley Forfana Ajax brought on Jorthy Mokpo for Oscar Gloukh in the 23rd minute. Chelsea made it 2-0 in the 27th minute as Moises Caicedo fired home a right-footed shot from 25 yards out which doubled the lead assist Jamie Gittens. Ajax penalty Wouf Weghorst fired home right footed and beat Jorgensen down to his left to make it 2-1 in 33rd minute.

Chelsea was awarded a penalty in the 43rd minute Fernandez was fouled by Weghorst. Enzo Fernandez got up to take the penalty, and he fired home right footed to make it 3-1. Estevao converted another penalty in 45th+4 minute to make it 4-1. Chelsea led 4-1 at half time. Chelsea kicked off second half 4-1 up. Tarique George shot in 48th minute assist Santos sealed 5-1 win and sent Chelsea fans home happy on a very wet and windy night.
