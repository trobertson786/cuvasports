## Match Report

JACK WILSHERE’S Luton Town were held to a 0-0 draw by a stubborn inform Rotherham United at Kenilworth Road yesterday afternoon as the two sides shared the points in front of a crowd of 11,091 fans. Wilshere’s side lacked quality and tenacity throughout the afternoon and appeared sluggish at times against a well drilled Millers outside marshalled by Joe Rafferty the skipper in a three-man defence.

Luton Town came into the game off the back of four wins Stockport County (away) 3-0, Forest Green Rovers FA Cup 1 (home) 4-3, Brighton & Hove Alion U21 EFL Trophy (home) 3-1 and Northampton Town (away) 1-0 and a loss to Mansfield Town (home) 0-2 in Jack Wilshere’s first game in charge of the Hatters after the sacking of Matt Bloomfield.

Rotherham United the visitors from South Yorkshire came into the game with three wins Lincoln City (home) 3-0, Manchester City U21 EFL trophy (home) 4-2 and Barnsley (away) 1-0, a draw 2-2 Burton Albion (home) and a single defeat at Swindon Town FA Cup 1 (away) 2-1.

At start of play Luton Town were 9th with 22 points and Rotherham United was 12th spot with 21 points. Luton Town have lost just three of their last 16 league games against Rotherham United (won seven, drawn six), keeping a clean seat in three of their last four against the Millers.

Rotherham United are unbeaten across their last four away league games against Luton Town (won two, drawn two), with this their first visit to Kenilworth Road since a 1-1 draw in the Championship in November 2022.

No team has scored as many goals as possible from set pieces in League One this season than Luton (ten), whilst only Stevenage (one) and Wycombe Wanderers (two) have conceded fewer set piece goals than the Hatters (three).

Rotherham United were unbeaten across their last six league games (won four, drawn two), last enjoying a longer run within the same campaign between January and March 2022 (ten games), a campaign which ended in promotion to the Championship.

Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere started with a 4-3-1-2 formation with Josh Keeley in goal, then a back four of Isaiah Jones, Teden Mengi, Mads Andersen and Kal Naismith then a midfield three of Jake Richards, Liam Walsh, and Cohen Bramall then Lamine Fanne then Jerry Yates and Nahki Wells up front. Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere brought in Jerry Yates for George Savile and changed from 4-4-1-1 to 4-3-1-2 formation. George Saville was on international duty with Northern Ireland but would have been suspended had he not been away anyway.

Rotherham United manager Matt Hamshaw started with a 3-5-2 formation with Cameron Dawson in goal, then a back three of Joe Rafferty, Hamish Douglas, and Reece James then a five-man midfield of Denzel Hall, Shaun McWilliams, Dan Gore, Martin Sherif, and Joe Powell with Jordan Hugill and Kian Spence up front. Rotherham United manager Matt Hamshaw brought in Kian Spence and Martin Sherif on loan from Everton for Arjany Martha and Sam Nombe aa former Hatter who was injured.

Luton Town kicked off the first half. In the seventh minute Joe Rafferty saw a right-footed volleyed cross sent into the penalty area where Jordan Hugill headed the ball on target, but his effort was well saved by Josh Keeley in the Hatters goal.

Kal Naismith was all over the place, centre left-back, centre midfield and centre forward in the space of a few moves. The utility player back at Luton Town permanently after a period on loan having moved to Bristol City from Luton originally.

Luton Town were playing some nice football and dominated the early play against a stubborn Rotherham United side. Joe Powell saw a left-footed shot deflected away off Isaiah Jones for a corner in the 23rd minute after a solid attack down their right flank.

Rotherham unbeaten in six games did not let Luton have it all their own way. After the half hour, Rotherham started to take a grip of the match and looked the more likely to open the scoring with Sherif on loan from Everton and Jordan Hugill causing problems for the Hatters.

Rotherham had four corners in the opening 43 minutes of the encounter with none for the home side. Jordan Hugill was replaced by Josh Benson in first half added time due to injury. The teams went in level at 0-0 at the interval with Rotherham, United the better of the two sides.

Rotherham United kicked off in the second half. Rotherham replaced Martin Sherif with Josh Ayres and Luton Town replaced Jake Richards with Gideon Kodua at half time.

Jack Wilshere replaced Nahki Wells with Zack Nelson in the 61st minute and he played high up the field but just behind Jerry Yates

In the 64th minute a Luton attack started by Gideon Kodua found Jerry Yates and his right-footed shot clipped the far post after Cameron Dawson got a hand on his shot.

Rotherham went down to the bare bones with no strikers after Hugill and Sherif were injured, saw Matt Hamshaw’s in-form side dig deep in the misty rain and the dark skies over Kenilworth Road to claw a deserved point to keep their unbeaten record going on the road.

Luton’s first corner came in the 75th minute but it came to nothing as Kal Naismith’s left-footed shot was blocked and cleared up field quickly. Luton brought on Millenic Alli for Cohen Bramall and Jacob Brown for his first game since March when he replaced Lamine Fanne in the 78th minute.

Luton Town pushed for a winner in second half added time with Town quick corners but the visitors from South Yorkshire held on for a valuable and deserved point.

Luton Town ended the day in 8th spot on 23 points and Rotherham united in 11th spot on 22 points.
