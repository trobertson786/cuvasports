## Match Report

CHELSEA narrowly beat Benfica 1-0 and former manager Jose Mourinho thanks to an own goal from Richard Rios in the 18th minute in front of 38,636 but the night was all about Chelsea v Jose Mourinho. Chelsea hosted Benfica and their manager the former Chelsea boss Jose Mourinho back to Stamford Bridge last night. Chelsea had won all four of their competitive meetings with Benfica (9-3 aggregate scoreline), with the most recent coming earlier this year at the FIFA Club World Cup (4-1 in the last 16 matches). This was the first meeting between Chelsea and Benfica in the group/league phase of a competition, with the previous four all having come in the knockout stages: UEFA Champions League quarterfinals in 2011-12, UEFA Europa League final in 2012-13 and the FIFA Club World Cup last 16 earlier this year (all won by Chelsea).

Benfica were winless in their last eight meetings with English teams (drawn 3 lost 5), with their last such victory coming over a decade ago, against Tottenham Hotspur in the UEFA Europa League (3-1 in the last 16 first leg). Meanwhile, they haven’t kept a clean sheet in any of their last 18 games against English sides (since a 2-0 win against Everton in November 2009, also in the UEFA Europa League). Benfica manager José Mourinho had won four of his 14 matches against Chelsea across all competitions (drawn 3 lost 7), while none of those victories have come in the seven most recent games (drawn 3 lost 4), last beating them in February 2018 (Manchester United 2-1 Chelsea in the Premier League).

Chelsea was currently on their longest losing streak in the UEFA Champions League (3 games). However, they hadn’t lost a home game in the group/league phase of the competition since September 2019 (0-1 v Valencia), going unbeaten in each of the last 11 since then (won 7 drawn 4). José Mourinho won his first ever game as a visiting manager at Stamford Bridge, with his Internazionale side beating Chelsea 1-0 in March 2010 in the UEFA Champions League. Since then, Benfica boss Jose Mourinho had failed to win in each of his six away games against the Blues across all competitions (drawn 2 lost 4).

There had been four instances in 2025 of a team losing a UEFA Champions League match in which they had led by 2+ goals, with half of those being accounted for by Benfica (2/4): 4-5 v Barcelona in January and 2-3 v Qarabag in their opening game of the current edition. Cole Palmer scored Chelsea’s only goal against Bayern Munich last time out in the UEFA Champions League, while making the most passes breaking the opposition’s defensive line (3) of any player on either team. Palmer could become just the third player to score in his first two Champions League games for the Blues, after Didier Drogba in 2004-05 and Michy Batshuayi in 2017-18 but he was unavailable due to injury.

Along with scoring Benfica’s opener against Qarabag on MD1, Enzo Barrenechea was one of only two players who made 10+ line-breaking passes (11) and won possession 10+ times (10) in the opening round of this season’s UEFA Champions League, along with Monaco’s Lamine Camara. João Pedro didn’t attempt a shot against Bayern Munich on his UEFA Champions League debut – however, he did lead all players across both teams for duels won (10), completed dribbles (3) and fouls won (4) at the Allianz Arena. Chelsea came into match after losing first Champions League match 1-3 away at Bayern Munich whilst Benfica lost 2-3 against Qarabag. Chelsea was in 28th spot whilst Benfica was in 24th in the Champions League table.

Chelsea manager Enzo Maresca started with a 4-2-3-1 Robert Sanchez in goal, then back four of Malo Gusto, Benoit Badiashile, Trevoh Chalobah and Marc Cucurella then two holding midfielders in Facundo Buonanotte and Moises Caicedo then three attacking midfielders in Pedro Neto, captain Enzo Fernandez, and Alejandro Garnacho with Tyrique George up front.

Benfica manager Jose Mourinho started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Anatoly Trubin in goal, then a back four of Amar Dedic, Antonino Silva, Nicolas Otamendi and Samuel Dahl then two holding midfielders in Richard Rios and Enzo Barrenechea then three attacking midfielders in Dodi Lukebakio, Fredrick Aursnes and Georgiy Sudakov with Vangelis Palidis up top.

Benfica kicked off the first half. Chelsea started well but it was Benfica who went close to opening the score in the 7th minute through a left-footed shot by Sudaov that was pushed away by Robert Sanchez for a corner to the visitors. In the 10th minute Pedro Neto crossed to the back post where Alejandro Garnacho took the ball down and given time shot right-footed wide of the near post.

Chelsea finally opened the scoring in the 18th minute through an own goal after a cross by Alejandro Garnacho came off the defender Ricard Rios to make it 1-0 the original cross to Garnacho came from Pedro Neto. In the 22nd minute Enzo Fernandez saw a left-footed shot from 15 yards out well saved by goalkeeper Anatoly Trubin. In the 33rd minute Barrenechea was yellow carded for a foul on Fernandez by referee Siebert.

In the 42nd minute Chelsea free kick was headed over the cross bar by Benoit Badiashile from a cross by Pedro Neto. Chelsea led 1-0 at half time. Chelsea kicked off the second half 1-0 up. Benfica had Sudakov and Rio yellow carded early in the second half for heavy challenges. Chelsea manager Enzo Maresca brought on Estevao for Facundo Buonanotte in the 54th minute.

Chelsea brought on Joao Pedro and Jamie Gittens in the 60th minute for Tyrique George and Alejandro Garnacho. Aursnes as yellow carded in the 63rd minute and became the fourth Benfica player to be carded.

Chelsea looked like he was scoring a second in the 69th minute but a left-footed cross by Pedro Neto was headed out for a corner by Barrenechea. Benfica manager Jose Mourinho replaced Antonino Silva with Tomas Araujo in the 72nd minute. Benfica then brought on Franco Ivanovic, Leandro Barreiro and Andreas Schjelderup for Lukebakio, Sudakov, and Pavlidis. Chelsea brought on Reece James and Joshua Acheampong for Malo Gusto and Benoit Badiashile in the 80th minute. In the 83rd minute Otamendi was yellow carded.

Chelsea hung on for the win to beat Benfica and Jose Mourinho side 1-0 thanks to an own goal but not before Joao Pedro was sent off for dissent in stoppage time – Chelsea’s third red card in recent matches.
