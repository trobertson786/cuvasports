## Match Report

FULHAM were beaten by a 90th+1 minute penalty converted by substitute Ross Stewart after he had been fouled by Joachim Andersen and referee Jarred Gillett pointed straight to the penalty spot. Up stepped Stewart to win the game with am added time penalty kick to reach the last eight of the FA Cup and give Saints chance of lifting the Cup 50 years ago again in May 2026 in front of a crowd of 21,974 fans down by the River Thames in SW6.

Fulham hosted Southampton in the Emirates FA Cup 5th Round tie yesterday, lunchtime at Craven Cottage in London SW6. Fulham came into the game off the back of three wins against Tottenham Hotspur (home) 2-1, Sunderland (away) 3-1 and Stoke City (away) FA Cup 2-1 and two defeats against Manchester City (away) 0-3 and West Ham United (home) 0-1 last time out. Southampton came not the game off the back of four wins against Sheffield Wednesday (away) 3-1, Queens Park Rangers (home) 5-0, Leicester City (home) FA Cup 2-1 and Leicester City (away) 4-3 and one draw against Charlton Athletic (home) 1-1.

This was the fourth FA Cup tie between Fulham and Southampton, with the Cottagers progressing in the third round in 1998-99 after a replay and the Saints winning in the second round in 1914-15 and third round in 2017-18. Southampton have lost three of their last four matches against Fulham (drawn one), all in the Premier League in the 2022-23 and 2024-25 seasons. The Saints last beat them in May 2021 under Ralph Hasenhüttl, a 3-1 home top-flight victory.

Fulham were looking to reach the FA Cup quarter-final stage for a third time in the last four seasons (also 2022-23 and 2024-25). Their previous three quarter-final appearances came over a period of 20 seasons before this. Since beating Manchester United in the 1976 final, Southampton had been eliminated in seven of their eight FA Cup ties against top-flight opponents while playing outside of the top division at the time. The one exception was a 2-0 win against Blackpool in the third round in 2010-11.

Kevin has been involved in three of Fulham’s five FA Cup goals this season (2 goals, 1 assist), while he had the most shots (eleven) and most touches in the opposition’s box (twenty-two) of any Premier League player across the third and fourth rounds in 2025-26. Oriol Romeu could make his 20th FA Cup appearance in this match – the Spaniard started for Southampton in their last FA Cup tie against Fulham in January 2018 in his first spell, a 1-0 win under Mauricio Pellegrino.

Fulham manager Marco Silva started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Benjamin Lecomte in goal, then a back four of Timothy Castagne, Joachim Andersen, Jorge Cuenca and Ryan Sessegnon then two holding midfielders in Harrison Reed and captain Tm Cairney then three attacking midfielders in Samuel Chukwueze, Emile Smith Rowe and Oscar Bobb with Rodrigo Muniz up front.

Southampton manager Tonda Eckert started with a 4-2-3-1 formation Daniel Peretz in goal, then back four of James Bree, captain Taylor Harwood-Bellis, Nathan Wood and Ryan Manning then two holding midfielders in Flynn Downes and Tom Fellows then three attacking midfielders in Casper Jander, Finn Azaz and Leo Scienza with Cyle Larin up top.

Southampton were a retro of the 1976 FA Cup Winning kit when Bobby Stokes goal beat Manchester United at the old Wembley Stadium to lift the trophy. Fulham kicked off the first half defending the Putney End where the Saints fans were housed in big numbers up front the South Coast.

Southampton started well and took the early play to Fulham with Leo Scienza and Ryan manning making good runs into the penalty area. Fulham came forward through Oscar Bobb and forced a corner of their own in the seventh minute. The resultant corner came to nothing with Joachim Andersen heading straight into the arms of Daniel Peretz in the Saints goal.

Fulham was left-footed shot by Oscar Bobb from 20 yards out saved easily by Peretz in the 13th minute. Fulham scored in 20th minute after a Peretz kicked out hit Manning and bounced into path of Muniz who slotted ball home, but referee adjudged the Fulham player was not ten yards away, so the goal was cancelled out.

Southampton missed a gilt-edged chance to open the scoring on the counterattack in the 22nd minute after Leo Scienza shot wide of goal when he should have scored. Fulham continued to press for an opening goal with Andersen heading close at the back post from a corner by Reed in the 29th minute which was pushed away for another corner by Peretz.

Southampton went close again in the 33rd minute as Leo Scienza snatched at another chance and shot wide of the target. Southampton attacked strongly in their 36th minute through Larin but the final pass inside the penalty area went astray and out for a corner. Southampton corner in the 37th minute but it came to nothing again for the visitors.

Teams went in level at halftime 0-0. Southampton kicked off the second half.  Neither side made any half time substitutions. Fulham went close through a Rodrigo Muniz header from a Timothy Castagne header in the 47th minute. Harrison Reed right-footed shot from 22 yards out in the 49th minute was saved by Peretz easily.

Fulham scored again in the 55th minute but the goal was adjudged to be offside. Tom Fellows on the counterattack brought a good save out of Benjamin Lecomte in the 58th minute. In the 62nd minute Jorge Cuenca was yellow carded for foul on Scienza.

Fulham manager Marco Silva brought Alex Iwobi for Tom Cairney in the 64th minute. The introduction of Iwobi did not bring instant success to teh Cottagers as Southampton were still strong in the middle of the park.

Southampton went close to scoring through Finn Azaz, but his effort was deflected off Cuenca onto the cross bar and away for a corner. Southampton manager Tonda Eckert made a double change in the 71st minute with Cyle Larin and Casper Jander replaced by Ross Stewart and Cameron Bragg.  Castagne shot wide with a left-footed effort in the 74th minute as the game was still without a goal.

Fulham came forward through Ryan Sessegnon and Emile Smith Rowe in the 78th minute before Sessegnon left-footed close-range shot was deflected for a corner. Southampton replaced Leo Scienza and Tom Fellows for Samuel Edozie and Kuryu Matuski in the 82nd minute. Ryan Sessegnon saw a left-footed close-range shot saved by Peretz in the 86th minute.

Fulham brought on Josh King for Emile Smith Rowe in the 89th minute. Southampton were awarded a penalty in the 90th minute after Joachim Andersen fouled Stewart. Ross Stewart took the penalty and gave Southampton a 0-1 lead in the 90th minute. Fulham immediately brought on Raul Jimenez for Harrison Reed in 90th+2 minutes but it was too late.

Southampton reached the last eight and it was a valiant victory at Craven Cottage to send the South Coast fans wild with joy.
