WEST HAM UNITED went three games, unbeaten after a 3-1 home win over Sunderland at the London Stadium yesterday lunchtime. West Ham United welcomed back African Nations winner with Senegal’s El Hadji Malik Diouf as he started on the bench but was given a rapturous return.

West Ham United opened the scoring in the 14th minute through Crysencio Summerville with a close-range header beyond the diving Robin Roefs from a lovely right wing centre cross by captain Jarrod Bowen for the assist to make it 1-0 to the home side.

West Ham United were awarded a penalty after Ollie Scarles was taken down by Trai Hume and referee Thomas Bramall pointed to the spot. Jarrod Bowen took the penalty to make it 2-0 and sent the Hammers on their way to a lovely home win in the 28th minute.

West Ham United came into the game off the back of two wins against Tottenham Hotspur (away) 2-1 and Queens Park Rangers FA Cup 3 (home) 2-1, a draw against Brighton & Hove Albion (home) 2-2 and two defeats against Nottingham Forest (home) 1-2 and Wolverhampton Wanderers (away) 0-3.
