## Match Report

Ollie Watkins emerged from the bench to spearhead a superb Aston Villa revival as they came from behind to confirm their Premier League title credentials at Chelsea with an 11th successive win in all competitions 2-1 in front of 39,765 fans in SW6..

Chelsea deservedly led after 37 minutes when striker Joao Pedro got a vital touch in a goalmouth scramble as Villa tried to clear Reece James' corner. Villa, seeking to equal a club record set in both September 1897 and March 1914, had been second best until manager Unai Emery made a triple substitution just before the hour, sending on Watkins, Amadou Onana and Jadon Sancho for Emiliano Buendia, John McGinn, and Donyell Malen.

Emery's golden touch worked inside four minutes as Watkins raced on to Morgan Rogers' pass, Chelsea keeper Robert Sanchez saving at his feet only to see the rebound bounce in off the striker. Villa was transformed, and it needed crucial saves by Sanchez from Ian Maatsen and Boubacar Kamara to keep Chelsea, who had dominated for so long, on level terms. However, Chelsea was unable to keep a rampant Villa at bay, Watkins the scorer once more as he rose to power Youri Tielemans' corner past Sanchez. Villa's win keeps them just three points behind Premier League leaders Arsenal.

Chelsea came into the game off the back of two wins against Cardiff City Carabao Cup (away) 3-1 and Everton (home) 2-0, two draws against Newcastle United (away) 2-2 and AFC Bournemouth (away) 0-0 and one loss against Atalanta Champions League (away) 1-2. Aston Villa came into the game off the back of five wins against Manchester United (home) 2-1, West Ham United (away) 3-2, Basel Europa League (away) 2-1, Arsenal (home) 2-1 and Brighton & Hove Albion (away) 4-3.

Chelsea lost two of their last three Premier League home games against Aston Villa, though they did win this exact fixture 3-0 in December last season. Aston Villa have won three of their five Premier League games against Chelsea under Unai Emery (drawn one, lost one) as many as in their previous 20 against the Blues before the Spaniards arrival (drawn three, lost fourteen) .Having scored in 21 consecutive Premier League home games against Aston Villa between December 1998 and September 2021 (50 goals in total), Chelsea have now failed to score in two of their last three at Stamford Bridge against the Villans.

Aston Villa had won each of their last 10 matches in all competitions and could equal their all-time club record of 11 consecutive victories, set in both September 1897 and March 1914. Chelsea have already suffered two home defeats in the Premier League this season (W4 D2 L2), losing to Brighton in September (1-3) and Sunderland in October (1-2). If they lose here, they will have lost more home league matches this term (three) than they did in Enzo Maresca’s first full campaign in 2024-25 (won twelve, drawn five and lost two).

Chelsea were unbeaten in the three Premier League games they’ve played this season against teams starting the day above them in the table, beating Liverpool 2-1 (October), Tottenham 1-0, and drawing against Arsenal (both in November).Aston Villa have won their last seven league games, last having a longer run in the top-flight between October and December 1910 (nine). The Villans have also won 12 of their 21 Premier League away games against London sides under Unai Emery (drawn five, lost four), more than they had in their previous 59 (won eleven, drawn ten and lost thirty-eight).

Chelsea’s top scorers in the league this season have netted just five goals (Pedro Neto and João Pedro). After 17 games of a Premier League campaign, only on three occasions has Chelsea’s top scorers netted fewer at this stage – 4 in 2022-23, 3 in 2015-16 and 4 in 1995-96. Enzo Fernández has created more chances from open play (twenty-four) and made more passes breaking the opponent’s defensive line (seventeen) than any other Chelsea player in the Premier League this season.

Aston Villa’s Morgan Rogers has been directly involved in more Premier League goals than any other English player across the opening 17 matchdays this season (10 – 7 goals, 3 assists); he could the third Villa player to net 2+ goals in three consecutive appearances in the competition after Dean Saunders (1992) and Dion Dublin (1998).

Chelsea manager Enzo Maresca started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Robert Sanchez in goal, then back four of Reece James, Benoit Badiashile, Trevoh Chalobah and Marc Cucurella then two holding midfielders in captain Enzo Fernandez and Moises Caicedo then three attacking midfielders in Pedro Neto, Cole Palmer, and Alejandro Garnacho with Joao Pedro up front.

Aston Villa manager Unai Emery with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Emi Martinez in goal, then a back four of Matty Cash, Ezri Konsa, Victor Lindelof, and Ian Maatsen then two holding midfielders in Boubacar Kamara and Youri Tielemans then three attacking midfielders in captain John McGinn, Emi Buendia and Morgan Rogers with Donyell Malen up front.

Aston Villa kicked off the first half. Cole Palmer went close with a left-footed shot in the second minute, but his shot went wide of the far left-hand post guarded by Argentine goalkeeper Emi Martinez. In the 18th minute Enzo Fernandez shot wide with a right-footed effort which went wide of the right-hand post. In the 29th minute Matty Cash was yellow carded for a foul on Alejandro Garnacho.

Reece James fired a right-footed shot wide of the target in the 34th minute after further pressure on the Villa goal. Chelsea opened the scoring from a corner headed in by Joao Pedro in the 37th minute to make it 1-0. Cole Palmer left-footed shot in 43rd minute saved by Martinez. Chelsea led 1-0 at half time. Chelsea kicked off in the second half. Emery is currently the manager who can do no wrong, and it was his game-changing substitutions that turned this encounter on its head to confirm that Villa simply must be in the Premier League title conversation.

The Spaniard saw his side struggling to lay a glove on Chelsea, who were 1-0 ahead and comfortable, so acted decisively to set the platform for a superb victory. He did not even wait until the hour before making the three substitutions that shifted the emphasis entirely in Villa's direction. Watkins was transformative, as his pace and direct running suddenly caused havoc in a Chelsea defence that had been in cruise control. He was razor-sharp, albeit with a sprinkling of good fortune, to pounce for Villa's equaliser, then showed his power in the air to send an unstoppable header past the previously defiant Sanchez.

It came at the end of a spell of fierce Villa pressure as Chelsea wilted and Emery's side grew in stature. Villa remains hard on the heels of pace-setters Arsenal and Manchester City, with a visit to Emirates Stadium to come in three days' time. They will travel to north London with growing belief they can continue to shake up the established order at the top of the Premier League.

Chelsea head coach Enzo Maresca was confined to the stands serving a suspension, and this was a spectacle that would have made for disappointing viewing for the Italian. All was progressing smoothly for an hour, Chelsea's superiority helped by a desperately lacklustre Villa performance until opposite number Emery put his stamp on proceedings. For all Chelsea's dominance, Villa keeper Emi Martinez was hardly over-worked, Maresca's team running out of steam badly in the final half hour.

Chelsea could have few complaints as Villa completed a dramatic turnaround to secure victory as they ran out of ideas, Cole Palmer tiring but unhappy to be replaced by Estevao with 18 minutes left. They had no answer once Villa went ahead, failing to cause any anxious moments in the visitors' defence in the closing stages. Chelsea drops down to fifth in the table - level on points with Manchester United - with the manner of this loss raising further questions about whether they can make a serious impact on the title race.
