## Match Report

QUEENS PARK RANGERS came from behind twice in the game to end a four-match winless run with a brilliant win over Hull City 3-2 at a rain swept Matrade Loftus Road Stadium yesterday afternoon after the third international break. QPR goals from Ilias Chair, Jimmy Dunne the skipper and the winner from Rumarn Burrell was the tonic for Julian Stephan’s side as the R’s returned to winning ways in front of a crowd of 16,657 in W12. Hull City goals were converted by Leeds United loan sharpshooter, Joe Gerhardt, and Enis Destan but the Hoops were victorious in the end.

QPR had come into the match off the back of one win Swansea City (away) 1-0, one draw Sheffield United (away) 0-0 and three defeats Derby County (away) 0-1, Ipswich Town (home) 1-4 and Southampton (home) 1-2. Hull City came into the game in better form with three wins Portsmouth (home) 3-2, Norwich City (away) 2-0 and Leicester City (home) 2-1, a draw against Charlton Athletic (home) 1-1 and a defeat to Derby County (away) 1-2.

QPR won their last league game against Hull City, but they haven’t registered consecutive wins against the Tigers since February 1970 but equalled that record with today’s win. Hull City had won five of their last nine league visits to Queens Park Rangers (drawn one, lost three), including a 3-1 victory last season under former boss Tim Walter. Queens Park Rangers had lost their last three home Championship matches, losing four in a row in 2022. The last Hoops manager to lose four home league games in a row was Chris Ramsey between February and April 2015 (five in a row). Hull City were one of three teams yet to score a Championship goal from outside the box this season, along with Middlesbrough and Sheffield United.

Queens Park Rangers manager Julien Stephan started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Ben Hamer in goal, then a back four of Mbengue, captain Jimmy Dunne, Jake Clarke-Salter making a return for his first game of the campaign after a lengthy injury and Rhys Norrington-Davies then two holding midfielders in Jonathan Varane and Nicolas Madsen then three attacking midfielders in Kader Dembele, Rumarn Burrell and Ilias Chair with Richard Kone up top.

Hull City manager Sergej Jakirovic started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Ivor Pandur in goal, then a back four of captain Lewie Coyle, John Egan, Charlie Hughes, and Ryan Giles then two holding midfielders in Amir Hadziahmetovic and Regan Slater then three attacking midfielders in Joe Gerhardt, Matt Crooks, and Kyle Joseph with Enis Destan up front.

QPR kicked off in the first half. QPR started well, they went close with a shot in the 7th minute by Rumarn Burrell, then Richard Kone saw a right-footed shot deflected for corner. Kader Dembele saw a left-footed close-range shot saved by Ivor Pandur in the 12th minute. Hull City took the lead in the 17th minute through a close-range right-footed shot by Joe Gelhrdt from an assist by Regan Slater to make it 0-1 after a fast counterattack to set the Tigers on the way.

Ilias Chair saw a right-footed shot in the 29th minute saved by Padur down to his left. QPR then went close through Ramarn Burrell with a header in 36th minute which was deflected off a Hull City defender at the back post for another corner which came to nothing. QPR equalised in the 38th minute through close right-footed shot from Ilias Chair to make it 1-1 and the initial shot by Amadou Mbengue was only partly blocked by John Egan and the loose ball fell nicely for Chair to convert the chance.

Richard Kone saw a right-footed shot in 40th minute tipped around the post by Ivor Pandur for corner as Rangers upped the tempo. The teams went in level 1-1 half time. Hull City kicked off second half. Hull City went 2-1 up in the 51st minute through a close header by Enis Destan after beating Jake Clarke-Clarke to the cross by Ryan Giles to head beyond the diving Ben Hamer in the Hoops goal to make it 2-1 to the visitors. QPR equalised in the 55th minute through a Jimmy Dunne left-footed curling shot from an assist by Illias Chair to make it 2-2 and set up a grandstand finish in the pouring rain in W12.

QPR replaced Jake Clarke-Slater with Steve Cook in the 61st minute. Hull City Amir Hadziahmetovic and Enis Destan with Joel Ndala and Darko Gyobi in the 62nd and 63rd minutes. The Hoops went 3-2 up in the 66th minute through a Rumarn Burrell right-rooted shot from close range inside the Hull penalty area after Chair’s initial swivel and left-footed shot was blocked and the loose ball fell to Burrell and he buried his shot beyond the diving Pandur to take the lead.

QPR brought on Kieran Morgan and Rayan Kolli for Amadou Mbengue and Richard Kone in the 72nd minute. Hull City brought on Mohammed Belloumi for Regan Slater in 72nd minute. Ryan Giles saw a left-footed shot on target from 22 yards out parried away by Ben Hamer in the 73rd minute as the Hoops goalkeeper kept shot out. Rayn Kolli saw a cross into six-yard box cleared by Hull City defender for another corner, that corner ended with Steve Cook firing a bullet right-footed shot just over the cross bar otherwise it would have surely been 4-2.

QPR replaced Kader Dembele with Koki Saito in the 79th minute. Kolli came forward and saw a right-footed shot saved by Pandur in the 81st minute which was a top save to deny the Hoops another goal. With time running out Hull City brought on David Akintola for Kyle Joseph in the 84th minute but further fresh legs for the Tigers brought no effect on the remaining play for the visitors. Matt Crooks was yellow carded in the 90th+2 minute for a rough challenge and he was on the booking all afternoon for James Linington despite the tricky wet conditions.

QPR held on to win 3-2 for the first time taking 3 points since a 1-0 away win at Swansea City back on 22nd October after only taking a single point in the last four matches it was a welcome victory for boss Julien Stephan and the faithful R’s fans who trudged home pleased with the result and good inspiring performance in the rain.

QPR ended the day in 15th on 22 points with Blackburn Rovers and Norwich City both away in the next week whilst Hull City remained in the play off area in 6th spot on 25 points after the loss.

**Half Time Queens Park Rangers 1 Hull City 1 (3 minutes added time)**
