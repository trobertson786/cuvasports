## Match Report

TOTTENHAM HOTSPUR were held to a 2-2 home draw by Manchester United in the lunchtime kick off in front of 61,620 fans at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium. Manchester United took the lead through a close range Bryan Mbeumo effort from an assist by Amad Diallo in the 32nd minute and United led until a late Spurs show with two goals in seven minutes first through Tel from an assist by Destiny Udogie in the 84th minute, then a Richarlison goal from an assist by Wilson Odobert in the 90+1 minute with Spurs leading the crowd thought that was the second win of the season at home but United equalised through Mathius de Light header from a corner from an assist by captain Bruno Fernandez in the 90th+6 minute to make it 2-2.

Tottenham Hotspur came into the game off the back of a 4-0 (home) win over Copenhagen in the Champions League and a 0-1 (home) defeat to Chelsea. The previous three games were mixed 2-0 (away) defeat to Newcastle United, a 3-0 (away) win at Everton and a 0-0 (away) draw with Monaco in the Champions League. Manchester United came into the game at the City Ground off the back of four wins against Brighton & Hove Albion (home) 4-2, Liverpool 2-1 (away), Sunderland 2-0 (home), and Chelsea 2-1 (home) a draw 2-2 with Nottingham Forestt (away) and a defeat with Brentford 1-3 (away).

Tottenham completed the Premier League double over Manchester United last season – they last won three consecutive league games against the Red Devils in 1959/1960. Manchester United is winless in their last seven meetings with Tottenham in all competitions (drawn two, lost five), losing each of the last four – the last team to win five in a row against the Red Devils were Liverpool between 2000 and 2002.

No side has lost more home Premier League matches in 2025 than Tottenham Hotspur (nine). Only in 1994 and 2003 (10 in each) have Spurs reached double figures for home defeats in a year in their league history. Manchester United were unbeaten in their last four Premier League games (won three, drawn one), their joint best run under manager Rúben Amorim, while United last had a longer run without losing in January/February 2024 (five games), which was also the last time they netted two or more goals in five in a row.

Across spells with Brentford and Spurs, Thomas Frank has suffered nine defeats in his last 16 home Premier League matches (won three, drawn four). Before this run, his Brentford side had won seven of their previous eight home games (D1). Only Liverpool (156) has had more shots in the Premier League this season than Manchester United (153) and no one has had more shots on target than they have (fifty-four). 66.7% of their shots have come from inside the box this season (102/153), their highest ratio since 2012-13 (66.8%), their last title-winning season. Spurs registered an xG of just 0.1 in their 1-0 defeat to Chelsea on MD10, the lowest by a team in a Premier League match so far this season. They are averaging just 9.7 shots per game this season (97 in ten games), their lowest average in a Premier League campaign on record since 1997-98.

Only three teams have given a lower proportion of minutes to English players in the Premier League this season than Spurs (7.8%) – it’s their lowest in a single campaign (previous lowest was 8.2% in 2023-24).Only against Southampton and Brighton (five) has Man Utd’s Bryan Mbeumo scored more Premier League goals than he has against Spurs (four), with three of his four against them coming at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium. Manchester United’s Bruno Fernandes has 53 Premier League assists – since his debut in February 2020, only Mohamed Salah (64) and Kevin De Bruyne (58) have more. He’s assisted in his last two away games and is looking to do so in three away appearances in a row for the first time since November 2020.

Tottenham Hotspur manager Thomas Frank started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Guglielmo Vicario in goal, then a back four of Pedro Porro, Captain Cristian Romero, Micky van de Ven the captain and Djed Spence then two holding midfielders in Joao Paulinho and Pape Sarr then three attacking midfielders in Brennan Johnson, Xavi Simons, and Richarlison with Kolo Muani up front.

Manchester United manager Ruben Amorim started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Senne Lammens in goal, then a back three of Matheus de Ligt, Harry Maguire, and Luke Shaw then a four-man midfield of Noussair Mazraoui, Casemiro, captain Bruno Fernandes and Patrick Dorgu then two attacking midfielders in Bryan Mbeumo and Amad Diallo with Matheus Cuna with up front.

Tottenham Hotspur Remembrance minutes silence ahead of kick off which was observed impeccably by both sets of fans. Tottenham Hotspur kicked off the first half. Tottenham Hotspur started well and took the early play to Manchester United forcing two early corners but United defensively survived. then with United’s first attack Bryan Mbeumo came forward but his left-footed shot from inside the Spurs penalty area went out wide but Matheus Cunha was badly placed to receive the ball, so the chance was wasted.

Tottenham should have scored in the 16th minute, but Richarlison saw a poor header go wide from a centre by Brennan Johnson down the right flank. Manchester United looked good of late in the Premier League. United thought they had penalty shots in the 22nd minute when Richarlison handled cross into the box, but referee Barrott waived play on, and the score remained 0-0.

Spurs corner in 26th minute come to nothing. Then Johnson booked yellow card for tug on De Ligt from a corner for United in 29th minute. United took the lead through a Bryan Mbeumo close range shot from an assist by Amad Diallo in the 32nd minute to make it 0-1 and continue Spurs poor home league record this season. Romero was yellow carded in the 34th minute for dissent.

United led until a late Spurs show with two goals in seven minutes first through Tel from an assist by Destiny Udogie in the 84th minute, then a Richarlison goal from an assist by Wilson Odobert in the 90+1 minute with Spurs leading the crowd thought that was the second win of the season at home but United equalised through Mathius de Light header from a corner from an assist by captain Bruno Fernandez in the 90th+6 minute to make it 2-2.

Matthijs de Ligt scored an equaliser deep into stoppage time to earn Manchester United a point in a dramatic finale to their Premier League match at Tottenham. The Dutch defender headed home a corner in the last of six minutes of injury time to snatch a draw for United in a game they had control of - then almost lost.

Bryan Mbeumo had put the visitors ahead in the first half by heading home Amad Diallo's precise right-wing cross. When United goalkeeper Senne Lammens made excellent saves to deny Cristian Romero and Micky van de Ven after the break, Tottenham looked to be heading for their third successive home Premier League defeat.

However, the book that accompanied Mathys Tel's introduction for Xavi Simons midway through the second half turned to cheers when he swiveled on to a cross six minutes from time, evaded the attentions of De Ligt, and crashed a shot home.

The relief inside the stadium was palpable - but much more was to come. Richarlison - the subject of so much criticism recently - thought he had won it for Spurs when he flicked Wilson Odobert's long-range shot past Lammens. But United, who finished with 10 men as striker Benjamin Sesko was injured after they had used all five substitutes, had other ideas. With Lammens also up for United corner, De Ligt headed home from an acute angle to give his side a point.

There were disbelieving looks from both sides at the end. Richarlison was in tears, having jubilantly celebrated his goal, only to realize it had not proved to be the winner. United head coach Ruben Amorim was equally stunned, having watched his side almost toss away a game they seemed certain to win.
