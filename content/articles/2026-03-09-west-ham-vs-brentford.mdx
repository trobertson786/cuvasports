## The Story of the Match

West Ham United put their relegation worries to one side to reach the quarterfinals of the FA Cup for the first time in 10 years by defeating London rivals Brentford 5-3 on penalties. In a fiercely contested cup tie under the London Stadium lights, Jarrod Bowen and Igor Thiago traded braces in normal time to send the game the distance.

With nothing to separate the sides after 120 minutes of breathless football, the match was ultimately decided by an astonishing lapse in judgement from the spot. Dango Ouattara was guilty of making a terrible mistake while attempting a Panenka. His woefully under-hit chip was comfortably read by Alphonse Areola, completely shifting the momentum of the shootout.

The hosts were clinical from 12 yards, dispatching every single one of their spot-kicks before Konstantinos Mavropanos stepped up. The Greek defender emphatically struck the winning kick to book the Hammers a last-eight home tie with Leeds United, providing a welcome boost in an otherwise turbulent season.
