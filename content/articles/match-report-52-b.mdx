## Match Report

WREXHAM moved into the SKYBET Championship play off zone after leaving it late to beat QPR 3-2 at Matrade Loftus Road thanks to two goals in second half added time to win in style it was a real smash and grab old style nicking of the points at the end of a keenly fought encounter in W12 thanks to goals by Josh Windass, in the 90th+3 Minute and Oliver Rathbone, in the 90th+4 minute both with powerful right-footed strikes to beat Joe Walsh.

QPR started well and the home side came forward through Kader Dembele who fired a left-footed shot against the Wrexham cross bar and then the ball bounced out a Wrexham defender who lost possession to Harvey Vale who fired a left-footed volley straight into the visitors net to make it 1-0 in the 6th minute.

Wrexham came on strong after the interval and in the 54th minute after heavy pressure Callum Doyle fired home with a left-footed shot on the near post to beat Joe Walsh and make it 1-1 from an influential Nathan Broadhead. Steve Cook headed in a Nicholas Madsen corner in the 80th minute to make the QPR fans happy but after Amadou Mbengue saw red for a second yellow card in the 90th minute the Red Dragons blundered forward and scored two killer goals to take the points through Windass and Rathbone.

QPR came into the match off the back of one win against Sheffield Wednesday (home) 3-0, two draws against Oxford United (away) 0-0 and Stoke City (away) 0-0 and two defeats against West Ham United FA Cupo 3 (away) 1-2 and Norwich City (home) 1-2. Wrexham came into the game off the back of two wins against Derby County (away) 2-1 and Blackburn Rovers (away) 2-0, two draws against Leicester City (home) 1-1 and Nottingham Forest FA Cup 3 (home) 3-3 (won on penalties) and a defeat against Norwich City (home) 1-2.

QPR had won each of their last four league games against Wrexham in succession, as many as across their prior 12 matches combined between 1960 and 2001 (drawn four, lost four). Wrexham had won just two of their nine away league games against QPR in club history, winning 2-1 in September 1962 and 1-0 in October 1980. This was their first visit to Loftus Road to face QPR since a 2-0 defeat in March 2004 in the third tier.

QPR had kept a clean sheet in each of their last three league games, drawing the last two 0-0. They last went four without conceding in December 2024. Wrexham had won each of their last two away league games and will be looking to win three in succession in the second tier of English football for the very first time. Wrexham’s Sam Smith scored nine goals in his last 16 league starts, netting in each of his last three in a row.

Wrexham kicked off the first half defending the School End wearing a changed strip of yellow shirts and dark green shorts and were well supported by a sell-out crowd.

QPR started well and the home side came forward through Kader Dembele who fired a left-footed shot against the Wrexham cross bar and then the ball bounced out a Wrexham defender who lost possession to Harvey Vale who fired a left-footed volley straight into the visitors net to make it 1-0 in the 6th minute.

Dan Scarr was yellow carded in the 11th minute for a pull-on Richard Kone. QPR had a free kick taken by Nicholas Madsen which Steve Cook headed but was deflected for a corner. QPR pushed for a second goal before the interval and pressured Wrexham strongly with good runs by Dembele, Smyth and Madsen.

Amadou Mbengue saw a bicycle kick go narrowly over the Wrexham cross bar in the 30th minute after strong defending by the Red Dragons. Amadou Mbengue was given a yellow card in the 44th minute.

QPR led 1-0 at half time through the only goal from Harvey Vale. QPR kicked off the second half, neither side made any half time substitutions. Ben Sheaf saw a left-footed shot saved by Walsh in the 53rd minute. Wrexham came on strong after the interval and in the 54th minute after heavy pressure Callum Doyle fired home with a left-footed shot on the near post to beat Joe Walsh and make it 1-1.

Paul Smyth was yellow carded in the 61st minute and so was Wrexham manager Phil Parkinson for dissent. QPR replaced Kieran Morgan with Isaac Hayden in 63rd minute.

Wrexham went close to another goal in the 69th minute but Nathan Broadhead’s right-footed shot was saved by Walsh. Nicholas Madsen for QPR came forward but his right-footed shot was poor and went wide of the target.

QPR made a double substitution with Rayan Kolli and Daniel Bennie who came on for Kader Dembele and Paul Smyth in 734th minute. Wrexham brought on George Dobson and Oliver Rahbone for Nathan Broadhead and Ben Sheaf in 74th and 75th minute.

QPR took the lead again through a Steve Cook header from a Nicholas Madsen corner in the 80th minute. QPR took off Harvey Vale and replaced him with Sam Field in 81st minute.

Wrexham took off Dan Scarr and Matty James for Sam Smith and Josh Windass in the 84th minute.

QPR looked like winning 2-1 and go 9th on 43 points equal on points with 6th placed Bristol City. Kieffer Moore saw a right-footed shot saved by Walsh in the 87th minute. QPR Amadou Mbengue was given a second yellow and red and went off in 90th minute. Wrexham made a further late substitute Issa Kabore with Ryan Longman in 90th minute. QPR then lost their shape and although Ronnie Edwards was on the bench he was not used to bolstering up the defence.

Wrexham came on strong in added time as QPR were down to ten men, and the Red Dragons scored two very late goals in second half added time to win thanks to Josh Windass in the 90th+3 Monte and Oliver Rathbone in the 90th+4 minute much to the shock of the watching Rs faithful.
