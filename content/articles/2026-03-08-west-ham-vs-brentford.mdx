## Match Report

West Ham United put their relegation worries to one side to reach the quarterfinals of the FA Cup for the first time in 10 years by defeating London rivals Brentford 5-3 on penalties.

Dango Ouattara was guilty of making a terrible mistake while attempting a Panenka before Konstantinos Mavropanos struck the winning kick to book the Hammers a last-eight home tie with Leeds United.

In a dramatic game, Jarrod Bowen's 19th-minute close-range finish - after Brentford failed to deal properly with a corner - put the Hammers ahead before Igor Thiago equalised eight minutes later with his 20th goal of the season.

Captain Bowen restored West Ham's lead by sending Caoimhin Kelleher the wrong way from the penalty spot in the 34th minute following Michael Kayode's foul on Adama Traore.

It looked like the Hammers were closing in on victory until in-form Brazilian forward Thiago forced extra time from the spot after Crysencio Summerville's 80th-minute foul on Kayode.

There were no further goals in extra time, with Kelleher producing an outstanding one-handed save to keep out substitute Callum Wilson.

But West Ham, coupled with Ouattara's poor attempt, held their nerve during the spot-kicks to go through.

The Final Emirates FA Cup 5th Round saw a Premiership tussle between an improving West Ham United and a solid Brentford side at the London Stadium in E20 last night. Brentford had already beaten West Ham United in the Premier League 0-2 at London Stadium earlier in the season so came into the game as favorites.

West Ham United came into the Cup tie off the back of two wins against Fulham (away) 1-0 and Burton Albion (away) FA Cup 4 1-0, two draws against AFC Bournemouth (Home) 0-0 and Manchester United (home) 1-1 and a single loss to Liverpool (away) 2-5.

Meanwhile, Brentford came into the Cup match off the back of two wins against Burnley (away) 4-3 and Macclesfield (away) FA Cup 4 1-0, two draws against AFC Bournemouth (away) 0-0 and Arsenal (home) 1-1 and a single defeat against Brighton & Hove Albion (home) 0-2.

Brentford had won seven of their 10 games against West Ham United in all competitions since becoming a Premier League side in 2021 (drawn one, lost two); they haven’t beaten a side more often during that period. This is just the third time that West Ham United and Brentford have been drawn against each other in the FA Cup – the Bees progressed in a fourth-round replay in 1926-27 while the Hammers won 1-0 in the third round in 2022-23 via a Saïd Benrahma strike.

West Ham United had been eliminated from their last three FA Cup fifth round ties – they last reached the quarter-final in the 2015-16 campaign, winning 5-1 away at Blackburn in that season’s fifth round. This is the first time since 2018-19 that Brentford has reached the fifth round of the FA Cup, though the Bees have failed to progress beyond this stage on the previous three occasions since beating Blackburn 2-0 in 1988-89 and going out to eventual winners Liverpool in the quarterfinals.

Crysencio Summerville had been involved in all three of West Ham United’s FA Cup goals this season (2 goals, 1 assist) and he has six goal involvements in his last five FA Cup appearances overall (3 goals, 3 assists). Brentford had kept a clean sheet in both their FA Cup matches this season, as many as in their previous 20 games in the competition. Indeed, they did so in three successive matches in the competition in 2004-05.

West Ham United manager Nuno Espirito Santo started with a 4-4-2 formation with Alphonse Areola in goal, then a back four of Kyle Walker-Peters, Axel Disasi, Konstantinos Mavropanos and Ollie Scarles then four-man midfield of Mateus Fernandes, Tomas Soucek, Mohamadou Kante and Pablo with Adama and captain Jarrod Bowen up front.

Brentford manager Keith Andrews started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Caoimhin Kelleher in goal, then a back four of Michael Kayode, Kristoffer Ajer, Nathan Collins and Keane Lewis-Potter then two holding midfielders in Jordan Henderson and Yehor Yarmoliuk then three attacking midfielders in Dango Ouattara, Mikkel Damsgaard, Kevin Schade and Igor Thiago up top.

Brentford kicked off the first half with Leeds United at home in the FA Cup Quarter Final at stake. West Ham United took the lead in the 19th minute through a close range left-footed close range shot by Jarrod Bowen from a header to him by Tomas Soucek headers which deflected into his party off Kristoffer Ajer to make it 1-0.

Brentford equalised through Nathan Collins’ header, which was touched by Igor Thiago to make it 1-1 in the 28th minute with a header which went in off Igor Thiago’s chest with the goal given after VAR decision.

West Ham United had a VAR check on a penalty in the 32nd minute after Mitchel Kayode had fouled Adama. Referee awarded penalty to West Ham United in 33rd minute after VAR. Penalty was converted by Jarrod Bowen for the second goal of the night to make it 2- in the 33rd minute.

Brentford had a call for a VAR decision for a penalty in the 40th minute but it was not given.

Brentford equalised late on through an Igor Thiago penalty converted in the 81st minute after Crysencio Summerville had fouled to make it 2-2.

Both sides failed to score in extra time of 30 minutes, and the game went to penalties with West Ham United winning 5-3 thanks to Dango Outtara missing his kick.
