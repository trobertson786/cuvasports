## Match Report

BRENTFORD saw off a poor West Ham United side 2-0 last night at the London Stadium as the Hammers failed to win at home again for the fourth time this term under new manager Nuno Espírito Santo thanks to an Igor Thiago strike in the 43rd minute assist by Kevin Schade and Brentford who made three second half substitutions it was  the third brought the final goal in the 90th+2 minute as Mathias Jensen added a second from an assist by Keane Lewis-Potter to seal the victory in style. Brentford ended at night in 13th spot, West Ham United 19th 2 points above bottom placed Wolverhampton Wanderers after another poor display.

West Ham United one off the bottom of the Premier League going into the evenings match hosted Brentford from the other side of London in an East - West London Derby encounter at the London Stadium. West Ham United came into game off the back of three losses, a draw and a win Arsenal lost 0-2 (away), Everton 1-1 drew (away), Crystal Palace lost 1-2 (home), Tottenham Hotspur lost 0-3 (home) and Nottingham Forest won 3-0 (away). Meanwhile Brentford came into match ff the back of a win, two draws and two losses Manchester City lost 0-1 (home), Manchester United won 3-1 (home), Fulham lost 1-3 (away), Aston Villa drew 1-1 (home) and Chelsea drew 2-2 (home).West ham United were 19th with 4 points and Brentford 16th with 7 points so it was a key relegation tussle.

West Ham United had won just one of their eight Premier League games against Brentford (draw 1, lost 6); among sides the Hammers have faced 3+ times in the competition, only against Brighton (6%) do they have a lower win rate than they do against the Bees (13%). Brentford had enjoyed more Premier League wins over West Ham (six) than they have against any other side, while, among teams they have faced 3+ times in the competition, only against Southampton (83%) do they have a higher win rate than against the Hammers (75%).

West Ham lost all four of their Premier League London derbies this season by an aggregate score of 12-2. The Hammers haven’t lost five consecutive league games against fellow London clubs since September 2010. For only the second time in their history, West Ham United lost each of their opening three home league games of a season, having also done so last term. The Hammers have never begun a league campaign with four successive home defeats before. Brentford lost all three of their away league games this season; the Bees had only lost three of their final 14 league matches of last term (won 7, drawn 4).

West Ham United had lost all three of their Premier League home games this season, while Brentford have lost all three of their Premier League away games. It’s just the third time a side with no home points will host a side with no away points having both played 3+ home/away games in a season in the competition, with both previous instances won by the away team: Sheffield Wednesday September 2002, Everton September 1999, and Everton 1-2 Luton Town September 2023.

Under Keith Andrews in 2025-26, Brentford are averaging their lowest possession (38.3%), fewest shots per game (just 8.1 shots), and fewest passes per game (only 319) than in any of their Premier League seasons under predecessor Thomas Frank. West Ham United’s Nuno Espírito Santo has already beaten Brentford in the Premier League this season, winning 3-1 with Nottingham Forest on the opening day of the season when he was in charge at the City Ground. Should he also win here, it would be the earliest into a season (by both date and matchdays) a manager has ever beaten the same opponent with two different clubs in the competition.

West Ham United’s Lucas Paquetá has made 99 Premier League appearances and is set to be the 30th Brazilian to reach 100 in the competition – the seventh nation to have 30+ players reach a century, after England (475), France (59), Republic of Ireland (56), Scotland (53), Wales (42), and Spain (40). Brentford had made 34 long throw-ins into the box in the Premier League this season, at least eight more than any other side, while opponents West Ham United had made the fewest (2). 30 of Brentford’s had been by Michael Kayode – at least nine more than any other player in 2025-26.

West Ham United manager Nuno Espírito Santo started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Alphonse Areoloa in goal, then with a back four of Kyle Walker-Peters, Jean-Clair Todibo, Max Kilman and Ollie Scarles then two holding midfielders in Andy Irving and Tomas Soucek then three attacking midfielders in captain Jarrod Bowen, Mateus Fernandez, and Crysencio Summerville with Lucas Paqueta up front.

Brentford manager Keith Andrews started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Caoimhin Kelleher in goal, then a four of Michael Kayode, Kristoffer Ajer, Sep van den Berg, captain Nathan Collins, and then two holding midfielders in Yehor Yarmoliuk and Jordan Henderson then three attacking midfielders in with Kevin Schade, Mikkel Damsgaard and Dango Ouattara with Igor Thiago up front.

Brentford kicked off the first half with the ground not entirely full as the Hammers fans were boycotting the fixture because of wanting a change of ownership with wanting David Sullivan and Karen Brady to sell the club up and leave.

There were some empty seats at London Stadium last night and this was because of the latest phase of protests organised by supporters' group Hammers United, who want chairperson David Sullivan and vice-chair Karren Brady to leave. The pair have been at the club for 15 years and were instrumental in the Hammers' move from the much smaller Upton Park to the London Stadium, which hosted the 2012 Olympics and has a capacity for football matches of more than 62,000.

However, many fans feel the club "sold their soul" with the move from Upton Park, which created a far better atmosphere, with the covered athletics track at London Stadium also leaving those in the top tier with a poor view. West Ham United started well and took the early match to Brentford and forced three early corners, but they came to nothing as Brentford defended them well. Brentford long thro in 18th minute caused problems for West Ham but the home side survived it. In the 25th minute Nathan Collins headed over a free kick from Jordan Henderson as the Bees looked the more likely to score first.

Igor Thiago the big Brazilian striker was a handful for West Ham’s back line, especially at set pieces. In the 28th minute Crysencio Summerville came forward and appeared to be fouled in the penalty area by Mikkel Damsgaard, but referee Andy Madley waived away appeal for a penalty for the Hammers.

Brentford went close again to opening the score in the 30th minute through a back post-footed shot by Igor Thiago from an assist by Dango Ouattara. The Bees were denied by Areola in the 31st minute from a Damsgaard header from 12 yards out which the goalkeeper tipped away. Brentford corner ended with Thiago shot deflected for another corner to the visitors. Brentford went close through an Ouattara back post header from the corner in the 33rd minute as the Hammers survived again.

Brentford went close from another corner in the 39th minute at Ouattara headed wide at the back post when it would have been easier to score. The West Ham goal was under siege for much of the last few minutes of the first half but in the 43rd minute they succumbed to a right-footed strike from Igor Thiago who scored to make it 0-1 from an assist by Kevin Schade. In first half stoppage time Jarrod Bowen saw a left-footed shot from the edge of the Brentford penalty area tipped away by Kelleher for corner which came to nothing. Igor Thiago scored a second in the first half stoppage time, but his effort was ruled out for offside.

The teams went in at half time with the scores with Brentford leading 1-0 which was thoroughly deserved. West Ham United kicked off the second half 0-1 down. The second half saw more of the same and West Ham United made three halftime substitutions and then two further in the second half, but it did not change their fortunes. Brentford deserved the 2-0 win it could and should have been three or four as they were by far the better side on the night. Brentford made three second half substitutions and the third brought the final goal in the 90th+2 minutes as Mathias Jensen added a second from an assist by Keane Lewis-Potter to seal the victory in style. Brentford ended at night in 13th spot, West Ham United 19th 2 points above bottom placed Wolverhampton Wanderers after another poor display.
