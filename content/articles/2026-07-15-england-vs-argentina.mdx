## The Story of the Match

ENGLAND'S dream of reaching a first World Cup final since 1966 was shattered in the cruellest of fashions in Atlanta as Argentina scored twice in the closing stages, both goals fashioned by Lionel Messi, to win the semi-final 2-1.

The game was tight and tense in the first half, with neither side able to force the breakthrough in front of 68,239 fans. England grew into the contest and took the lead after 55 minutes when Anthony Gordon, so often England's threat down the left, fired the Three Lions in front to send the English supporters into raptures.

Thomas Tuchel's side sensed their moment of history was at hand. Jordan Pickford produced important saves to keep England ahead as Argentina pushed forward in search of a leveller, and Alexis Mac Allister headed against the post as the reigning champions turned up the pressure.

The equaliser came after 85 minutes and it bore the hallmark of the greatest player of his generation. Messi slipped the ball to Enzo Fernandez on the edge of the area and the Chelsea midfielder lashed a strike beyond Pickford to level the tie at 1-1 and swing the momentum firmly back towards Argentina.

With extra time looming, Messi struck the decisive blow. He drifted to the byline and delivered a perfect cross into the box in the second minute of stoppage time, and substitute Lautaro Martinez rose to head Argentina in front and complete a devastating late turnaround. There was no way back for England.

It was a heartbreaking end to a wonderful tournament for England, who had come so close to the final only to be undone by two moments of Argentine quality when it mattered most. Argentina march on to defend their title against Spain, while England must regroup for the third-place play-off against France.
