## Match Report

ARSENAL remained on course to win the Premier League title with a London derby 1-1 draw against a well organised Brentford at the GTECH Community Stadium last night thanks to a 61st minute header by Noni Madeuke from an assist by Piero Hincapie which was the difference between the two sides until Brentford equalised through a close range Lewis Keane-Potter header from an assist by Sep van den Berg headed flick on from a Michael Kayode long throw in to make it 1-1 in the 71st minute to set up a grandstand finish in front of a crowd of 17,224. It was two points dropped by Arsenal with the draw on the night.

Brentford hosted Arsenal in the only Premier League match last night at the GTECH Community Stadium in West London. Brentford came into the match off the back of three wins against Newcastle United (away) 3-2, Aston Villa (away) 1-0 and Sheffield Wednesday (away) FA Cup 3 2-0 and two losses against Nottingham Forest (home) 0-2 and Chelsea (away) 0-2. Arsenal came into the game off the back of seven wins against Inter Milan (away) 3-1 Champions League, Chelsea 3-2 (away) EFL Cup 1st leg and Portsmouth (away) 4-1 FA Cup 3, Kairat Almaty (home) 3-2 Champions League, Leeds United (away) 4-0, Chelsea 1-0) home EFL Cup 2nd Leg and Sunderland (home) 3-0 last time out, one draw against Nottingham Forest (away) 0-0 and one loss against Manchester United (home) 2-3.

Brentford’s first ever Premier League victory came against Arsenal, in their first game in the competition in August 2021. Since then, they’re winless in their last eight against the Gunners (drawn two, lost six). Arsenal lost their first Premier League game 2-0 against Brentford but have since won their last three by an aggregate score of 7-1. Brentford have won just one of their last 11 home London derbies in the Premier League (drawn six, lost four) and are winless in seven since a 2-1 win over Crystal Palace in August 2024.

Arsenal had lost just one of their last 23 Premier League London derbies (won seventeen, drawn five), while since the start of 2022-23 they’ve lost just one of their 20 away games against fellow London sides (won fourteen, drawn five).This is just Brentford’s fourth Premier League game played on a Thursday – they lost 2-0 at Spurs in December 2021, beat Nottingham Forest 2-0 in May 2025 and drew 0-0 with Spurs last month.

Arsenal was unbeaten in their last 12 midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday) Premier League games (W9 D3). However, they’re without a win or a goal (drawn one, lost two) in any of their last three, specifically on Thursdays. Brentford lost 2-0 against Nottingham Forest in their last home league game, ending a seven-game unbeaten run at the Gtech Stadium. Since the start of last season, they’ve only lost consecutive home league games twice, the first time of which was a 2-0 defeat to Nottingham Forest followed by a loss to Arsenal.

Brentford’s Igor Thiago has scored nine goals in his last 10 Premier League home games, netting four braces in that run. Only Erling Haaland (5) has scored multiple goals in more different home matches this term. Arsenal boss Mikel Arteta has won 41 of his 67 Premier League London derbies as manager, the best win rate (61%) of anyone to take charge of at least 20. Overall, only Arsène Wenger (106 from 194 games) and José Mourinho (42 from 70 games) have won more London derbies than the Spaniard. Bukayo Saka has been involved in 35 goals in 63 Premier League London derbies (17 goals, 18 assists), the third most of any Arsenal players after Thierry Henry (55) and Dennis Bergkamp (38).

Brentford manager Keith Andrews started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Caoimhin Kelleher in goal, then a back four of Michael Kayode, Kristoffer Ajer, Sep van den Berg and Rico Henry then a two-man midfield of Yehor Yarmoliuk and Vitaly Janelt then three attacking midfielders in Dango Ouattara, captain Mathias Jensen and Myles Lewis-Potter with Igor Thiago up front.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta started with a 4-3-3 formation with David Raya in goal, then back four of Jurrien Timber, Cristian Mosquera, captain Gabriel, and Piero Hincapie then three midfielders in Eberechi Eze, Martin Zubimendi and Declan Rice then three attackers in Noni Madeuke, Viktor Gyokeres and Leandro Trossard up front.
