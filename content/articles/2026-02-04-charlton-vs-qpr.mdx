## Match Report

CHARLTON ATHLETIC drew a blank with QPR in a London derby encounter in front of a crowd of 21,290 fans in SE7. Bot sides had chances with Lyndon Dykes and Charlie Kelman both ex-QPR strikers going close for the home side. Richard Kone had the best chance for QPR but neither could score.

Charlton Athletic came into the match off the back of two wins against Leicester City (away) 2-0 last time out, Sheffield United (home) 1-0, and three losses against Millwall (away) 0-4, Derby County (home) 1-2 and Chelsea (home) FA Cup 3 1-5.

QPR came into the game in indifferent form with two wins against Sheffield Wednesday (home) 3-0 way back on 4th January and Coventry City (home) 2-1 last time out drawn two against Oxford United (away) 0-0 and Stoke City (away) 0-0 and lost against West Ham United (away) FA Cup 3 1-2 and Wrexham (home) last time out 2-3.

Charlton have lost just one of their last 10 home league games against QPR (won six, drawn three), and have won each of the last three in a row without conceding.

Following their 3-1 win in August, QPR are looking to complete their first league double over Charlton since 2007/08. Charlton Athletic have only failed to score in one of their last 24 home league games, netting in each of the last 11 since a 1-0 loss to Leicester in August.

Eight of Charlton’s nine league wins this season have been to nothing – the Addicks have won just one of their 20 league games when conceding at least once this term (drawn seven, lost twelve).

Charlton Athletic Manager Nathan Jone started with a 3-4-3 formation with Thomas Kaminski in goal, then a back three of Kanye Ramsay, Lloyd Jones, and Amari Bell then a four-man midfield of Harry Clarke, making his debut Conor Coady, Greg Docherty the captain and Luke Chambers then three attackers in Sonny Carey, Lyndon Dykes, and Charlie Kelman up front.

QPR manager Julian Stephan started with a 4-5-1 formation with Joe Walsh in goal, then a back four of captain Jimmy Dunne, Ronnie Edwards, Steve Cook and Rhys Norrington-Davies then a five-man midfield of Kader Dembele, Nicholas Madsen, Isaac Hayden, Harvey Vale, and Paul Smyth with Richard Kone up front.

QPR kicked off the first half defending the Jimmy Seed Stand End of the Valley. Richard Kone saw a right-footed shot from inside the Charlton penalty area saved easily by Thomas Kaminski in the 2nd minute.

Harvey Vale was yellow carded in the 4th minute for a high boot on Conor Coady. Richard Kone and Isaac Hayden saw weak right-footed shots go wide of the target in the 7th and 11th minutes of the game as QPR came forward on the counterattack.

Lyndon Dykes saw a right-footed shot from 22 yards out take a deflection off Steve Cook and go out for a corner in the 12th minute. In the 20th minute, from a Charlton corner the loose ball felt Dykes, but his right-footed shot was fired high and wide of the target.

Charlton corner in the 23rd minute taken long to the back post was cleared by QPR up field quickly through Ronnie Edwards. Charlton free kick on the edge of the QPR penalty area after Isaac Hayden fouled Dykes in 26th minute. The resultant free kick came to nothing as the shot was deflected off the QPR defensive wall for another corner and the third of the first half which was punched away by Walsh for another corner.

The Addicks were having the best of the first half and Dykes shot wide again from 15 yards out in the 28th minute as the home side pressed for an opening goal.

In the 29th minute after another good Charlton attack Sonny Carey saw a left-footed shot from 12 yards out blocked by Jimmy Dunne. In the 31st minute a free kick by Luke Chambers was pushed low down his right by Joe Walsh for a sixth corner for Charlton.

Conor Coady saw a close-range right-footed shot saved by Walsh’s legs in the 32nd minute. Charlton were the better team in the opening 45 minutes of this London derby.

The teams went in level at half time 0-0. Charlton kicked off the second half and in the 46th minute Charlie Kelman first a right-footed shot on target which Walsh tipped away for a corner in the 47th minute.

QPR corner in 49th minute taken by Nicholas Madsen into the penalty area which ended with Steve Cook volley a right-footed shot high and wide of the target in the 50th minute.

Luke Chambers’ left-footed shot from 15 yards out was saved by Walsh in the 54th minute. Lyndon Dykes right-footed shot from close range saved by Walsh and follow up was put wide by Dykes in the 57th minute.

QPR replaced Koki Saito with Daniel Bennie in the 58th minute. Charlton went close through a left-footed shot by Bell which went high over the cross bar from close range after a long throw in the 63rd minute.

QPR corner in 67th minute taken by Madsen into the near post where Daniel Bennie headed wide of the target. Halton replaced Charlie Kelman with Matty Godden in the 70th minute.

On the counterattack for QPR in the 73rd minute Rhys Norrington-Davies fired a left-footed shot wide of the target. Charlton replaced Luke Chambers with Tyreece Campbell in the 75th minute, as soon as he came on Campbell ell whipped down the left flank and crossed into Sonny Carey at the back post, but his right-footed shot went wide of the far post.

QPR replaced Paul Smyth with Rayan Kolli in the 77th minute. Kone weak left-footed shot saved by Kaminski in the 80th minute. Both sides had chances in the latter minutes of the second half but neither side could score a winning goal in what remained of a dismal encounter.

QPR ended on 44 points and Charlton on 36 points 4 points above the drop zone.
