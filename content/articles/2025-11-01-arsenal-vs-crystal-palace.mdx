## Match Report

ARSENAL continued their excellent start to the season as they remained top of the Premier League after a 1-0 win over Crystal Palace at the Emirates Stadium in front of crowd of 60,103 fans thanks to a shot fired home from inside the penalty area by Eberechi Eze in the 39th minute. It was the difference between the two teams. Arsenal ended the day 4 points ahead of second placed Bournemouth and 3rd placed Sunderland!

Arsenal hosted Crystal Palace in a London derby fixture at the Emirates Stadium yesterday afternoon as the League leaders and this season’s Community Shield winners and last season’s FA Cup winners went head-to-head. Arsenal came into the match off the back of five straight wins Atletico Madrid 4-0 (home), Fulham 1-0 (away), West Ham United 2-0 (home), Olympiakos 2-0 (home) and Newcastle United 2-1 (away). Meanwhile Crystal Palace came into the game off the back of two losses AEK Larnaca 0-1 (home), Everton 1-2 (away) a draw Bournemouth 3-3 (away) and two wins Dynamo Kiev 2-0 (away) and Liverpool 2-1 (home).

Arsenal had won five of their last six Premier League meetings with Crystal Palace, though the exception was a 2-2 draw in this exact fixture last season. Crystal Palace had won just one of their last fourteen away league games against Arsenal (draw five, lost eight), beating them 3-2 in April 2019 under Roy Hodgson. Arsenal had only lost two of their 70 home Premier League matches when they’ve started the day top of the table (won fifty-two drawn sixteen), losing to Hull in September 2008 and Man City in February 2023. They have won seven out of eight (drawn one) at the Emirates when in 1st place since that loss to Man City, scoring 28 goals in those eight games. Crystal Palace had drawn eight of their last 14 Premier League matches (won five, lost one), including a 3-3 draw at home against Bournemouth. Since the start of April, the Eagles have drawn nine Premier League matches – three more than anyone else.

Arsenal had not faced a single shot on target in either of their last two Premier League matches. On record since 2003-04, the only side to go three games in a row without facing a shot on target are Manchester City between May and August 2014. Arsenal faced the fewest shots (sixty-five), fewest shots on target (eighteen), the lowest xG (4.8) and conceded the fewest goals (three) in the Premier League this season. It’s the joint fewest goals they’ve conceded after eight Premier League matches, along with three in 1998-99, which was also the last time they kept five clean sheets in their first eight matches.

Crystal Palace led the Premier League for shots on target (forty-three), xG (17.4) and big chances (thirty-three) this season. However, they are underperforming their xG by the biggest margin (-5.4) and have missed the biggest chances (twenty-two) in the competition. Arsenal led the way for corner goals (seven) and goals from set pieces (ten) in the Premier League this season, finding the net with a set piece in six of their eight games so far. It is the earliest into a season any team has scored 10 set piece goals in Premier League history.

Leandro Trossard scored Arsenal’s winner against Fulham last week, while the Belgian has netted in each of his last two Premier League starts against Crystal Palace. He’s never ended on the losing side when scoring for the Gunners in the competition (twenty-one games, won seventeen, drawn four), with only Gabriel Martinelli (38), Cesc Fàbregas (32) and Paul Merson (27) scoring in more without defeat for Arsenal. Eight of Jean-Philippe Mateta’s last nine Premier League goals for Crystal Palace have been scored in London, and he’s netted five goals in his last eight London derbies. He’s also only the third Palace player to net five goals in their eight matches of a Premier League season, along with Andrew Johnson in 2004-05 and Wilfried Zaha in 2020-21.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta started with a 4-3-3 formation with David Raya in goal, then the back four of Jurren Timber, William Saliba, Gabriel, and Myles Lewis-Skelly then three midfielders in Eberechi Eze, Martin Zuimendi and Declan Rice then three attackers in Bukayo Saka, Viktor Gyokeres and Gabriel Martinelli up top.

Crystal Palace manager Oliver Glasner started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Dean Henderson –in goal then a back three of Chris Richards, Maxence Lacroix, and captain Marc Guehi then a four-man midfield of Daniel Munoz, Daichi Kamada, Adam Wharton and Tyrick Mitchell then Ismaila Sarr and Yeremy Pino with Jean-Michel Mateta up front.

Arsenal kicked off the first half. Crystal Palace forced aa corner in the 9th minute taken by Will Hughes after early Arsenal pressure in the initial stages of the match but Arsenal’s defence survived. During the opening 15 minutes of the game Arsenal had 83 percent possession of the football without scoring. Crystal Palace pushed up from the back to confidence the play in the middle third of the pitch as Oliver Glasner the manager tried hard to thwart the runs of Eberechi Eze and Leandro Trossard.

David Raya made a good save in the 24th minute to deny Palace’s Daniel Munoz. Adam Wharton saw a left-footed shot from 22 yards go wide of the target in the 27th minute. Jurrien Timber was fouled by Pino in the 29th minute and the Gunners had a free kick in a dangerous spot which was taken by Declan Rice.

Arsenal came forward through Viktor Gyokeres in the 33rd minute but he had to go back on track to find Declan Rice and then he found Leandro Trossard who fired on target, but Dean Henderson saved well for Palace in the 33rd minute. Defensively Crystal Palace was resolute but after a free kick which Arsenal had just outside the Crystal Palace penalty area taken by Declan Rice was only half cleared by the visitors defence and former Palace midfielder volleyed home right-Foote from 12 yards to make it 1-0 to the Gunners in the 39th minute which was thoroughly deserved.

Arsenal led 1-0 at half time through the only goal by Eze. Arsenal replaced William Saliba with Cristian Mosquera at eh interval.

Crystal Palace kicked off the second half a goal down. Arsenal went close to scoring a second goal in the 50th minute after heavy pressure but the woodwork and a Palace defender on the line denied Arsenal a second goal. In the 58th minute a long ball over the top by Gabriel found Trossard but his shot when one on one with Dean Henderson went narrowly wide of the goals and was then cleared by Palace for a corner which when taken came to nothing.

Crystal Palace replaced Yeremy Pino with Arsenal former striker Eddie Nketiah in the 59th minute. Arsenal forced another corner through Gyokeres off Maxence Lacroix in the 63rd minute. Arsenal manager Mikkel Arteta brought on Gabriel Martinelli for Bukayo Saka in the 66th minute.

Martin Zubimendi saw a left-footed shot blocked in the 71st minute as Arsenal continued to press for a second goal, but Palace held firm. Crystal Palace brought on Will Hughes for Daichi Kamada in the 73rd minute. Crystal Palace on the counterattack in the 79th minute through Mateta forced a corner which was taken by Will Hughes but headed over the cross bar by the captain Marc Guehi in the 80th minute. Arsenal replaced Riccardo Calafiori by Pedro Hincapie and Declan Rice by Mikel Merino in the 82nd minute.

Arsenal replaced Eberechi Eze with Myles Lewis-Skelly in the 87th minute. Crystal Palace brought on Jefferson Lerma, Christantus Uche and Borja Sosa in the 90th minute for Adam Wharton, Tyrick Mitchell, and Daniel Munoz. Arsenal held on to win the game 1-0 with the only goal by Eze in the 39th minute as Arsenal went 4 points clear at the top of the Premier League from second place Bournemouth and Sunderland in 3rd!

Arsenal next play Brighton in the Carabao Cup midweek whilst Crystal Palace are in action in the same competition against Liverpool.
