## Match Report

ARSENAL welcomed back Champions League football to London N5 with Olympiacos the visitors from Greece the Greek side had beaten Arsenal on the last three occasions they had had played at the Emirates Stadium. Arsenal beat Olympiacos through the only goal tapped in by Gabriel Martinelli in the 12th minute after a Viktor Gyokeres slot hit the post and bounced back to Martinelli to tap in and in the 90th+1 stoppage time Bukayo Saka added a second with a close range left-footed shot to make it 2-0.

This will be the 13th time Arsenal and Olympiakos have faced each other in European competition, with none of the previous 12 ending in a stalemate and both sides winning six games apiece. Arsenal faced Olympiakos on four occasions during Mikel Arteta’s reign as manager (won 2 lost 2); only facing PSV (6) more often in European competition. The Greeks eliminated them from the UEFA Europa League in 2019-20 (last 32), before the Gunners eliminated Olympiakos from the same competition the following season (last 16). Olympiakos have won two of their last three away games against English sides (lost 1); one of those being against Arsenal at the Emirates (1-0 in March 2021), along with a 4-2 win at Aston Villa in May 2024. This is as many victories as the Greek side managed in their first 17 away games versus English opponents in major European competition (won 2 lost 15).

Arsenal have won each of their last five games in the group/league phase of the UEFA Champions League, their longest run since winning six in a row between December 2004 and November 2005. Olympiakos have only scored one goal in their last six UEFA Champions League matches (D1 L5), despite attempting 51 shots across those outings and hitting the target 11 times.

Among teams who have been involved in the last two editions of the UEFA Champions League, Arsenal have conceded fewer goals per game than any other team in the competition (0.72 – 18 in 25 games). Indeed, they also have the lowest expected goals conceded per game average (0.9) amongst that group. Based on the xG of shots on target faced, Arsenal’s David Raya has prevented more goals than any other goalkeeper in the UEFA Champions League since the start of last season (+5.8 – nine goals conceded from 14.8 xG on target, excluding own goals). Noni Madueke made the joint-most into the penalty area (7) in the opening round of the 2025-26 UEFA Champions League campaign (level with Rodrygo, 7).

He also progressed to the ball up field via carries by a total of 210 metres in the match against Athletic Club, with Jérémy Doku being the only winger with a higher total across all MD1 fixtures (285 metres v Napoli). Ayoub El Kaabi has scored 21 goals in 24 appearances in major European competition for Olympiakos. However, he failed to score with any of his four shots on MD1 of this UEFA Champions League game against Pafos: his joint-most attempts without finding the net in a major European outing for the Greek side. Martín Zubimendi made 13 line-breaking passes in his first UEFA Champions League game for Arsenal (v Athletic Club on MD1); the most of any player in the match. Indeed, this was also the most by a midfielder in the defensive half (10) across all the matches played across the opening matchday of 2025-26.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta started with a 4-3-3 formation with David Raya in goal, then a back four of Ben White, William Saliba, Gabriel, and Myles Lewis-Skelly then a three-man midfield of Mikel Merino, Martin Zubimendi, and captain Martin Odegaard then three attackers in Leandro Trossard, Viktor Gyokeres and Gabriel Martinelli in attack.

Olympiacos manager Jose Luis Mendilibar started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Kostas Tzolakis in goal, then a back four of Costinha, captain Panagiolis Retsos, Lorenzo Pirola and Francisco Oriega then two holding midfielders in Dani Garcia, Santiago Hezze then three attacking midfielders in Gelson Martins, Chiquinho and Daniel Podence with Ayoub El Kaabi up front.

Olympiacos kicked off the first half wearing all grey with red and white trim. Arsenal went close to opening the scoring with their opening attack in the second minute through Myles Lewis-Skelly who crossed into the Olympiacos penalty area for Gabriel Martinelli who headed wide of the target. Arsenal opened the scoring through a simple tap in from Gabriel Martinelli to make it 1-0 in the 12th minute after Viktor Gyokeres initial shot had hit the post and fell into the path of Martinelli. In the 19th minute Daniel Podence saw a shot tipped over the cross bar by David Raya in the Gunners’ goal.

Arsenal continued to play with pace and tenacity going forward but did not add a second goal quickly. The visitors Olympiacos came into the game after more than 25 minutes and pegged Arsenal back in their own half for periods of the game. The tempo of the game was played at a much quicker pace than Chelsea and Benfica last evening across London in SW6. In the 40th minute Viktor Gyokeres was through on goal, but he fired his right-footed shot high over the cross bar into the Clock End when he should have scored. Then with the next attack in the 41st minute Leandro Trossard fired a right-footed shot wide from a cross by Gabriel Martinelli.

Arsenal led 1-0 at half time. Arsenal kicked off the second half. Olympiacos started well in the second half and forced an early corner, but it came to nothing. Trossard fired wide with a curling shot in the 49th minute fort the Gunners. Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta brought on Declan Rice for Mikel Merino in the 57th minute and Jurrien Timber on for Ben White in the 58th minute. Olympiacos brought on Mendi Taremi for Gelson Martins in the 62nd minute just after Martin Zubimendi was yellow carded for fouling him. Arsenal came forward through ball carrier Martin Odegaard but his pass to Trossard saw the Arsenal striker fire a shot on target which was blocked by an Olympiacos defender in the 66th minute.

Olympiacos pulled a goal back in the 66th minute but Chiquinho was offside, so the goal was struck off. Arsenal brought on two further substitutes with Gabriel Martinelli replaced by Ebere Eze, Leonardo Trossard for Bukayo Saka in the 73rd minute and Cristian Mosquera for Gabriel in 75th minute. Olympiacos replaced Dani Garcia for Lorenzo Scipioni in the 74th minute. In the 82nd minute Martin Odegaard was denied by Tzolakis and Pirola otherwise Arsenal would have finally doubled their lead. Costinha shot tipped away by Tzolakis.

Olympiacos brought on Christos Mouzakitis and Gillian Biancone and Gabriel Stretezza in the 85th minute for Santiago Hezze, Panagiotis Retsos and Chiquinho. Arsenal added a second in the 90th+1 stoppage time Bukayo Saka added a second with a close-range left-footed shot to make it 2-0. Arsenal held on to win 2-0 and remain in the top third of the Champions League after the narrow 2-0 win.
