## Match Report

CHELSEA let a 2-0 lead slip to 2-2 as Leeds United fought back at Stamford Bridge to share the points in front of 39,253 fans. Leeds United came from two goals down scored for  Chelsea by Joao Pedro with his 10th goal of season from a left-footed chip over Karl Darlow from close range after an assist by Cole Palmer in the 24th minute and a Cole Palmer penalty converted from the spot in the 58th minute after Jaka Bijol had fouled Joao Pedro but Leeds United came back scoring goals by Lukas Nmecha from the penalty spot after Jayden Bogle was fouled by Moisés Caicedo in the 67th minute and then substitute Noah Okafor equalised with a left-footed shot from the centre of the box to the bottom left corner from an assist by Lukas Nmecha in the 73rd minute to share the points

Chelsea hosted Leeds United at Stamford Bridge last night in the Premier League with both teams recording 3-1 wins last time out Chelsea at Wolverhampton Wanderers and Leeds United at home to Nottingham Forest. Chelsea came into the match with Leeds United off the back of six wins for Liam Rosenior the new coach against Napoli 3-2 (away) Champions League, Crystal Palace (away) 3-1, Pafos 1-0 (home) Champions League, Brentford (home) 2-0 and West Ham United (home) 3-2 and Wolverhampton Wanderers (away) 3-1, and a two defeats against Arsenal (home) 2-3 and (away) 0-1 in the EFL Cup Semi Final 1st and 2nd Legs.

Leeds United came into the game at Stamford Bridge off the back of three wins against Nottingham Forest (home) 3-1, Fulham (home) 1-0 and Derby County (away) FA Cup 3 3-1, one draw against Everton (away) 1-1 and one loss against Arsenal (home) 0-4. Chelsea was unbeaten in their last seven Premier League home games against Leeds, winning the last six in a row (D1). Their last defeat was in December 1999, losing 2-0 thanks to a Stephen McPhail brace. Following their 3-1 win at Elland Road in December, Leeds are looking to complete the league double over Chelsea for the first time since their 1991-92 title winning campaign.

Leeds United were looking to become just the second promoted side to complete the Premier League double over Chelsea, after Charlton Athletic in 2000-01. Chelsea was winless in their three midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday) Premier League games this season (drawn one, lost two), including a 3-1 loss at Leeds United at Elland Road back in December.

Chelsea had won their first two home Premier League matches under Liam Rosenior – he’s looking to become the first Blues boss to win his first three at Stamford Bridge since Maurizio Sarri in September 2018. Leeds United have won just two of their last 25 away Premier League matches (drawn six, lost seventeen), with both wins coming at Wolves (3-2 in March 2023, 3-1 in September 2025). Five of their last seven away wins in the competition have been against sides beginning with W, winning none of their last 22 against sides beginning with any other letter (drawn six, lost sixteen) since a 2-1 win at Liverpool in October 2022.

Chelsea manager Liam Rosenior failed to win either of his two previous meetings with Leeds, drawing 0-0 at home and losing 3-1 away in the 2023-24 Championship campaign. Those were also his only two previous meetings with Whites manager Daniel Farke Jaka Bijol, Ao Tanaka and Dominic Calvert-Lewin were all on target for Leeds in their 3-1 win over Chelsea in December. The last player to score home and away for the club against the Blues in the same campaign was Mark Viduka in 2000-01.

Leeds haven’t won any of their last seven midweek (Tues, Weds, Thurs) away games in the Premier League (drawn three, lost four), with the Whites conceding 24 goals in these matches (3 ½ goals a game). João Pedro has been involved in four goals in his last four home Premier League appearances for Chelsea (3 goals, 1 assist) but he has never scored or assisted against Leeds, losing all three of his top-flight games against the Whites (two with Watford, one with Chelsea).

Chelsea manager Liam Rosenior started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Robert Sanchez in goal, then a back four of Malo Gusto, Josh Acheampong, Trevor Chalobah and Marc Cucurella then two holding midfielders in Moises Caicedo and captain Enzo Fernandez then three attacking midfielders in Estevao, Cole Palmer and Andrey Santos with Joao Pedro up front.

Leeds United manager Daniel Farke started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Carl Darlow in goal, then a back three of James Justin, Joe Rodon, and Jaka Bijol then a four-man midfield of Jayden Bogle, captain Ethan Ampadu, Illa Gruev, and Gabriel Gudmundsson then two attacking midfielders in Brenden Aaronson and Nmecha and Sebastian Bornauw up top.

Leeds United kicked off the first half. Both sides started slowly but steadily with neither side making nor opening in the first quarter of an hour of the encounter on a damp night in SW6. Lukas Nmecha was yellow carded in the 20th minute fort a foul on Trevor Chalobah. Sebastiaan Bornauw was yellow carded in 23rd minute for foul on Cole Palmer which gave Chelsea a free kick in a dangerous position.

Enzo Fernandes took the free kick which was deflected off the Leeds defensive all and was saved by Karl Darlow. But Chelsea opened the scoring with their next attack as Joao Pedro chipped over the advancing Darlow to score with a left-footed chip into the net in the 24th minute from an assist by Cole Palmer. Cole Palmer came forward in the 27th minute on the counterattack but his left-footed shot from 22 yards was well saved by Darlow. In the 32nd minute Enzo Fernandes found Joao Pedro just inside the Leeds penalty area. And he turned acutely and shot right footed by his effort on target and was saved again by Darlow.

Chelsea came forward through Estevao in the 42nd minute but his right-footed shot went of the target. James Justin was yellow carded in 44th minute for foul on Fernandez. Chelsea led 1-0 at half time through the only goal from Joao Pedro. Chelsea kicked off the second half 1-0 up. Chelsea replaced Marc Cucurella with Jorrel Hato in the 46th minute. Leeds corner in 52nd minute cleared at near post by Malo Gusto. Leeds United replaced Sebastiaan Bornauw with Noah Okafor in the 53rd minute. Chelsea was awarded a penalty in the 58th minute after Jaka Bijol pushed down Joao Pedro and the referee Robert Jones awarded a penalty. Cole Palmer took the penalty and fired home to make it 2-0 against Karl Darlow in the 58th minute.

With Chelsea’s next attack in the 62nd minute Enzo Fernandez fired a right-footed shot wide of the target. Chelsea manager Liam Rosenior brought on Pedro for Estevao in the 63rd minute. Leeds United were awarded a penalty in the 66th minute after Moises Caicedo brought down James Justin. The spit kick was awarded, and Lukas Nmecha took it right footed and scored to make it 2-1 in the 67th minute.

Leeds United equalised in the 73rd minute through a right-foot shot from close range by Noah Okafor but it appeared that James Justin handled the ball in the buildup but after VAR the referee awarded the goal 2-2. Chelsea manager Liam Rosenior brought on Wesley Fofana for Josh Acheampong and Liam Delap for Andrey Santos in the 78th minute. Chelsea corner in 79th minute taken by Fernandez but cleared by Leeds.

Leeds United manager Daniel Farke brought on Daniel James for Lukas Nmecha in the 81st minute. Pedro Neto cut in from the right flank and curled a left-footed shot to the back post which went narrowly wide of the far post otherwise it would have been 3-2 but the teams must settle for a point apiece with a 2-2 result after Chelsea had been pegged back from 2-0 up to 2-2.
