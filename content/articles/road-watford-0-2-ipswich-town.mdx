## Match Report

IPSWICH TOWN moved closer to the top two in the SKYBET Championship with a deserved 2-0 victory against Watford at Vicarage Road last night in front of 18,249 fans thanks to goals in either half by Sindre Walle Egeli and George Hirst to seal the points. Watford hosted promotion hopefuls Ipswich Town at Vicarage Road last night in the SKYBET Championship. Watford came into the game off the back of one win against Derby County (home) 2-0 last time out, two draws on the road at Preston North End (away) 2-2, Hull City (away) 0-0 and two defeats against Southampton (away) 0-1 and Swansea City (home) 0-2. Ipswich Town came into the game in poor form with one win against Derby County (away) 2-1, one draw against Preston North End (home) 1-1 and three defeats against Wrexham (away) FA Cup 4 0-1, Wrexham (away) 3-5 last Saturday and Sheffield United (away) 1-3.

Watford were winless in their last five league games against Ipswich (drawn two, lost three), having only dropped points in four of their previous 17 against them (won thirteen, drawn three lost one). After losing six consecutive away league games against Watford between 2006 and 2012, Ipswich Town have now won three of their last four visits to Vicarage Road (losing just one game).

The reverse fixture between Watford and Ipswich Town this season finished 1-1 at Portman Road – only three times before have both league meetings between the sides ended level (1983-84, 1985-86 and 1990-91). Watford were unbeaten in their last 10 midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday) league games (won four, drawn six), though five of their last six have finished level.

Ipswich Town had lost just one of their last nine midweek (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday) league games (won five, drawn three), going down 3-0 at home in Suffolk to Charlton Athletic in October. Watford manager Ed Still started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Egil Selvik in goal, then a back four of James Abankwah, Jeremy Petrie, Mattie Pollock, and Stephen Mfuni then captain Imran Louza and Nampalys Mendy then three attacking midfielders in Nestory Irankunda, Giorgio Chakvetadze and Marc Bola then Kjerrumgaard up front.

Ipswich Town manager Kieran McKenna started with a 4-2-3-1 Christian Walton in goal, then a back four of Darnell Furlong, captain John O’Shea, Cedric Kipre and Jacob Greaves then a two-man midfield of Azor Matusiwa and Dan Neil then three attacking midfielders in Marcelino Nunez, Sindre Walle Egeli and Jack Clarke with Ivan Azon up front. Ipswich Town kicked off the first half and were backed by a sellout away following in the Vicarage Road stand. Ipswich Town started the better of the two teams but failed to capitalise on the play.

Egil Selvik was almost caught out by a through ball from Ivan Azon who rounded the goalkeeper and crossed into the six-yard box for a fellow striker, but Mattie Pollock cleared for the Hornets. Ipswich Town were running midfield and Watford were getting little of the ball midway through the first half and there was only one team in it. Ipswich had a free kick in a dangerous position in the 26th minute after James Abankwah had fouled Jack Clarke.

Jeremy Petrie came forward on the counterattack for Watford in the 33rd minute but his right-footed effort was hit high and wide of the goal guarded by Christian Walton. Ipswich were awarded another free kick in a dangerous position in the 36th minute when Stephen Mfuni fouled Azon. Ipswich Town free kick by Marcelino Nunez was deflected off rage wall into the path of Sindre Walle Egeli fired home with a left-footed shot into the far corner of the net to make it 0-1 in the 37th minute.

Ipswich Town led 1-0 at half time. Watford kicked off the second half 0-1 down. Watford replaced Jeremy Petris and Giorgi Chakvetadze with Jeremy Ngakia and Eduardo Bove making his debut for the Hornets. Watford tried hard to get back on level terms early in the second half, but Ipswich always did enough to combat the Hornets attacking ideas. Cedric Kipre was yellow carded for a foul on Nestory Irankunda in the 56th minute.

Watford went close through a close-range James Abankwah right-footed shot in the 58th minute but Walton saved, Watford replaced Luca Kjerrumgaard with Mamadou Doumbia in the 58th minute. Marcelino Nunez Field a right-footed shot wide of the target from 20 yards out in the 61st minute as the visitors looked to seal a second goal on the night. Watford had a couple of shots on goal from Imran Louza and Marc Bola, but both were blocked by Ipswich defenders in the 63rd and 64th minutes.

Ipswich Town replaced Sindre Walle Egeli and Dan Neil with Wes Burns and Jens Cajuste in the 66th minute. On the counterattack in the 70th minute Nunez fired a right-footed shot over the cross bar when clear on goal. Nestory Irankunda tormented Ipswich with some great wing play but the visitors from Suffolk were well organised. Watford replaced James Abankwah with Tom Ince in the 73rd minute.

Marcelino Nunez and Ivan Azon are replaced by Anis Mehmeti and George Hirst in the 74th minute for Ipswich Town. Watford went close with an Eduardo Bove right-footed shot which went wide of the far post from 18 yards out. Within three minutes of coming on George Hirst added a second goal to end Watford’s chances of taking anything from the game in the 77th minute. Ed Still the Watford boss replaced captain Imran Louza with Pierre Ekwah who was making his Hornets debut in what remained of the encounter.

Watford was awarded a penalty in the 83rd minute after Bove was bundled over by Daryl Furlong. Tom Ince took the penalty, but it was saved by Christian Walton down to his left to deny the Hornets a goal in the 83rd minute. Ipswich Town brought on ben Johnson for Jack Clarke in the 88th minute. Ipswich Town thorough deserved the 0-2 win.
