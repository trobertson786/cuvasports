## Match Report

MANCHESTER CITY beat Brentford 1-0 at the Gtech Community Stadium with the only goal coming from Erling Haaland with a left-footed shot from an assist by Josko Gvardiol in the 9th minute to seal the points in front of a crowd of 17, xxx.

Since completing a Premier League double over Manchester City in 2022-23, Brentford are winless in their last four against the Citizens in the division (drawn 1, lost 3). Manchester City have lost one of their four Premier League visits to Brentford (won 2, drawn 1), going down 1-0 on the final day of the 2022-23 season.

Brentford won 3-1 against Manchester United last time out. The only team to face both Manchester clubs in consecutive Premier League games and win them both are Tottenham Hotspur in January 1996 (4-1 v Man Utd, 1-0 v Man City). Brentford have won four of their last six Premier League home games (drawn 1, lost 1), scoring at least twice in five of those matches. This comes after an 8-game winless run at Gtech Community Stadium, a run in which they failed to score on five occasions.

Manchester City are the only side to have spent more than half of their game time in a winning position in the Premier League this season (55.3%). The Citizens have spent over two hours in the lead in their last two away league games but have failed to win either match (drawn 1, lost 1).

Manchester City (18.4%) and Brentford (17.7%) have the two highest conversion rates in the Premier League this season. Indeed, the Bees have had the joint-fewest shots so far this term (51) but rank joint-sixth for goals scored (9). Both Brentford and Manchester City lead the way in goals from fast breaks in the Premier League this season (3 each), with two of the Bees’ coming against Manchester United in their last match.

Pep Guardiola has won 249 of his 348 Premier League matches as Man City manager (drawn 50, lost 49). The Spaniard could become the 16th manager to win 250 top-flight games with a single club and would reach that total in only 349 games – 74 games quicker than anyone else has managed (Arsene Wenger at Arsenal, 423 games).

Brentford’s Igor Thiago has scored five goals from six shots on target in all competitions this season. Meanwhile, in the Premier League the Brazilian has the highest shot conversion rate of any player to have 10+ shots this term (40% - 4 goals from 10 shots).

Two of the three players to apply the most high-intensity pressures to opponents in the Premier League this season are Brentford’s Igor Thiago (354) and Yehor Yarmoliuk (340). Thiago ranks first for this metric in the opposition half (234), while Yarmoliuk is top in his own half of the pitch (244). Man, City’s Phil Foden has only scored more Premier League goals against Brighton (8) than he has against Brentford (7), with six of his seven goals against the Bees coming at the Gtech Community Stadium. Brentford manager Keith Andrews started with a 5-3-2 formation with Caoimhin Kelleher in goal, then a five of Michael Kayode, Kristoffer Ajer, Sep van den Berg, captain Nathan Collins, and Aaron Hickey then three holding midfielders in Yehor Yarmoliuk, Jordan Henderson and Mikkel Damsgaard with Kevin Schade and Igor Thiago up front.

Manchester City manager Pep Guardiola started with a 4-1-4-1 formation with Gianluigi Donnarumma in goal, then a back four of Matheus Nunes, captain Ruben Dias, Jason Gvardiol and Nico O’Reilly then Rodri then a four-man midfield of Savinho, Tijani Reijnders Phil Foden and Oscar Bobb with Erling Harland up front.

Brentford kicked off the first half attacking the goal where the Brentford home fans were seated in the shade. Manchester City came forward in the sixth minute through Tijani Reijnders with a great dribble but once in teh penalty area the ball was collected by Caoimhin Kelleher the Brentford goalkeeper before the Manchester City player could shoot.

Manchester City started well witch great possession and forced a corner in the 7th minute which saw Ruben Dias head on target, but the Brentford goalkeeper Kelleher saved easily. Manchester City opened the scoring through Erling Haaland with a left-footed shot through the Brentford defence to shoot on target with determination and brute force resulted in his shot ending up in the back of the Bees net to make it 1-0 in the 9th minute with the assist for the goal coming from Josko Gvardiol after Sep van den Berg was easily bullied.

Brentford corner in the 18th minute came to nothing as Manchester City’s O’Reilly cleared the ball. Manchester City replaced Rodri who pulled up, and City replaced him with Nico Gonzalez in the 20th minute. Oscar Bobb crossed for Phil Foden in the 31st minute beating Aaron Hickey but the final finish was a let down by Foden. It should have been 0-2. Manchester City went close through a Reijnders right-footed volley from 15 yards out which was expertly saved by Kelleher in the 39th minute.

Manchester City led 1-0 at half time and thoroughly deserved the lead. Manchester City kicked off the second half 1-0 up. Neither team made any substitutions at the interval. Brentford came forward through Kevin Schade in the 58th minute and he crossed to the back post where Michael Kayode headed just over the cross bar. Brentford pressed for an equaliser in the 61st minute from a long through by Kayode but City cleared the ball out for a corner and then took care of the resultant corner as Gvardiol cleared.

Brentford replaced Igor Thiago with Dango Ouattara and Mikkel Damsgaard for Mathias Jensen in the 68th minute. In the 74th minute Brentford brought on Frank Onyeka and Vitaly Janet for Jordan Henderson and Yehor Yarmoliuk. Manchester City brought on Jeremy Doku and Bernardo Silva in the 76th minute for Oscar Bobb and Tijani Reijnders.

Manchester City despite a poor second half as Brentford improved held on for the 3 points with a narrow 1-0 win for the Citizens.
