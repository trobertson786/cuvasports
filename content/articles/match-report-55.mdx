## Match Report

CHELSEA came back to beat West Ham United late on 3-2 in the London derby at Stamford Bridge. West Ham United went up 2-0 through goals scored through Jarrod Bowen left-footed long range shot from an assist by Aaron Wan-Bissaka in the 7th minute and Crysencio Summerville with a right-footed shot from an assist by Aaron Wan-Bissaka in the 36th minute.

Chelsea then pulled back to win through goals by Joao Pedro with back post header from centre by Wesley Fofana in the 57th minute to make it 1-2. Chelsea equalised in the 69th minute through a Marc Cucurella header to make it 2-2 after heavy Chelsea pressure as the Hammers defence cracked and Enzo Fernandez fired home in 90+2 minutes from an assist by Joao Pedro to seal the win.

Chelsea came into the London derby match against West Ham United off the back of four wins for Liam Rosenior the new coach against Napoli 3-2 (away) Champions League, Crystal Palace (away) 3-1, Pafos 1-0 (home) Champions League and Brentford (home) 2-0 and a single defeat against Arsenal (home) 2-3 EFL Cup Semi Final 1st Leg.

It’s their longest winning run against the Hammers since a run of six between 2006 and 2008.
