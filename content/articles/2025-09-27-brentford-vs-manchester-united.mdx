## Match Report

BRENTFORD thoroughly deserved a 3-1 home win over Manchester United at the Gtech Community Stadium yesterday lunchtime thanks to goals by Igor Thiago with a brace one assist by Jordan Henderson and Mathias Jensen shot in added time assist by Yehor Yarmoliuk. Manchester United was out play but scored a first half goal after heavy pressure through Benjamin Sesko with his first United goal, but the Bees deserved three points.

Brentford came into the game off the back of a 3-1 loss to Fulham last time out in a West London derby, two home draws 1-1 versus Aston Villa (won on penalties 4-2 in the Carabao Cup) and 2-2 versus Chelsea and a 2-1 away loss at Sunderland and a 2-0 away Carabao Cup win at AFC Bournemouth.

Manchester United came into the match off the back of home wins 2-1 against Chelsea and 3-2 against Burnley and a Manchester derby 3-0 away loss against rivals City and 2-2 draw with Grimsby Town (Carabao Cup) lost on penalties and a 1-1 away draw at Fulham.

Brentford have won two of their last three home Premier League games against Manchester United (D1), as many as their first six home league matches versus the Red Devils beforehand (W2 D1 L3). After their 4-3 defeat in May, Manchester United lost back-to-back league meetings with Brentford for only the third time, after previously doing so in the second tier in December 1934 and in February 1937.

Since Manchester United won 3-1 away at Brentford in January 2022, the home team has won six of the last seven Premier League meetings between Brentford and Man Utd (D1). Between February 2020 and January 2022, Manchester United went 13 Premier League games without defeat in London (W8 D5). Since then, they’ve lost 15 of 24 visits to the capital (W3 D6).

Manchester United is winless in their last seven Premier League matches (D2 L5) since a 3-0 win at Leicester in March. The Red Devils last had a longer run without an away win between March and October 2019 (eight games). Brentford conceded 10 goals in their five Premier League games this season, the most at this stage of a league season since 2003-04 in the third tier (also 10). Only Burnley (95) faced more shots in 2025-26 than the Bees (70).

Manchester United have had the most shots in the Premier League this season (81), while Brentford have had the fewest (41). The Red Devils are averaging 16.2 shots in 2025-26, up from 13.9 under Ruben Amorim in 2024-25, while their xG per game is 2.03 compared to 1.33 last season under Amorim. Brentford has dropped eight points from winning positions in the Premier League so far this season, scoring the first goal in four games but winning only one (D1 L2). Only Newcastle in 1999-00 (11) and Fulham in 2007-08 (9) have ever dropped more points when winning in the first five games of a season.

Only Erling Haaland (21) has had more shots in the Premier League this season than Man Utd’s Bryan Mbeumo (15), with that the most shots by a player in their first five league games for the club since Antony in 2022 (18). Manchester United’s Bruno Fernandes has made the most line-breaking passes into the opposition penalty area in the Premier League this season (8), while the Red Devils captain also ranks first for line-breaking passes that have led to a shot (8).

Brentford manager Keith Andrews started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Caoimhin Kelleher in goal, then a four of Michael Kayode, Sep van den Berg, captain Nathan Collins, and Aaron Hickey then two holding midfielders in Yehor Yarmoliuk and Jordan Henderson then three attacking midfielders in Dango Ouattara, Mikkel Damsgaard and Kevin Schade with Igor Thiago up front.

Manchester United manager Ruben Amarion started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Altay Bayindir in goal, then a back three of Matthias de Ligt, Harry Maguire, and Luke Shaw then a four-man midfield of Manuel Ugarte Patrick Dorgu, captain Bruno Fernandes, and Diogo Dalot then Bryan Mbeumo and Matheus Cunha with Benjamin Sesko up front.

Manchester United kicked off the first half and started on the back foot as Brentford took the early play to Manchester United and in the 8th minute Brentford took the lead in style through a left-footed shot from Igor Thiago from an assist by Jordan Henderson to give the Bees a solid 1-0 lead. Nathan Collins went close to adding further goals with two great headers in the 16th and 17th minute, which were both tipped away by excellent saves from Altay Bayindir. Manchester United could easily be 3-0 down after 18 minutes but for the goalkeeper.

Brentford went 2-0 up in the 20th minute through a close range shot from Igor Thiago after a great run and cross into the penalty area which was carried away by Bayindir into the path of the big Brazilian striker. Benjamin Sesko pulled a goal back for United in the 25th minute to make it 2-1 after a VAR decision. Matheus Cunha came forward for United in the 37th minute and saw his right-footed shot deflected off Collins for a corner. Brentford led 2-1 at half time. Brentford kicked off the second half and controlled the second period by scoring a third in 90th+5-minute added time through substitute Matthias Jensen to seal a deserved 3-1 win.
