## Match Report

SWINDON TOWN manager Ian Holloway saw his side come from behind to beat Luton Town 2-1 at Kenilworth Road last night in the EFL trophy thanks to goals from Will Wright and Filozofe Mabete in the 37th and 53rd minutes after Jerry Yates had given Luton Town an early lead with a 7th minute penalty after Emilo Lawrence had drawn a foul. Luton Town took the lead through a weak penalty slotted home by Jerry Yates luckily under goalkeeper Lewis Ward who dived the right way in the 7th minute. Swindon Town equalised in the 37th minute through Will Wright with a 25-yard shot from outside the penalty area from a short corner by Michael Olakigbe. Swindon Town took the lead with a back post right-footed close range shot after goalkeeper James Shea had only parried a shot from Aidan Borland into the path of the Robins defender who made it 1-2 in the 53rd minute.

Luton Town who hosted Ian Holloway’s Swindon Town in the EFL Trophy 3rd round tie at Kenilworth Road last night were dumped out by lower league opponents. Luton Town came into the game off the back of three wins against Stevenage (home) 2-1, Leyton Orient (home) 3-0 and Wycombe Wanderers (home) 4-0 and two defeats against Exeter City (away) 0-1 and Reading (away) 2-3. Swindon Town came into the game with four wins against Gillingham (home) 2-0, Cheltenham Town (away) 2-0, Crawley Town (home) 1-0 and Bristol Rovers (away) 3-0 and one defeat at Milton Keynes Dons (away) 0-1. Their last match at Salford City in FA Cup 3rd round was postponed the winners when played will travel to Manchester City in round.

Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere started with a 4-1-4-1 formation with captain James Shea in goal then a back four of Reuell Walters, Harry Fox, Christ Makosso, Nigel Lonwijk and Harry Fox then George Saville then four midfielders Shayden Morris, Lamine Fanne, Zack Nelson, and Emilo Lawrence with Jerry Yates up front.

Swindon Town manager the masterful Ian Holloway with over 1000 games at the helm started with a 3-4-1-2 formation with Lewis Ward in goal, then a back three of Will Wright, James Ball and Filozofe Mabete then a four man midfield of Joe Snowdon, captain Ollie Clarke, Gavin Kilkenny and Tom Wilson-Brown and Aidan Borland in the pocket with Michael Olakigbe and Ollie Palmer up front.

Luton Town kicked off the first half attacking the empty Kenilworth Road end of the ground and the home side started well early in the game. Luton Town took the lead through a weak penalty slotted home by Jerry Yates luckily under goalkeeper Lewis Ward who dived the right way in the 7th minute. Swindon Town forced a corner in the 21st minute taken by Will Wright but cleared the danger quickly and started a quick counterattack of their own until Filozofe Mabete brought down Emilo Lawrence and was shown a yellow card.

In the 25th minute Shayden Morris saw a left-footed shot from 20 yards out tipped over the cross bar for a corner by Ward. Harry Fox saw a left-footed shot from distance take a deflection for a corner in the 36th minute. Swindon Town equalised in the 37th minute through Will Wright with a 25-yard shot from outside the penalty area from a short corner by Michael Olakigbe. The teams went in level at 1-1 at half time. Swindon Town kicked off the second half with score level at 1-1.

Swindon Town replaced Ollie Palmer, Tom Wilson-Brown and Joe Snowdon with Aidan Drinan, Billy Kirkham, and Joel McGregor in the 46th minute. Luton Town started the second half well and in the 47th minute Lamine Fanne saw a right-footed shot blocked by James Ball. Swindon Town took the lead with a back post right-footed close range shot after goalkeeper James Shea had only parried a shot from Aidan Borland into the path of the Robins defender who made it 1-2 in the 53rd minute.

Emilo Lawrence saw alert-footed shot saved by Lewis’s ward in the 57th down to his left-hand post. Joel MacGregor was yell carded in 60th minute for a foul on Morris. Luton had a free kick in a dangerous position at the Oak Road end in front of the travelling Swindon fans. George Saville took the free kick left footed which went narrowly wide of the far post, but Ward had it covered in the 61st minute. Swindon Ollie Clarke right-footed shot just over the Luton cross bar from 25 yards out in the 62nd minute. Delay in match 64th to 71st minute.

Swindon’s Aaron Drinan tested James Shea with a vicious shot 62 minutes, and his appearance was made known to the referee with the Robins striker not listed on the official team sheet despite him being brought on as a half-time substitute. After discussions with both managers and captains, play continued. Luton Town manager Jack Wilshere brought on Marvelous Nakamba the Zimbabwean international and Ethon Archer for Emilio Lawrence and Lamine Fanne in the 71st minute after a six-minute stoppage for racist chanting by Swindon fans of the Luton players.
