## Match Report

MIDDLESBROUGH beat QPR yesterday at the Matrade Loftus Rod Stadium 4-0 in front of a crowd of 15,940 thanks to goals by David Strelec close range tap in, Alan Browne with a header and super strike from Hayden Hackney right-footed curler and a penalty converted by Tommy Conway from the penalty spot to improve the Riverside Club’s automatic promotion push to the Premier league come May.

QPR hosted promotion pushing Middlesbrough at the Matrade Loftus Road Stadium yesterday at teatime in the SKYBET Championship. QPR came into the game off the back of one win against Hull City (away) 3-1, one draw against Charlton Athletic (away) 0-0 and three defeats against Blackburn Rovers (home) 1-3, Southampton (away) 0-5 and Sheffield United (home) 0-2 last time out. Middlesbrough came into the game off the back of two wins against Sheffield United (away) 2-1 and Birmingham City (away) 3-1 last time out, two draws against Oxford United (home) 0-0 and Leicester City (home) 1-1 and a defeat against Coventry City (away) 1-3.

QPR had lost their last four league games against Middlesbrough but have never lost five in a row against Boro previously. Middlesbrough have won their last two league visits to Queens Park Rangers, having won just three of their first 26 visits beforehand between 1966 and 2022. Since Christmas, only Sheffield Wednesday (nil), West Brom (one) and Leicester (two) have won fewer Championship matches than QPR (three), who’ve lost their last two by an aggregate score of 7-0.

Middlesbrough have won nine away league games this season are looking to reach double figures for away wins in a season for the ninth time – five of the previous eight have come this century (2011-12, 2014-15, 2015-16, 2018-19, 2023-24). Middlesbrough defender Matt Targett scored twice in the first half of their 3-1 win over Birmingham on Monday, scoring as many times in that game as he had done in his previous 74 league games combined.

Queens Park Rangers manager Julien Stephan started with a 4-5-1 formation with Walsh in goal then a back four of Amadou Mbengue, Ronnie Edwards, captain Jimmy Dunne and Rhys Norrington-Davies then a five-man midfield of Harvey Vale, Kieran Morgan, Isaac Hayden, Jonathan Varane and Koki Saito with Richard Kone up front.

Middlesbrough manager Kim Hellberg started with a 3-4-1-2 formation Sol Brynn in goal then a back three of Luke Ayling, captain Dael Fry and Alan Browne then four midfielders in Callum Brittain, Aidan Morris, Hayden Hackney and Matt Targett then Riley McGree then two up top in David Strelec and Morgan Whittaker.

Middlesbrough kicked off the first half.  QPR started well but never really opened Middlesbrough up and then the visitors opened the scoring in the 20th minute through a simple tap in from David Strelec after his initial shot by Morgan Whittaker was blocked by Rhys Norrington-Davies-Davies the loose ball fell to Whittaker and he converted from close range at the second attempt.

QPR came back well and Richard Kone saw a header saved well by Sol Brynn in the 24th minute. Dael Fry was substituted in the 31st minute by Adilson Malanda. Middlesbrough went close to a second through Hayden Hackney in the 32nd minute. Middlesbrough led 0-1 at half time.

QPR kicked off the second half. QPR started the second half well and went in search of an equaliser but the Middlesbrough defence stood firm. Middlesbrough went close to a second goal when David Strelec saw a left-footed shot from close range saved by Walsh in the 54th minute. Middlesbrough replaced Morgan Whittaker and David Strelec for Jeremy Sarmiento and Tommy Conway in the 66th minute. Middlesbrough went 2-0 up at Alan Browne headed in from close range after the initial header from Tommy Conway had hit the cross bar and bounced back into the path of Browne who made no mistake in the 68th minute.

QPR replaced Kiernan Morgan with Paul Smyth in the 71st minute. Hayden Hackney made it 3-0 in the 72nd minute with a lovely right-footed curling effort to seal the victory in style in front of the travelling ‘Boro fans. QPR brought on Jake Clarke-Salter and Daniel Bennie for Koki Saito and Jonathan Varane in the 80th minute. Middlesbrough replaced Riley McGree and Hayden Hackney with Leo Castledine and Alex Gilbert in the 82nd minute.

QPR replaced Amadou Mbengue with Tylon Smith and Rhys Norrington-Davies-Davies with Esquerdinha in the 85th minute. Tommy Conway fired a penalty home in the 88th minute to complete the rout for ‘Boro after Tylon Smith had fouled. Middlesbrough deserved the 4-0 win, which was comfortable as they went closer to top placed Coventry City. The Loftus Road end was empty by the end of the game.
