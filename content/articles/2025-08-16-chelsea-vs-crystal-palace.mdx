## First 45 minutes

CHELSEA had to settle for a goalless draw against an inform Crystal Palace in the Premier league pipe opener in London SW6 yesterday as fans from both sides basked in warm August sunshine in the 39,867 crowd. Chelsea the World Champions after beating PSG in the final in USA during the summer hosted South London’s loud and proud Crystal Palace fresh from winning the FA Cup last season against Manchester City and beating Liverpool in last weekend’s Community Shield at Wembley to achieve two trophies in in three calendar months after nothing for over 100 years history of the Glaziers and then Eagles.

Chelsea was unbeaten in their last fifteen Premier League games against Crystal Palace having won thirteen and drawn two, their longest active run against any of the 19 other sides involved in the 2025-26 season. Crystal Palace’s last victory at Stamford Bridge in the Premier League came in April 2017 under Sam Allardyce 2-1 win – since then, they’ve failed to win on any of their last eight trips in league action, losing seven of those matches and drawing just one.

This will be just the second time Chelsea and Crystal Palace have faced each other on the opening day of a league season, with the Blues having won 3-0 at Stamford Bridge on the opening day of the 2021-22 campaign. Chelsea came into the match from friendly 4-1 and 2-0 wins over AC Milan and Bayer Leverkusen a 3-0 win over PSG and 2-0 and 2-1 wins over Fluminense and Palmeiras. Crystal Palace beat Liverpool last week 2-2 and 3-2 on penalties, lost 1-0 and beat 3-1 Augsburg, plus Crawley Town 3-1 win and Millwall 1-0 win.

Chelsea manager Enzo Maresca started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Robert Sanchez in goal then back four of captain Reece James, Trevor Chalobah, Josh Acheampong and Marc Cucurella then two holding midfielders in Enzo Fernandez and Moises Caicedo then three attacking midfielders in Jamie Gittins, Cole Palmer, and Pedro Neto with Joao Pedro up front. Crystal Palace manager Oliver Glasner started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Dean Henderson in goal then a back three of Chris Richards, Maxence Lacroix and captain Marc Guehi then a four-man midfield of Daniel Munoz, Adam Wharton, Will Hughes and Tyrick Mitchell then two attacking midfielders in Ismaila Sarr and Eberechi Eze with Jean Phillips Mateta upfront.

Crystal Palace included Eberechi Eze who was subject to transfer to Arsenal or north London archrivals Tottenham Hotspur for circa £80m plus fee. Chelsea kicked off the first half after both teams stood for minutes silence is especially of Diego Jota and his brother who passed away in a car crash in the summer. Chelsea went close to opening the scoring in the third minute for a near post backward header by Josh Acheampong which was headed off the line at the back post by Ismaila Sarr.

Crystal Palace was awarded free kick in a dangerous position in 12th minute and Eberechi Eze fired home right footed to make it 0-1 with a pile driver in the 13th minute. His last goal for the Eagles. Reece James took the corner to the near post aiming for Josh Acheampong but Crystal. Palace captain Marc Guehi cleared the danger.

In the 27th minute Daniel Munoz was yellow carded for a heavy challenge on Marc Cucurella. Then in the 33rd minute Jean Phillipe Mateta was yellow carded for a heavy challenge on Josh Acheampong.
