## Match Report

WEST HAM UNITED went through three unbeaten games after a 3-1 home win over Sunderland at the London Stadium yesterday lunchtime. West Ham United welcomed back African Nations winner with Senegal El Hadji Malik Diouf as he started on the bench but was given a rapturous return.

West Ham United opened the scoring in the 14th minute through Crysencio Summerville with a close-range header beyond the diving Robin Roefs from a lovely right wing centre cross by captain Jarrod Bowen for the assist to make it 1-0 to the home side.

West Ham United were awarded a penalty after Ollie Scarles was taken down by Trai Hume and referee Thomas Bramall pointed to the spot. Jarrod Bowen took the penalty to make it 2-0 and sent the Hammers on their way to a delightful home win in the 28th minute.

West Ham United were not finished as Matheus Fernandez fired home from 22 yards out to make it 3-0 as the Hammers ended the first half in style. Sunderland improved in the second half and Brian Brobbey headed home from a consolation from an assist by Nordi Mukiele in the 66th to leave it at 3-1.

West Ham United came into the game off the back of two wins against Tottenham Hotspur (away) 2-1 and Queens Park Rangers FA Cup 3 (home) 2-1, a draw against Brighton & Hove Albion (home) 2-2 and two defeats against Nottingham Forest (home) 1-2 and Wolverhampton Wanderers (away) 0-3.

Sunderland arrived at the London Stadium off the back of one win against Crystal Palace (home) 2-1, three draws against Everton FA Cup 3 (away) 1-1 (won on penalties), Tottenham Hotspur (away) 1-1 and Manchester City (home) 0-0.

West Ham United had lost just one of their last 12 Premier League home games against Sunderland (won nine, drawn two), going down 3-0 in May 2011. They’ve won their last three all by the same 1-0 scoreline. Sunderland was looking to complete the Premier League double over West Ham United for just the second time, previously doing so in 2010-11 when the Hammers were relegated. West Ham United had lost just one of their last 21 Premier League home games against promoted sides (won sixteen, drawn four) and are unbeaten in 12 since a 2-1 loss to Brentford in October 2021.

West Ham United ended their ten-game winless run by beating Tottenham Hotspur 2-1 last time out. However, the Hammers hadn’t kept a clean sheet in any of their last 19 Premier League games, only once having a longer run (twenty-four between March and November 2008).

Sunderland was looking to win consecutive Premier League games for just the second time this season, previously beating Wolves and Chelsea in October. However, they’re winless in six away from home (drawn three, lost three) since that 2-1 win over the Blues.

Sunderland had won sixteen points from losing positions in the Premier League this season, with only Aston Villa gaining more (18). It’s the Black Cats’ most in a single campaign in the competition, and most by a promoted side since West Brom in 2010-11 (twenty-seven).

West Ham United had conceded a league-high twelve headed goals in the Premier League this season. Only in 2010-11 have they shipped more (fifteen), which was the last campaign they were relegated to.

West Ham United had not kept a clean sheet in any of their 17 Premier League games under Nuno Espírito Santo. Only three managers have gone longer from the start of their career with a club before – Brian Laws (18 with Burnley in 2009-10), Paul Lambert (21 with Norwich in 2011-12) and Ruud van Nistelrooy (21 with Leicester last season).

After a run of 28 games without a goal since October 2024, Crysencio Summerville has now scored in his last two appearances for West Ham in all competitions. He last scored three in a row in February 2024 while in the Championship with Leeds.

Sunderland’s Granit Xhaka has made more line breaking passes that have led to a goal than any other player in the Premier League this season (eight). Four of these have been direct assists by the Swiss, another league high.
