## The Story of the Match

TOTTENHAM HOTSPUR were dealt a late blow which left them deep in relegation trouble after Brighton & Hove Albion equalised in the fifth minute of added time to secure a 2-2 draw in front of 61,167 fans at a disgruntled Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.

Brighton & Hove Albion looked like losing at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium on Saturday teatime in one of the most important matches in Tottenham Hotspur's history as they tried to salvage their Premier League status. Spurs started the game in the relegation zone, two points behind West Ham United who play Crystal Palace away on Monday night, but three points turned to only one by the end of the night. The distraught Spurs fans trudged away from the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium disappointed once again. This time under the third manager this season in Robert De Zerbi, nothing had changed for the supporters as they remarked "same old, same old".

Tottenham Hotspur were winless in their last three Premier League games against Brighton & Hove Albion, having drawn one and lost two, having previously never gone more than once without a win against the Seagulls in the top flight.

Tottenham Hotspur were the only Premier League side without a win in 2026, winning none of 14 games (drawn five, lost nine). It was their second-longest winless league run in the club's illustrious history, only having a longer stretch without a win between December 1934 and April 1935, which stretched to sixteen matches.

Tottenham Hotspur had won just one of their last 26 Premier League matches played across January, February, March, April and May (drawn seven, lost eighteen), and were winless in their last 21 games in those months since a 3-1 win over Southampton last April.

This was ex-Brighton & Hove Albion manager Roberto De Zerbi's first home Premier League game in charge of Tottenham Hotspur. Only one of the previous thirteen managers to take charge of Spurs had lost their first home game, though it was his predecessor Igor Tudor as recently as February 2026 against arch North London rivals Arsenal.

Tottenham Hotspur had lost their last four home games, only losing five in a row once in their league history in February 1994. Since his first start of his second spell at Brighton & Hove Albion on 7th January, only three players had created more chances in the Premier League than Pascal Gross (twenty-four). He also ranked second for passes into the box (one hundred and eight) behind Bruno Fernandes (one hundred and thirty-nine), and the German topped the charts for successful crosses (twenty-eight).

Roberto De Zerbi's first home Premier League game in charge of Brighton & Hove Albion was, amazingly, against Tottenham Hotspur in October 2022, a 1-0 defeat. It is the first time in Premier League history that a manager's first home game in charge of two clubs has been the same fixture.

Brighton & Hove Albion had won two of their last four away league games against Tottenham Hotspur (lost two), including a 4-1 victory last season. They had only won one of their first nine visits between 1977 and 2020 (drawn two, lost six). Brighton & Hove Albion had scored fourteen goals in their last five Premier League games against Tottenham Hotspur, having scored just ten goals in their first twelve meetings in the league.

Brighton & Hove Albion had won five of their last six Premier League matches (lost one against league leaders Arsenal), as many wins as they had in their previous twenty matches combined. Since playing their 27th Premier League match, Brighton had won more points than any other team (fifteen). The Seagulls had also won their last three away Premier League games and had never won four in a row in the top flight. They had kept a clean sheet in all three of those wins.

Tottenham Hotspur came into the match off the back of a single win against Atletico Madrid in the Champions League Last 16 second leg at home (3-2), a draw against Liverpool away (1-1), and three defeats against Atletico Madrid in the Champions League Last 16 first leg away (2-5), Nottingham Forest at home (0-3), and Sunderland away (0-1) last time out.

Brighton & Hove Albion came into the match off the back of four wins against Nottingham Forest at home (2-1), Sunderland away (1-0), Liverpool at home (2-1) and Burnley away (2-0), and one defeat against Arsenal at home (0-1).

Tottenham Hotspur manager Roberto De Zerbi, in only his second match in charge, started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Guglielmo Vicario in goal. The back four consisted of Pedro Porro, Kevin Danso, captain Micky van den Ven and Destiny Udogie. Three midfielders in Conor Gallagher, Rodrigo Bentancur and Yves Bissouma supported three attacking players in Kolo Muani, Dominic Solanke and Xavi Simons.

Brighton & Hove Albion manager Fabien Hirtzler started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Bart Verbruggen in goal, a back four of Mats Wieffer, Jan Paul van Hecke, Oliver Boscagli and Ferdi Kadioglu, then two holding midfielders in Yasmin Ayari and Pascal Gross, three attacking midfielders in Diego Gomez, Jack Hinshelwood and Yankuba Minteh, and a rejuvenated Danny Welbeck up top.

Tottenham Hotspur set themselves on their way when Pedro Porro headed home from the centre of the penalty area from an assist by Xavi Simons in the 39th minute to make it 1-0.

Brighton & Hove Albion equalised late in the first half through Kaoru Mitoma, who curled a left-footed shot from the left side of the penalty area into the top left corner from an assist by Pascal Gross in the 45th-plus-third minute to make it 1-1. That was the score at the break.

Tottenham Hotspur retook the lead through Xavi Simons, who struck a right-footed shot from outside the penalty area into the top right corner from an assist by substitute Lucas Bergvall in the 77th minute to make it 2-1. The Spurs fans felt they were going to win and move above West Ham United in the league table, holding on for three precious points.

But it was not to be. Up stepped Georginio Rutter to fire home a left-footed shot from the centre of the penalty area from an assist by Jan Paul van Hecke in the 90th-plus-fifth minute to break Spurs hearts and make it 2-2. That was how it finished.

Tottenham Hotspur are still deep in relegation trouble. The "project" at the football club appears cursed, witnessed by a crowd of 61,167 on Saturday. Spurs' Premier League status is teetering on the edge. They stand to lose over 400 million pounds if they go down to the SkyBet Championship. The club appears so badly run behind the scenes. They employ over 900 full-time staff, the most of any club in Europe, and the current setup is run by non-football people at the top. Relegation would be the biggest disaster to come unless they change course very quickly.
