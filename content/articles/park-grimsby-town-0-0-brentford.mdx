## Carabao Cup 4th Round

Grimsby Town (0) 0

**Brentford (3) 5 Jensen 22, Lewis-Potter 26, Nelson 43, Fabio Carvalho (pen) 54, Collins 75**

---

Brentford cruised into the next round of the Carabao Cup by the banks of River Humber at Cleethorpes last night thanks to a 5-0 with goals by Mathias Jensen, Keane Lewis-Potter, Reiss Nelson, Fabio Carvalho (penalty), and Nathan Collins as Keith Andrews side recorded four wins in the last five matches.

The Bees started slowly, yet methodically against the League 2 side who took the early play to the Premier League side. Brentford appeared far better organised than the Manchester United side who were beaten by Grimsby Town on spot kicks 12-11 at the same venue in the Carabao Cup 2nd round tie back in August when Dave Artell the Mariners boss got the better of Ruben Amorim’s Red Devils.

Grimsby Town came into the game off the back of two wins against Gillingham 1-0 and Salford City 2-0 and three losses Crewe Alexandra 2-3, Colchester United 1-2 and Doncaster Rovers 0-3. Meanwhile Brentford came into the game off the back of three wins Liverpool 3-2, West Ham United 2-0, Manchester United 3-1 and two losses Manchester City 0-1 and Fulham 1-3.

The Mariners had beaten Shrewsbury Town, Manchester United and Sheffield Wednesday enroute to last night’s encounter with Brentford who themselves had beaten AFC Bournemouth and Aston Villa to reach the 4th Round themselves.

Bees made nine changes from the starting line-up against Liverpool on Saturday evening with only van den Berg and Ouattara remaining. Mariners made three changes from the last game with Crewe Alexandra with Warren, Amaluzor and Walker dropping out for Lavelle, Burns and Khouri who came into the starting XI.

Brentford oozed class in this game after early Mariners pressure and the Bees opened the scoring through captain Mathias Jensen who scored with a right-footed shot from outside the penalty area into the bottom left corner of the net from an assist by the lively Keane Lewis-Potter to make it 1-0.

Then four minutes later Keane Lewis-Potter turned goal scorer with a header from inside the penalty area into the bottom right corner of the Mariners net guarded by goalkeeper Christy Pym to double the lead from an assist by Reiss Nelson to make it 2-0.

Two minutes from the half time interval the Bees added a third goal through a Reiss Nelson right-footed shot from just outside the Grimsby Town penalty area into the bottom right corner of the net to make it 3-0 from an assist by Fabio Carvalho.

Grimsby Town had a couple of half chances in the first half which were saved by goalkeeper Hakon Valdimarsson from attempts by Charles Vernam with a shot and Evan Khouri with a header. In the second half the travelling Bees fans witnessed two further second half goals.

Brentford were not finished and nine minutes into the second half scored again through Fabio Carvalho who was fouled and the midfielder picked himself up to strike home the dead ball from the penalty spot beyond the diving Pym to make it 4-0.

Bees substitute Nathan Collins scored the final goal on the night to complete a resounding win with a near post header from an assist by his captain on the night Mathias Jensen to head beyond the diving Pym again to make it 5-0.

It was a very comprehensive professional performance from the Bees side who travelled back to West London with a place in the Quarter Finals of the Carabao Cup competition to look forward to for boss Keith Andrews in his first season at the helm.
