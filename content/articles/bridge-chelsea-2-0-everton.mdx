## Match Report

CHELSEA returned to winning ways in the Premier league with workmanlike 1-0 win over an improving Everton at Stamford Bridge yesterday afternoon in London SW6 in front of 39,552 fans thanks to goals from Cole Palmer with a left footed shot from close range to beat Jordan Pickford in the 21st minute from an assist by Malo Gusto and then Malo Gusto fired home a second in the 45th minute from an assist by Pedro Neto to seal the win.

Chelsea hosted Everton at Stamford Bridge yesterday afternoon in the Premier League. Chelsea sorted day in 5th spot with Everton in 8th spot. Chelsea came into the game with one win against Barcelona (home) 3-0, two draws against AFC Bournemouth (away) 0-0, and Arsenal (home) 1-1 and two losses against Atalanta (away) 1-2 and Leeds United (away) 1-3 in the last five matches.

Everton came into the game in better form with four wins against Nottingham Forest (home) 3-0, AFC Bournemouth (away) 1-0, Manchester United (away) 1-0 and Fulham (home) 2-0 with just one defeat against Newcastle United (home) 1-4 in the last five matches.

Chelsea last three matches against Everton were 1-0 win (home), 0-0 draw (away) and 6-0 win (home). Chelsea was unbeaten in their last 30 Premier League home games against Everton (won seventeen, drawn thirteen). It’s their longest unbeaten home run against an opponent in their league history, while only at Leeds United have Everton had a longer run without a win (thirty-six from 1953 to 2001).

Everton had failed to score in 27 different Premier League games against Chelsea, including each of the last three in a row. Only against Manchester United and Liverpool (twenty-eight each) have they drawn a blank more often. Chelsea was winless in their last three Premier League matches (drawn two, lost one), having won five of their previous six games (lost one). The Blues’ only longer winless run under Enzo Maresca came in December and January last season (five games).

Everton had won four of their last five Premier League matches, keeping a clean sheet in each victory. Since David Moyes’ first game back in charge on 15 January this year, only Man City (thirteen), Arsenal (thirteen) and Chelsea (eleven) have more Premier League victories without conceding than the Toffees (ten). Chelsea have made 46 line-up changes in the Premier League this season, 11 more than they’d made after 15 games in 2024-25 (thirty-five). They’ve made 4+ changes in each of their last four games, last doing so in more in a row between September and December 2022 (ten games).

Everton had won their last two Premier League matches, last having a longer away winning run between October and December in 2023 (four). Their last two away wins have both been 1-0 – the Toffees haven’t won three on the trot without conceding since December 2008. Chelsea had dropped more points from winning positions in Premier League home games than any other side this season (8), drawing 1-1 with Arsenal having scored first last time out. It’s already one more point than they dropped at home having led from the whole of last season (seven).

David Moyes is winless in all 20 of his Premier League, away games against Chelsea (drawn seven, lost thirteen), only facing Liverpool on the road more often without a win (twenty-one). However, the Scotsman did end Everton’s only other 30+ game winless away run against an opponent, beating Leeds 1-0 in November 2002. Thierno Barry ended a run of 14 consecutive Premier League appearances without a goal, scoring his first for Everton in the 3-0 win over Nottingham Forest. Two ‘Barrys’ have scored in consecutive Premier League appearances – Barry Hayles in August 2003 and Gareth Barry (seven times). Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall is Everton's joint leading Premier League scorer this season with four goals – he has netted three goals in his last four games, as many as he had scored in his previous sixty-two combined for Leicester, Chelsea, and the Toffees.

Chelsea manager Enzo Maresa started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Robert Sanchez in goal, then the back four of Malo Gusto, Wesley Forana, Trevoh Chalobah and Marc Cucurella, two holding midfielders in Reece James the captain and Enzo Ferandez then three attacking midfielders in Pedro Neto, Cole Palmer, and Alejandro Garnacho with Joao Pedro up front.

Everton manager David Moyes started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Jordan Pickford in goal, then a back four of Jake O’Brien, captain James Tarkowski, Michael Keane, and Vitali Mykolenko then two holding midfielders in James Garner and Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall then three attacking midfielders in Iliman Ndiaye, Idriss Gueye and Jack Grealish with Thierno Barry up front.

Everton kicked off the first half wearing changed strip of yellow shirts with navy blue shorts and navy-blue socks. Chelsea was in their traditional blue and white with red trim. Everton started well taking the early play to Chelsea forcing corner, but it came to nothing for the Toffees. Everton continued to press Chelse back having won four of their last five games on the road they have confidence coming into this encounter in London SW6.

Dewsbury-Hall ex-Chelsea player left the field injured in the 14th minute he was replaced by Carlos Alcaraz in the 16th minute. Everton had couple of corners in the 19th and 20th minutes, but Chelsea defended them well. Chelsea took the lead through Cole Palmer with. Close range left-footed shot from the pass by Malo Gusto to make it 1-0 in the 21st minute from open play. It was a well-worked goal for the Blues.

In the 37th minute James Tarkowski headed wide at the far post from free kick into the penalty area but Gueye. Jack Grealish went close with a dribble and a shot in the 44th minute. Chelsea made it 2-0 in the 45th minute after Pedro Neto Cross was fired home by Malo Gusto to double the lead. Chelsea led 2-0 at half time. Chelsea kicked off in the second half. Neither side made any half time substitutions.

Chelsea 2-0 to the good at half time continued to look for more goals to ruin Everton’s afternoon in London. Chelsea replaced Cole Palmer with Andrey Santos in the 58th minute. Chelsea replaced Alejandro Garnacho with Jamie Gittens in the 65th minute. In the 66th minute Grealish headed on target, but Sanchez tipped his effort away for corner which came to nothing for Everton in the 67th minute. David Moyes brought on Beto for Barry in the 68th minute. Everton corner in the 71st minute was taken short, but Chelsea defended it away until Grealish saw an effort from wide at the back post from a through ball by O’Brien.

Chelsea went close with a free kick in the 75th minute from Neto but Everton survived for another corner which was then headed out for another corner as the Toffees goal was under siege. They survived and cleared the ball up field. Wesley Fofana was yellow carded for foul in 76th minute by referee Thomas Bramall. Chelsea brought on Estêvão for Joao Pedro in 80th minute. Everton brought on Tyler Dibling and Tim Iroegbunum for Jack Grealish and James Garner in 84th minute. In the 87th minute on counterattack Iliman Ndiaye saw left-footed shot bounce back off the post. Then in 88th minute Carlos Alcatel saw a right-footed shot from inside the penalty area deflected away for another corner.

Chelsea held on to win 2-0 and went to 4th in the table. Everton remained in 8th spot.
