## Match Report

PETERBOROUGH UNITED came from 1-3 down after a mad first eight minutes of the second half when Peterborough went from 1-0 up to 1-3 down thanks to a Jayden Wareham hat-trick but the home side came back to draw 3-3 after two goals from striker Harry Leonard. Peterborough debutant at 17 years old Patryk Sykut scored a worldwide opener with a stunning finish, but the goals continued as the crowd of 7,341 fans witnessed a six-goal thriller at the West Homes London Road Stadium.

Peterborough United hosted Exeter City at London Road yesterday afternoon in SKYBET League 1. Peterborough United were 11th placed on 44 points four points outside the playoffs from 33 games played whilst Exeter City were 14th placed on 40 points from 31 games. Exeter City had two games in hand on Peterborough United at start of play.

Peterborough United came into the game off the back of two wins against Mansfield Town (away) 2-1 and Wigan Athletic (home) 6-1 last time at home and three defeats against Barnsley (away) 1-2, Bradford City (away) 0-2 and Huddersfield Town (home) 2-3.

Exeter City meanwhile came into the game off the back of one win against Rotherham United (away) 4-0 and four draws against Wycombe Wanderers (home) 1-1, Northampton Town (home) 0-0, Mansfield Town (away) 0-0 and Reading (away) 2-2.

Peterborough United had only ever lost two of their 25 home Football League games against Exeter City (won ten, drawn thirteen) and had won five of their last six since August 1998. After winning the reverse fixture 3-0 in August last year, Exeter City will be looking to record a first Football League double over Peterborough United since the 1988-89 season.

Peterborough United won their last home league game 6-1 versus Wigan Athletic, they last scored 5+ goals in successive home matches in November 2024 when they beat Blackpool 5-1 before winning 6-1 vs Cambridge United in the local derby. Exeter City were unbeaten across their last five away league games (won two, drawn three), last enjoying a longer unbeaten run on the road between August and October 2021 (seven games).

Peterborough United manager Lee Williams started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Alex Bass in goal, then a back four of James Dornelly, Peter Kioso, David Okagbue, and captain Carl Johnston then two holding midfielders in Archie Collins and Patryk Sykut then three attacking midfielders in Declan Frith, Jimmy-Jay Morgan, and Kyrell Lisbie with Harry Leonard up front. Peterborough United made one change with Khela replaced by Sykut.

Exeter City interim manager Dan Green started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with Joe Whitworth in goal, then a back three of captain Pierce Sweeney, Ed Turns and Luca Woodhouse then a four-man midfield of IImari Niskanen, Ethan Brierley, Liam Oakes, and Timur Tutierov then Jack Aitchison and Sonny Cox then one striker Jayden Wareham up front. Exeter City made three changes from the previous match with Rydel, McMillian and Magennis replaced by Oakes, Tutierov and Cox.

Exeter striker Jayden Wareham bagged an eight-minute hat-trick in an entertaining six-goal draw at Peterborough. Wareham cancelled Patryk Sykut's opener and then put the visitors 3-1 ahead but Harry Leonard's brace ensured a share of the spoils. Sonny Cox fired over from close range early on for Exeter before Ukrainian teenager Sykut marked his first start for the club with a fine goal in the 17th minute.

City stopper Joe Whitworth kept the deficit at one as he produced a good save to keep Jimmy-Jay Morgan's free-kick. There was a crazy start to the second half with four goals in the first 15 minutes. Moments after the restart, on-song hitman Wareham met Ilmari Niskanen's cross to head home the equaliser. Wareham put the visitors in front six minutes later as he stabbed home Jack Aitchison's pass and completed his quick-fire treble when he fired in his 17th goal of the campaign. Leonard pulled one back for the hosts on the hour as he flicked Cian Hayes' corner home.

Carl Johnston saw his stinging strike tipped over the bar by Whitworth, but Leonard levelled with a header with 11 minutes to go after Morgan did well on the wing. Peterborough United opened the score through debutant Patryk Sykut with a lovely strike in the 19th minute to make it 1-0. Posh had further chances in the first half but did not take them and went in 1-0 at the interval.

In the opening eight minutes of the second half Peterborough went from 1-0 to 1-3 Dow thanks to a hat-trick from Jayden Wareham the Exeter City centre forward who bagged three goals in the 46th, 52nd and 54th minutes from assists by IImari Niskanen twice and Jack Aitchison once for the three goals. Peterborough United pulled back two goals through Harry Leonard in the 60th and 79th minute the first from an assist by Cian Hayes to level the game 3-3.
