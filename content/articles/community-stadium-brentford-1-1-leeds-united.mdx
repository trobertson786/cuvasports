## Match Report

BRENTFORD drew with Leeds United 1-1 with both goals coming late on in the second half. Brentford scored thanks to a Jordan Henderson strike in the 70th minute which was enough to breakdown a stubborn Leeds United in front of a crowd of 17,519 at the Gtech Community Stadium yesterday afternoon. Then Leeds United equalised in the 82nd through a Dominic Calvert-Lewin header his fourth goal in four games from a centre assist out right by Wilfred Gnonto to make it 1-1.

Dominic Calvert-Lewin continued his fine scoring run to rescue a point for Leeds against Brentford at Gtech Community Stadium. The striker netted for the fourth straight game - the first time since five for Everton in 2020 - with a close-range header in the 82nd minute.

It cancelled out Jordan Henderson's first Brentford goal with a deflected effort in the 70th minute - the England midfielder's first Premier League goal since December 2021 for Liverpool in a Merseyside derby. In a game of few chances, Brentford was awarded a penalty late in the first half when Dango Ouattara felt contact from Gabriel Gudmundsson, but the decision was overturned for offside after a lengthy check by the video assistant referee (VAR).

Bees’ goalkeeper Caoimhin Kelleher made four saves but was never seriously troubled until summer signing Calvert-Lewin afforded too much space to guide his header home. Brentford climbed up to 14th in the standings, with Leeds 17th and three points clear of the relegation zone.

Daniel Farke's future as Leeds manager has looked much more secure since he switched things up to play with a 3-5-2 formation earlier this month. An agonising injury-time loss at Manchester City, victory over Chelsea and comeback draw against Liverpool helped to silence his critics and the German set his side out in that same structure once again at Brentford. Those results are likely to have earned Farke enough credit to keep his job if they lost at Brentford, but his players showed similar spirit to ensure that wasn't the case.

Leeds undoubtedly looks more organised at the back in this new system and nullified the hosts for most of the game. Heads could have dropped when they conceded the opener, but Henderson's goal only served to galvanise the visitors.

Calvert-Lewin had a quiet game, but there was little doubt he would convert when he leaped high in the air to meet Gnonto's cross. Farke said he was "desperate" to sign the 28-year-old on a free transfer following his release by Everton and, after a slow start, Leeds are now reaping the rewards as he stays fit and shows the form that earned him England honours.

Calls for a return to the Three Lions fold may feel premature, but if he maintains this scoring streak, he might help save Leeds from relegation and force his way into the reckoning for a spot at the 2026 World Cup.

Brentford in 14th spot on 19 points hosted an improving Leeds United in 16th spot on 15 points at the Gtech Community Stadium yesterday teatime. Brentford came into the match with two wins against Burnley (home) 3-1 and Newcastle United (home) 3-1 and three defeated Tottenham Hotspur (away) 0-2, Arsenal (away) 0-2 and Brighton & Hove Albion (away) 1-2 in their last five matches.

Meanwhile Leeds United had won one against Chelsea (home) 3-1, drawn one against Liverpool (home) 3-3 and lost three against Manchester City (away) 2-3, Aston Villa (home) 1-2 and Nottingham Forest (away) 1-3 in their last five matches. The last three Brentford matches against Leeds United resulted in 0-0 draw (away), 5-2 win (home) and 1-2 loss (home) between 2022 and 2023.

Brentford had won just one of their four Premier League games against Leeds (drawn two, lost one), beating them 5-2 at home in September 2022. Leeds United had won just one of their last 12 away league games against Brentford (drawn four, lost seven), beating them 2-1 on the final day of the 2021-22 campaign to preserve their Premier League status.

Leeds United had lost their last nine Premier League games in London, with their last victory coming at Brentford in May 2022. It’s their second longest losing run in the capital in the top-flight, after a 12-game streak between March 1925 and September 1930. Brentford had lost their last two Premier League games, losing 2-0 to both Arsenal and Tottenham. The Bees haven’t lost three in a row since February 2024, while they’ve never gone three in a row without scoring in the competition.

Leeds United scored three goals in each of their last two Premier League matches (3-1 v Chelsea, 3-3 v Liverpool), as many times as they’d done so in their previous 37 combined. The last time the Whites scored 3+ goals in three top-flight games in a row was in December 2001. In their last two Premier League matches, Leeds started in a 3-5-2 formation, avoiding defeat in both (won one, drawn one). Brentford have played seven games in the competition against sides starting in that formation, failing to win any of their first five (drawn one, lost four) but winning their last two in April and August 2024 against Sheffield United and Southampton, respectively.

Leeds United had conceded five goals in the five minutes of games after half-time in the Premier League this season, more than any other side. It’s only the third time they’ve conceded that many goals in that time in a single season, also doing so in 2002-03 (five) and 2003-04 (six). Leeds United had lost their last eight Premier League matches played on Sundays, conceding 24 goals, including two defeats this season to Nottingham Forest and Aston Villa. No team in Premier League history has lost nine Sunday games in a row.

Dominic Calvert-Lewin had scored in three consecutive Premier League appearances for Leeds, only once having a longer scoring streak in the competition – he scored in his first five games in the 2020-21 season for Everton. The only Englishmen to score four in a row in the competition for the Whites are Rod Wallace (five in November/December 1993) and Michael Bridges (two runs of four in 1999-00). Brentford’s Igor Thiago scored seven goals in his last five home Premier League appearances, scoring a penalty in each of his last three. Only two players scored a penalty in four home games in a row: Dion Dublin in 1997-98 for Coventry and James Beattie in 2002 for Southampton.

Brentford manager Keith Andews started with a 4-2-3-1 formation with Caoimhin Kelleher in goal, then a back four of Michael Kayode, Nathan Collins, Sep van den Berg and Aaron Hickey then two holding midfielders in Jordan Henderson and Vitaly Janelt then three attackers in Dango Ouattara, Mathias Jensen and Keane Lewis-Potter with Igor Thiago up front.

Leeds United manager Daniel Farke started with a 3-5-2 formation with Lucas Perri in goal, then a back three of Joe Rodon, Jaka Bijol and Pascal Struijk then a five-man midfield of Jayden Bogle, Anton Stach, captain Ethan Ampadu, Ao Tanaka and Gabriel Gudmundsson with Dominic Calvert-Lewin and Noah Okafor up front.

Brentford kicked off the first half. Brentford started well and took the early match to Leeds United with Michael Kayode thrown into the Leeds United six-yard box causing problems for Lucas Perri in the Whites’ goal in the second minute. Leeds wore a changed strip of all blue with yellow and white trim. Brentford were in their traditional red, white, and black. Brentford looked more likely to open the score as they had more possessions during the initial stages of the game. Leeds defended resolutely and were well marshalled by the young captain and Welsh international Ethan Ampadu at the heart of a five-man midfield.

The home side had a free kick in the 27th minute which was launched into the Leeds penalty area by Mathias Jensen, but Jayden Bogle headed the ball clear. Another Kayode long throw in the 29th minute was headed clear by Pascal Struijk as the Bees pressured the visitors defence. Leeds went close through Noah Okafor with a run into the Bees penalty area in the 32nd but his effort was cleared away before Dominic Calvert-Lewie could get a foot on the ball inside the six-yard box.

Brentford was awarded a penalty in the 35th minute after Gabriel Gudmundsson appeared to foul Dango Ouattara inside the penalty area and the referee John Brooks awarded a spot kick which went to VAR for an offside which resulted in the penalty being cancelled. In the 42nd minute Keane Lewis-Porter saw a left-footed shot from 15 yards out saved well by Lucas Perri to deny the Bees an opening goal. The teams went in level at 0-0 at the half time interval.

Leeds United kicked off in the second half with score at 0-0. Neither side made any half time substitutions. Anton Stach saw a left-footed shot blacked from12 yards out after a long throw in from Ethan Ampadu had reached him after Sepp van den berg cleared the ball with a header. Leeds United started the second half well and had more possessions with Brentford doing more defending. The Elland Road club looked improved compared with earlier in the season especially after taking 4 points out of games recently against Chelsea and Liverpool in the last two games.

Brentford manager Keith Andrews replaced Keane Lewis-Potter and Aaron Hickey with Mikkel Damsgaard and Rico Henry in the 61st minute. Brentford finally broke the deadlock as Jordan Henderson fired the ball from close range after a Rico Henry cross and only been half cleared by a Leeds United defender Jaka Bijol and the home side took the lead, but it went to VAR but was given to make it 1-0 in the 70th minute.

Leeds United brought on Brendan Aaronson and Wilfred Gnonto for Jaka Bijol and Ao Tanaka in the 71st minute. Brentford replaced Mathias Jensen and Vitaly Janelt with Yehor Yarmoliuk and Frank Onyeka in the 81st minute. Leeds United equalised in the 82nd through a Dominic Calvert-Lewin header his fourth goal in four games from a centre assist out right by Wilfred Gnonto to make it 1-1. Leeds United replaced Gabriel Gudmundsson with James Justin in the 89th minute. Jack Harrison came on for Leeds in the 0th+2 minute for Noah Okofor.

Brentford rose to 14th spot on 20 points whilst Leeds United remained 17th on 16 points above West Ham United by 3 points. Brentford travel to Manchester City for a Carabao Cup quarter-final tie-on Wednesday, 17 December before a Premier League trip to Wolves on Saturday, 20 December. Leeds United return to Elland Road on Saturday, 20 December as Crystal Palace visit in the league.
