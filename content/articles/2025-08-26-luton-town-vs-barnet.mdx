## First 45 minutes

LUTON TOWN hosted SKY BET League 2 new comers Barnet at Kenilworth Road in the EFL Trophy last night in the first group match of the round which the Hatters won 4-1 in front of 3,157 fans thanks to first half goals by Lasse Nordas in 11th minute, Jerry Yates in 19th minute and Cohen Bramall in 45th+2 minute to seal a deserved win. Shayden Morris took the penalty to make it 4-0 as he scored on his debut in the 87th minute. Barnet pulled back a late goal through Adam Senior with a header from a corner to make it 4-1 in 90th+6 minute. They now play Cambridge United and Brighton & Hove Albion U21 in the next two group matches in the competition.

Luton Town manager Matt Bloomfield started with a 3-4-2-1 formation with James O’Shea in goal then back three of Christ Makosso, captain Kai Naismith, and Christin Chigozie then a four-man midfield of Gideon Kodua, Liam Walsh, Zack Nelson, and Cohen Bramall then Lasse Nordas and Jake Richards signed from Exeter City behind striker Jerry Yates. Luton Town were missing Nahki Wells up front. Nahki Wells was away with Bermuda on international duty so was not present last night, which gave Nordas and Yates the opportunity to lead the line.

Barnet Dean Brennan manager started with a 3-5-1-1 formation with Owen Evans in goal, then a back three of Ryan Galvin, Ollie Kensdale, and captain Adam Senior then a five-man midfield of Scott High, Emmanuel Osadebe, Winterburn, Kane Smith and Romoney Crichlow then Callum Stead then Dennis Adeniran. The Barnet coach was late due to heavy rain and traffic on the M1 out of London.

Barnet kicked off the first half. Ollie Kensdale yellow carded for foul on Lasse Nordas in 6th minute. Barnet seemed incredibly positive early on and took the match to Luton Town. Luton Town opened the lead in the 11th minute through a right-footed shot by Lasse Nordas with a shot in off the post from an assist by Nelson to make it 1-0. Nordas opened the scoring a Burton Albion on Saturday, so it was two goals in two games for the Hatters striker.

Luton Town made it 2-0 in the 19th minute as Jerry Yates fired home with a right-footed shot from close range beyond Owen Evans to double lead from an assist by Cohen Bramall. Luton Town started slowly and after the two goals were well on top. Last season Luton were in the SKYBET Championship and Barnet were in the National League.

Barnet replaced Ollie Kensdale with Daniele Collinge in the 27th minute due to injury. Nordas came forward in the 31st minute after an assist from Bramhall but his shorts were tipped away for a corner by Evans. Nordas should have crossed to Yates who had a clear view on goal rather than shooting. Luton Town added a third in added time through a powerful left-footed shot from Cohen Bramall off the cross bar to beat Evans and make it 3-0 in the 45th+2 minute. Luton Town led 3-0 at half time.

**Half Time Luton Town 3 Barnet 0 (4 minutes added time)**

## Second 45 minutes

Luton Town off the second half 3-0 up. Barnet replaced Ryan Galvin with Ryan Glover in the 46th minute. Barnet started the second half well and the Bees forced the Hatters into defending. Barnet brought on Lee Ndlouv for Dennis Adeniran in the 60th minute. Luton Town manager Matt Bloomfield replaced Gideon Kodua and Jerry yates with Shayden Morris a signing yesterday from Aberdeen and Millenic Alli in the 62nd minute.

Luton Town replaced Cohen Bramall with Nigel Lonwijk and Nordas for Lamine Fannie in 73rd minute. Barnet brought on Nicklaus Tavares for Romoney Crichow in 73rd minute. Luton Town were in full control of the second half, but Dean Brennan’s side put themselves better in the second half.

Luton Town final change saw Josh Phillips on loan from Torquay united come on for Jake Richards in 84th minute. Ben Winterburn was yellow carded in 86th minute. Shayden Morris took the penalty to make it 4-0 as he scored on his debut in the 87th minute. Barnet pulled back a late goal through Adam Senior with a header from a corner to make it 4-1 in 90th+6 minute. Luton Town settled for a deserved 4-1 home win.
