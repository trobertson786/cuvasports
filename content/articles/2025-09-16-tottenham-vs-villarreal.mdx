## Match Report

Thomas Frank enjoyed winning Champions League debut as a calamitous fourth-minute mistake by Villarreal goalkeeper Luiz Junior sealed a narrow victory for Tottenham.

Under no pressure, Junior fumbled Lucas Bergvall's cross into his own net to hand Spurs the dream start in front of their own fans.

This is the first time ex-Brentford boss Frank has managed this competition, but his Tottenham side lived dangerously at times before the break, with only a crucial intervention by Micky van de Ven preventing ex-Arsenal winger Nicolas Pepe from curling in, while Tajon Buchanan dragged a shot wide when he looked certain to score.

But only a good save by Junior denied Tottenham adding a second midway through the first half when he kept out Pape Matar Sarr's strike.

Villarreal came on strong in the second half, but Tottenham defended superbly to repel them.

Richarlison should have done better with a header as Spurs tried to get the security of a second goal, but it was not needed.

Former Arsenal midfielder Thomas Partey came on for Villarreal in the closing stages, despite being due to appear in court on rape and sexual assault charges in London on Wednesday.

Ghana midfielder Partey, 32, was charged last month with five counts of rape against two women and a charge of sexual assault against a third woman.

It was the first match he had played in England since the allegations first became known. This was the result Tottenham wanted as they made their return to the Champions League for the first time since the 2022-23 season, but it was far from a perfect performance.

Junior's unfortunate mistake got the home crowd going and could have been the platform for Tottenham to go on and put in a commanding performance, but that failed to materialise as they laboured to win.

Tottenham's start this season has been impressive, with eye-catching wins against West Ham and Manchester City, but there was a concerning display in the 1-0 loss to Bournemouth.

There were more similarities in this game to the Cherries performance with some poor passages of play and sloppy passes whenever they did break forward.

Despite that, their defending was resolute with Van de Ven and Rodrigo Bentancur making some crucial tackles, particularly in the second half when Villarreal pushed for an equaliser.

The win means Tottenham are now unbeaten in their last 21 home games in UEFA competitions.

Tottenham Hotspur returned to Champions League against Villarreal last night at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.

Thomas Frank the Tottenham Hotspur manager started with 4-3-3 formation with Guglielmo Vicario in goal, then a back four of Pedro Porro, Cristian Romero, Micky van de Ven and Djed Spence then a three-man midfield of Lucas Bergvall, Rodrigo Bentancur, and Pape Sarr then three attackers in Mohammed Kudos, Xavi Simons, and Richarlison up front.

Marcelino the Villarreal Manager started with a 4-4-2 formation with Luiz Junior in goal, then a back four of Junior in goal, then a back four of Santiago Mourino, captain Juan Foyth, Renata Veiga and Sergi Cardona then a four-man midfield of Nicholas Pepe, Santiago Comesana, Pape Gueye, and Tajon Buchanan with two strikers in Ayoze Perez and Georges Mikautadze

Tottenham Hotspur kicked off the first half, and it was not too long before Tottenham Hotspur took the lead through an own goal by goalkeeper Luiz Junior to make it 1-0 to Tottenham Hotspur in the 4th minute with an assist through Lucas Bergvall down the right flank.

In the 12th minute Villarreal came forward through Nicolas Pepe, and he flashed a left-footed effort across the face of the Spurs goal. Villarreal went close again to an equaliser through Tajon Buchanan who shot wide with a right-footed effort from an assist by Pepe in the 24th minute.

Tottenham Hotspur first shot on goal was a right-footed effort from Pape Sarr which was saved by Junior in the 29th minute. There were four yellow cards in quick succession two for each side in the 28th to 35th minutes. Xavi and Richarlison for the home side and Comesana and Morino for the visitors.
