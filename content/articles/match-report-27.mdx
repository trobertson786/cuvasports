## Match Report

WEST HAM UNITED recorded back-to-back wins in style at home in the Premier League for the first time since October 2024 yesterday as they came from behind against Burnley to win 3-2 at the London Stadium.

The home side came into the match at the London Stadium off the back of a 3-1 (home) win over Newcastle United last time out, and three defeats against Leeds United (away) 1-2, Brentford (home) 0-2 and Arsenal (away) 0-2. Meanwhile Burnley came into the game off the back of the 0-2 (home) defeat to Arsenal last time out and two wins against Wolverhampton Wanderers (away) 3-2, Leeds United (home) 2-0 and an away loss to Aston Villa (away) 1-2.

West Ham United fans stood to observe a minute’s silence in their Remembrance Tribute ahead of tomorrow ahead of kick off which was observed impeccably by both fans. The London Stadium is normally quiet and subdued of late but after the unexpected win over Newcastle United last week the Hammers fans were back at their noisy best with the side seeking two home wins on the bounce.

The last time the Hammers won two back-to-back home games in the Premier league was against Manchester United and Ipswich Town in October 2024. The Hammers started well and oozed class in the opening exchanges with Lucas Paqueta and Jarrod Bowen causing the five-man Burnley defence trouble. Burnley did not let West Ham have it all their way as Jaidon Anthony and Zian Flemming almost caused problems for Alphonse Areola in the 15th minute after building up the left flank, but the chance went begging.

West Ham United under Nuno Espírito Santo at home seem to sit back and let away teams come onto them and then the Hammers play on a fast counterattack when they get the ball through Bowen, Paqueta and Summerville. Burnley opened the scoring through Zian Flemming with a close-range header from an assist by Loum Tchaouna in the 35th minute to give the Clarets the lead. The goal was awarded after a VAR decision for an earlier offside in the buildup play. Burnley build up play for the goal would have pleased Scott Parker a former Hammer himself you were a cultured midfielder during his time at the Boleyn Ground.

West Ham United came back strongly after falling behind through captain Jarrod Bowen in the 37th minute but his left-footed shot from outside the penalty area from an assist by Callum Wilson was blocked. The Hammers continued to press and with the next attack Maximilian Kilman saw a solid header blocked after an assist from Freddie Potts in the 38th minute.

West Ham United finally equalised through a Callum Wilson close range header following a corner in the 44th minute it was checked for offside by VAR and subsequently given by referee Michael Salisbury to level the scores at 1-1. Wilson has scored seven goals in his last five starts against the Clarets. Wilson has also scored in six of his last eight starts against newly promoted teams.

Flemming went close with a header in the 58th minute which went just over Alphonse Areola’s cross bar as the visitors looked to regain the lead. Freddie Potts who looked particularly good but started to tire was replaced by stalwart midfielder Tomas Soucek in the 61st minute.

Jarrod Bowen saw a left-footed shot from inside the penalty area saved by Martin Dubravka in the 75th minute as the home side came forward in search of a winning goal. Then in the 77th minute from a corner West Ham United went ahead through a close range tap in from Tomas Soucek after Martin Dubravaka could only parry a left-footed shot by Lucas Paqueta into his path and he stabbed the ball home to make it 2-1 and send the Hammers faithfully into raptures.

West Ham United weren’t finished as a late substitute Kyle Walker-Peters tapped in from close range after a long range shot by Tomas Soucek was charged down by Martin Dubravka into the path of Walker-Peters who made no mistake to make it 3-1.

In the seventh minute of added time Josh Cullen scored for Burnley to make it 3-2 to set up a tense couple of minutes or so for the Hammers but they hung on to win a five-goal thriller. The win saw West Ham United go into the international break on 10 points in 18th place with Burnley above them in 17th place on 10 points but with a better goal difference which was less negative.

West Ham were unbeaten in their last six Premier League games against Burnley (won three, drawn three), having lost four of their previous five against the Clarets (won one). Burnley have won just two of their last 11 top-flight away games against West Ham (drawn two, lost seven) – 3-0 in March 2018 and 1-0 in July 2020. West Ham had not lost any of their last 11 Premier League games against Burnley when they’ve scored at least once in the match (won eight, drawn three), since a 2-1 loss at Turf Moor in February 2010.

West Ham United ended a run of six winless Premier League matches (drawn one, lost five) with a 3-1 win over Newcastle last time out. The Hammers haven’t had consecutive wins since February, when they beat Arsenal and Leicester. The two sides with the worst shot differential when they’ve been drawing in Premier League games this season are Burnley (-54) and West Ham United (-35), while the Clarets also have the worst difference when they’ve been losing (-25).

West Ham United have faced the most shots from set pieces (excl. pens) in the Premier League this season (fifty-three), with over a quarter of the attempts they’ve faced coming from corners (26%, 37/145), also a league high. Burnley has had the fewest shots in the Premier League this season (74), with this average of 7.4 shots per game the lowest by a team on record in a Premier League season since 1997-98. Despite this, their 16.2% shot conversion is the second best of any side this campaign, behind only Spurs (17.5%).

Over half (9/16) of the shots on target West Ham United have had in the Premier League under Nuno Espírito Santo came in their 3-1 win over Newcastle United last week.

It was their most in a match against a side who weren’t newly promoted since having nine against Leeds in May 2023. Quilindschy Hartman has assisted in each of his last three Premier League matches (four assists), with those four assists coming within his first five away appearances.

In Premier League history, only Lee Sharpe registered more assists in his first five away games (five). Callum Wilson only has more Premier League goals against current club West Ham United (twelve) and Everton (eight) than he does against Burnley (seven), scoring six goals in his last four starts against the Clarets. Wilson has also scored in five of his last seven starts against newly promoted teams (five goals). West Ham United manager Nuno Espirito Santo started with a 4-3-3 formation with Alphonse Areola in goal, followed by a four-man midfield with Aaron Wan-Bissaka, Max Kilman, Todibo and El Hadji Malick Diouf then a three-man midfield of Matheus Fernandes, Freddie Potts and Lucas Paqueta then three attackers in captain Jarrod Bowen, Callum Wilson and Crysencio Summerville up front.

Burnley manager Scott Parker started with a 4-2–3-1 formation with Martin Dubravka in goal, then a back five of Kyle Walker, Axel Tuanzebe, Maxime Esteve and Quilindschy Hartman then a two-man midfield of Florentino and captain Josh Cullen then a three of Lesley Ugochukwu, Loum Tchaouna and Jadon Anthony with Zian Flemming up top.
